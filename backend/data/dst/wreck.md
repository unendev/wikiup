A **Wreck** is a naturally occurring [structure](/wiki/Structure "Structure") found in the *[Shipwrecked](/wiki/Don%27t_Starve:_Shipwrecked "Don't Starve: Shipwrecked")* DLC. It can occasionally be encountered in the [Ocean](/wiki/Ocean "Ocean") [biome](/wiki/Biome "Biome"), on dark-colored "ship graveyard" tiles, and in the [Coral Reef](/wiki/Coral_Reef "Coral Reef") biome. It appears a broken down part of a sunken ship. It is covered with [Limpets](/wiki/Limpets "Limpets") and can be harvested every 3 days to yield 1-3 Limpets. It can be [hammered](/wiki/Hammer "Hammer") down to obtain 1 [Boards](/wiki/Boards "Boards"), but doing so may summon a [Pirate Ghost](/wiki/Ghost "Ghost").

## Placeholder Trivia[]

* [Nautilus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nautilus_(Verne)) may be a reference to the fictional submarine featured in [*Twenty Thousand Leagues Under the Se*a](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Twenty_Thousand_Leagues_Under_the_Sea), a novel by [Jules Verne](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jules_Verne).
* [Mackay-Bennett](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/CS_Mackay-Bennett) may be a reference to the first ship that was contracted to recover bodies from [Titanic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/RMS_Titanic) disaster.
* [Mary Celeste](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mary_Celeste) may be a reference to a cargo ship, crew of which have disappeared under mysterious circumstances.
* [Beagle](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/HMS_Beagle) may be a reference to the ship that took [Charles Darwin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Charles_Darwin) on his voyage around the world.
* [Monitor](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/USS_Monitor) may be a reference to an American Civil War era ship that plays a central role in [Battle of Hampton Roads](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Battle_of_Hampton_Roads).
* [Santa Maria](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Santa_Mar%C3%ADa_(ship)) is a reference to the ship that took [Christopher Columbus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Christopher_Columbus) on his first voyage.
* [Bluenose](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bluenose) may be a reference to Canadian ship, featured on Canadian currency.
* [Adriatic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/RMS_Adriatic_(1906)) may be a reference to the first ship to have an indoor swimming pool on board.
* [Nomadic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/SS_Nomadic_(1911)) may be a reference to the only surviving [tender](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ship%27s_tender) to Titanic.
* [Mauretania](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/RMS_Mauretania_(1906)) may be a reference to the ship which held the record for fastest transatlantic crossing for 20 years from 1909.
* [Endeavour](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/HMS_Endeavour) is a reference to the ship commanded by [James Cook](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/James_Cook) on his first voyage of discovery.
* [Mont-Blanc](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/SS_Mont-Blanc) may be a reference to the ship carrying the explosives which resulted in [Halifax Explosion](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Halifax_Explosion).
* Caine may be a reference to the ship featured in [*The Caine Mutiny*](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Caine_Mutiny), a Pulitzer Prize-winning novel by [Herman Wouk](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Herman_Wouk).
* Orca may be a reference to the boat used to hunt down the shark in [*Jaws*](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jaws_(film)).
* Nellie may be a reference to the boat featured in [*Heart of Darkness*](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Heart_of_Darkness), a novel by [Joseph Conrad](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Joseph_Conrad).
* [Piper Maru](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Piper_Maru) may be a reference to the ship featured in the eponymous episode of TV series [*The X-Files*](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_X-Files).
* [Minnow](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/S._S._Minnow) may be a reference to a boat featured in TV sitcom [*Gilligan's Island*](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gilligan%27s_Island).
* [Syracusia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Syracusia) may be a reference to an ancient large transport ship, designed by [Archimedes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Archimedes).
* [Baron of Renfrew](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Baron_of_Renfrew_(ship)) may be a reference to one of the largest wooden ships ever built.
* [Ariel](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ariel_(clipper)) may be a reference to a ship that narrowly lost the [Great Tea Race of 1866](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Great_Tea_Race_of_1866).
* [African Queen](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/African_Queen_(boat)) may be a reference to the historic boat used in the movie with the same name.
* [Resolution](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/HMS_Resolution_(1771)) may be a reference to the ship used by James Cook on his explorations.
* Pelican and [Golden Hind](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Golden_Hind) are references to the ship which sailed around the world between 1577 and 1580. The name of the ship was changed mid-voyage.
* Hispaniola is a reference to the ship featured in [*Treasure Island*](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Treasure_Island), a novel by [Robert Louis Stevenson](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Robert_Louis_Stevenson).
* [Mississinewa](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/USS_Mississinewa_%28AO-59%29) may be a reference to a ship sunk in World War II.
* [Pequod](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pequod_(Moby-Dick)) may be a reference to the whaling ship featured in [*Moby-Dick*](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Moby-Dick), a novel by [Herman Melville](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Herman_Melville).
* [Edmund Fitzgerald](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/SS_Edmund_Fitzgerald) is a reference to the largest ship ever sunk in North America's [Great Lakes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Great_Lakes). The sinking has been memorialized in the song *["The Wreck of the Edmund Fitzgerald"](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PH0K6ojmGZA)*, by Gordon Lightfoot.
* [Batavia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Batavia_(ship)) may be a reference to a ship that was shipwrecked on her first voyage.
* [Blackadder](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Blackadder_(clipper)) may be a reference to a ship that was shipwrecked in 1905.
* If sailing next to a Wreck, it's possible to hear eerie sounds, such as creaks and echoes.
