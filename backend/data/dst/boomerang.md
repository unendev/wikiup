The **Boomerang** is a craftable [Ranged Weapon](/wiki/Weapon#Ranged_Weapons "Weapon"). It requires 1 [Boards](/wiki/Boards "Boards"), 1 [Silk](/wiki/Silk "Silk"), and 1 [Charcoal](/wiki/Charcoal "Charcoal") to craft and an [Alchemy Engine](/wiki/Alchemy_Engine "Alchemy Engine") to prototype.

The Boomerang returns to the player after being thrown. The player must catch it by holding down the action key (default Space key) while it is on its way back. If it isn't caught after being thrown, it will hit the player, causing damage and losing twice the [durability](/wiki/Durability "Durability") on return, and the player will have to pick it up from the ground in order to use it again.

## Prototype Tips[]

* The player can have more than one Boomerang out at a time. The action key must be held for each returning Boomerang, however.
* The Boomerang kills [Rabbits](/wiki/Rabbit "Rabbit") and [Birds](/wiki/Birds "Birds") in one throw (except for characters who don't hit as hard, such as [Wendy](/wiki/Wendy "Wendy") and [Wes](/wiki/Wes "Wes")), making it a great tool for gathering [Morsels](/wiki/Morsel "Morsel") and [Feathers](/wiki/Feather "Feather"). However, nearby Rabbits and Birds will run away after a death, and additionally, killing them raises the player's [naughtiness](/wiki/Naughtiness "Naughtiness").
  + Birds are perhaps more preferable to kill with a Boomerang since Rabbits can still be caught easily with other means such as Traps, and the objects needed to craft items to kill birds are more demanding than Rabbit Traps.
* When playing as [Wolfgang](/wiki/Wolfgang "Wolfgang"), he may do more or less damage depending on his hunger level, or mightiness in DST, He also receives the same damage multiplier from uncaught Boomerangs.
* The Boomerang serves much the same purpose on the console versions of the game, though because of the auto targeting system, it is not a recommended weapon. One must not stand near anything interactive ([Grass](/wiki/Grass "Grass"), [Flowers](/wiki/Flowers "Flowers"), [Flint](/wiki/Flint "Flint"), etc.) and wait for the Boomerang to be selected on its way back before quickly pressing "X" to catch it. This can be hard to do, and if the player's health is too low, it can easily cause death.
* Because of the low damage, the easy to craft recipe, and durability the **boomerang** is most useful at aggroing fast mobs such as [Koalefant](/wiki/Koalefant "Koalefant") and [Volt Goats](/wiki/Volt_Goat "Volt Goat") or to kill [Birds](/wiki/Birds "Birds") and [Rabbits](/wiki/Rabbit "Rabbit").

## Placeholder Trivia[]

* If the player dies from being struck by a Boomerang, the [Morgue](/wiki/Morgue "Morgue") will show the Character's name under "Cause of Death".
* The Boomerang's description ("It comes from a land down under.") is a reference to Australia's nickname, the "Land Down Under", and also to a famous song by the Australian band [Men at Work](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Men_at_Work "wikipedia:Men at Work").
* Being struck by a Boomerang while on a [Boat](/wiki/Boats "Boats") in the *[Shipwrecked](/wiki/Don%27t_Starve:_Shipwrecked "Don't Starve: Shipwrecked")* DLC, will cause damage to the Boat rather than to the players.
* When playing PvP in *[Don't Starve Together](/wiki/Don%27t_Starve_Together "Don't Starve Together")*, players are able to catch Boomerangs thrown at them.
* In *Don't Starve Together*, failing to catch a boomerang will mark the receiver as an attacker, causing other characters to see them as such if examined.
* As shown by his examination quote, [Warly](/wiki/Warly "Warly") seems to be affected by [separation anxiety disorder](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Separation_anxiety_disorder). An individual affected by it experiences excessive anxiety regarding separation from home and/or from people to whom the individual has a strong emotional attachment (e.g., a parent, caregiver, significant other, or siblings).
  + This is reinforced when Warly is shown to feel a strong attachment to his maman in the animated short *[Taste of Home](/wiki/Animated_shorts_and_trailers#Taste_of_Home "Animated shorts and trailers")*.

## Mosquito Bugs[]

* If a [Bird](/wiki/Birds "Birds") targeted by a Boomerang flies away before it hits it, the Boomerang will go to the spot where the Bird was and spin perpetually. If picked up and placed back down without being thrown again, it will continue to move to the same spot. This is a very rare occurrence, since most of the time when the bird does fly away, the Boomerang will just return immediately.
* If an [Eyeplant](/wiki/Eyeplant "Eyeplant") eats a Boomerang mid-throw, the player will be harmed upon killing the [Lureplant](/wiki/Lureplant "Lureplant") if the Boomerang is undigested.
* Boomerangs may fly away instead of returning to the player after hitting its target. It will return to the player later (even after several days) and will still damage the player if not caught.
* If the player is currently equipping [The Lazy Forager](/wiki/The_Lazy_Forager "The Lazy Forager"), the boomerang will be added back to the inventory as soon as it is thrown.
