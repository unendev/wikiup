**Turkey Dinner** is a [Meat](/wiki/Meats "Meats") [Food](/wiki/Food "Food") [Item](/wiki/Item "Item") cooked in the [Crock Pot](/wiki/Crock_Pot "Crock Pot") by adding 2 [Raw Drumsticks](/wiki/Drumstick "Drumstick"), 1 [Meats](/wiki/Meats "Meats"), and 1 [Fruit](/wiki/Fruit "Fruit") or [Vegetable](/wiki/Vegetable "Vegetable"). It takes 60 seconds to cook and 6 [Days](/wiki/Day-Night_Cycle "Day-Night Cycle") to [Spoil](/wiki/Spoil "Spoil").

When consumed, it restores 20 Health, 75 Hunger and 5 Sanity.

Adding a [Mandrake](/wiki/Mandrake "Mandrake") may result in [Mandrake Soup](/wiki/Mandrake_Soup "Mandrake Soup").

Using Fried Drumsticks may result in [Meatballs](/wiki/Meatballs "Meatballs"), as the recipe strictly requires uncooked drumsticks.

### Downloadable Content[]

In the *[Reign of Giants](/wiki/Reign_of_Giants "Reign of Giants")* and *[Shipwrecked](/wiki/Shipwrecked "Shipwrecked")* DLCs, Turkey Dinner increases the player's perceived temperature by 1 degree per second for 10 seconds when consumed.

In the *[Hamlet](/wiki/Hamlet "Hamlet")* DLC, Turkey Dinner can be bought for 10 [Oincs](/wiki/Oinc "Oinc") at [The Sterling Trough Deli](/wiki/The_Sterling_Trough_Deli "The Sterling Trough Deli").

### Don't Starve Together Don't Starve Together[]

The Turkey Dinner is [Wigfrid](/wiki/Wigfrid "Wigfrid")'s favorite food.

## Tips[]

* It is recommended to cook this Crock Pot recipe rather than to eat two [Drumsticks](/wiki/Drumstick "Drumstick") on their own, as it restores more [Health](/wiki/Health "Health") and [Hunger](/wiki/Hunger "Hunger") than most of their recipe ingredients.
* Keep your drumsticks uncooked for this recipe, as this will not work with [Fried Drumsticks](/wiki/Drumstick "Drumstick").

## Trivia[]

* During the [Pig Fiesta](/wiki/Pig_Fiesta "Pig Fiesta") event in the *Hamlet* DLC, Turkey Dinner takes on an even more festive appearance.
* While the Turkey Dinner can be made with either Fruits or Vegetables, the appearance of the item in-game appears to show [berries](/wiki/Berries "Berries") in a similar appearance to cranberry sauce.
