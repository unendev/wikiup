The **Clockwork Bishop** is an aggressive [Mob](/wiki/Mob "Mob") usually seen in a [Chess biome](/wiki/Chess "Chess") guarding the [Wooden Thing](/wiki/Wooden_Thing "Wooden Thing") in [Sandbox Mode](/wiki/Sandbox_Mode "Sandbox Mode") and other places in [Adventure Mode](/wiki/Adventure_Mode "Adventure Mode"). It also spawns from a mined [Maxwell Statue](/wiki/Maxwell_Statue "Maxwell Statue") in the chapter *Epilogue* of Adventure Mode. It is a part of Maxwell's [Chess Pieces](/wiki/Chess_Monsters "Chess Monsters").

## Behavior[]

The Bishop usually stands close to the [Wooden Thing](/wiki/Wooden_Thing "Wooden Thing"), guarding it from any intruders. When any creature gets near its sight range, the Bishop will suddenly wake up and stare at them as a warning. Getting closer will make the Bishop attack by firing lightning bolts from its head. The Bishop will chase its enemy a short distance from its spawn area before returning and going back to sleep.

## Hunting[]

The Bishop is overall the most difficult of the Chess Pieces, it is a strong ranged mob, and very accurate, making it almost impossible to kite effectively so it is recommended to be armored and armed for a battle with one. Sending some [Pigs](/wiki/Pigs "Pigs") to attack it first may prove to be an effective distraction. The player may try and use a [Boomerang](/wiki/Boomerang "Boomerang") to kill it from far away, but this will grab the Bishop's attention, and it will start attacking. The player may also use an [Ice Staff](/wiki/Ice_Staff "Ice Staff") to freeze it, and then, start hitting it with a Spear, but it will cause some damage to the player. Another way to kill a Bishop would be to aggravate a nearby [Clockwork Rook](/wiki/Clockwork_Rook "Clockwork Rook") without waking the Bishop and get the Rook to ram the Bishop, as this will most likely kill the Bishop instantly (This strategy also works with [Clockwork Knights](/wiki/Clockwork_Knights "Clockwork Knights") and other [mobs](/wiki/Mobs "Mobs")).

It is possible to dodge the Bishop's lighting bolts if the player is quick enough. Suddenly running to one side just as the Bishop is about to attack will usually leave the player unharmed by the lighting bolt, although the bolt does have a small area effect.

Another tactic to kill the Bishop is to lead all other mobs away, then use a [Log Suit](/wiki/Log_Suit "Log Suit") and [Spear](/wiki/Spear "Spear") and hold down the attack button until it is dead, although this will cost the player a good amount of health, so it is only recommended to use this method when there is a decent amount of healing items in the player's immediate possession.

When a Bishop is located near a Spider Den, it is advised to lead some Spiders close enough to the Bishop without aggrevating it, as Bishops attack all monsters except other chess pieces when coming too close. Bishops also naturally attack Pigs, Bunnymen, Chester and similiar.

## Tips[]

* Clockwork Bishops (as well as knights and rooks) can be lit on fire with a [torch](/wiki/Torch "Torch"). If on fire, bishops and knights jump around until the flame extinguishes without attacking. While you will take fire damage from being close to them, you may take less damage than you would from tanking the bishop. [Wormwood](/wiki/Wormwood "Wormwood") may be the worst at this strategy since he catches on fire easier and takes more damage from fire. [Willow](/wiki/Willow "Willow") takes no damage from fire.
* [Wheeler](/wiki/Wheeler "Wheeler") can avoid taking damage from clockwork bishops by timing her Dodge ability correctly, since her Dodge has I-frames.

## Trivia[]

* The Clockwork Bishop was originally added in the *[Doorway to Adventure](/wiki/Don%27t_Starve/Version_History#Doorway_to_Adventure "Don't Starve/Version History")* update.
* According to Maxwell's quote, the Bishop is his favorite clockwork monster.
