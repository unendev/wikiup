**Mushtrees** are naturally generated objects that act as light sources inside [Caves](/wiki/Caves "Caves").

The most common variant is Blue Mushtree, which can be chopped down, providing 2 [Logs](/wiki/Logs "Logs") and a [Blue Cap](/wiki/Blue_Cap "Blue Cap"). Using a [Shovel](/wiki/Shovel "Shovel") or a [Regal Shovel](/wiki/Regal_Shovel "Regal Shovel") to dig up the stump that will provide another Log or burn it to yield 1 [Ash](/wiki/Ashes "Ashes"). Mushtrees also come in green and red varieties, which drop a [Green Cap](/wiki/Green_Cap "Green Cap") and [Red Cap](/wiki/Red_Cap "Red Cap") respectively upon harvest, as well as a single Log. When set on [fire](/wiki/Fire "Fire"), a Mushtree will eventually burn to a crisp and disappear, dropping 1 Ash and 50% chance for 1 [Charcoal](/wiki/Charcoal "Charcoal").
Unlike [Evergreens](/wiki/Evergreens "Evergreens"), which drop [Pine Cones](/wiki/Pine_Cone "Pine Cone") that can be planted, there is no way for the player to regrow Mushtrees unless they reset the Cave they were chopped in with the [Seed of Ruin](/wiki/Seed_of_Ruin "Seed of Ruin").

## Downloadable Content[]

During [Full Moons](/wiki/Moon_Cycle "Moon Cycle") in all [DLCs](/wiki/DLC "DLC"), [Mushrooms](/wiki/Mushrooms "Mushrooms") (both in Caves and on the [Surface](/wiki/Surface_World "Surface World")) will turn into Mushtrees. If a Mushroom-turned-Mushtree has been chopped, its stump will turn into a harvested Mushroom spawn point once the Full Moon ends.

In *[Don't Starve Together](/wiki/Don%27t_Starve_Together "Don't Starve Together")*, Mushtrees have a blooming state where they produce light-generating [Spores](/wiki/Mushroom_Spore "Mushroom Spore"). The blooming season is different for each variety. Blue Mushtrees start blooming at [night](/wiki/Night "Night") during [Winter](/wiki/Winter "Winter"). Green Mushtrees start blooming at [dusk](/wiki/Dusk "Dusk") during [Spring](/wiki/Spring "Spring"). Red Mushtrees start blooming at [day](/wiki/Day "Day") during [Summer](/wiki/Summer "Summer"). All three types of Mushtrees are renewable through [Regrowth](/wiki/Regrowth "Regrowth"), and will spawn during their blooming [Seasons](/wiki/Seasons "Seasons").

A variant of the Blue Mushtree, called the **Webbed Blue Mushtree**, can be found near [Dangling Depth Dweller](/wiki/Dangling_Depth_Dweller "Dangling Depth Dweller") dens. Each den will convert surrounding blue mushtrees at a rate of 1 per day up to a maximum of 6. Webbed Blue Mushtrees will spawn spiders from nearby dens when chopped at with an axe.
It is worth noting that, even if you are playing as [Webber](/wiki/Webber "Webber") , the Dangling Depth Dwellers will not hesitate from attacking you after chopping this particular mushtree variant from where they originate.

## Tips[]

* Chopping down Mushtrees is an easy way to get caps while in the caves. However, Mushtrees also provide a small but free and unending source of light. Illumination frequently means life or [death](/wiki/Death "Death") in the caves, so discretion should be used when deciding whether or not to harvest.

## Trivia[]

* According to [Wickerbottom](/wiki/Wickerbottom "Wickerbottom") and Wagstaff, Mushtrees are regular trees infected with fungal parasites.
* Wickerbottom also notes that the red and green mushtrees are "*[Mycena luxaeterna](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mycena_luxaeterna "wikipedia:Mycena luxaeterna")*" and "*Mycena silvaelucens*" respectively.
  + The fungal genus "*Mycena*" is known in real life for their bioluminescence.
  + "Luxeterna" translates to English as "eternal light", and "silvaelucens" translates to "bright forest".
