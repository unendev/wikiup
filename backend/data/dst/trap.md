A **Trap** is a [Survival](/wiki/Survival "Survival") item used to catch small animals such as [Rabbits](/wiki/Rabbit "Rabbit"), [Frogs](/wiki/Frog "Frog"), and [Spiders](/wiki/Spider "Spider"). It can be crafted at the beginning of the game with 6 [Cut Grass](/wiki/Cut_Grass "Cut Grass") and 2 [Twigs](/wiki/Twigs "Twigs").

Once set, Traps can be baited with [food](/wiki/Food "Food") items attractive to said animals. Baited or not, a Trap will trigger once a small mob moves close enough to it.

## Icon Tools Usage[]

*https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/dont-starve-game/images/c/cb/Glommer.png/revision/latest?cb=20231029201751 See also: [Rabbit Trapping Guide](/wiki/Guides/Rabbit_Trap "Guides/Rabbit Trap")*

Traps are set by placing them on the ground and can be picked up again at any time. To bait a Trap once set, simply place a food item in it. Bait will be consumed upon a successful catch. Once a Trap has caught something, it will shake and rattle around with the animal inside.

Traps do not need bait in order to catch creatures; if a creature touches the Trap for any reason, it will fall and possibly catch it. This means if a Trap is placed directly on top of a [Rabbit Hole](/wiki/Rabbit_Hole "Rabbit Hole"), it will attempt to catch the Rabbit when it leaves home or comes back. However, sometimes a Rabbit will disappear into its hole before the Trap falls completely, leaving no loot.

When a Trap is picked up from the ground still set to catch an animal, no durability is lost, allowing it to be moved around freely. When a Trap is picked up after being triggered, it will lose durability. A Trap will lose durability whether or not it catches the animal that triggered it.

Rabbits and Spiders are placed into the player's [inventory](/wiki/Inventory "Inventory") when Traps are harvested. Caught Frogs instead automatically deposit the loot that would be gained from killing them normally.

## Spear Hunting[]

A hostile creature can be lured into a Trap if it is chasing the player character; Spiders for example will always try to travel in a straight line and make no effort to avoid traps or other obstacles. Traps can be used in this way to form a barrier when destroying [Spider Dens](/wiki/Spider_Den "Spider Den"), or as a great method to farm [Silk](/wiki/Silk "Silk").

Traps are particularly useful during [Winter](/wiki/Winter "Winter"), as they can be spread around a large area and placed atop Rabbit Holes for instant capture. In vanilla *Don't Starve*, [Chests](/wiki/Chest "Chest") can be built nearby Rabbit Holes and filled with Rabbits to create a food stockpile.

## Gift Icon Downloadable Content[]

In [DLCs](/wiki/DLC "DLC"), the creatures caught under a trap will struggle for a few days before starving to death and dropping their loot on the ground. For example, a [Rabbit](/wiki/Rabbit "Rabbit") will starve under a trap in two days, the same time it does in the inventory. If the loot (such as a [Morsel](/wiki/Morsel "Morsel")) can spoil, it will do so as normal while on the ground. The loot will also be open for any creatures to eat or steal as their normal rules. After the creature has starved to death, the trap will remain in its sprung state.

In the *[Reign of Giants](/wiki/Reign_of_Giants "Reign of Giants")* DLC, [Moleworms](/wiki/Moleworm "Moleworm") tunneling underground will trigger Traps. Even though [Flint](/wiki/Flint "Flint") and [Rocks](/wiki/Rocks "Rocks") can be used as bait, Moleworms cannot be caught with Traps.

In the *[Shipwrecked](/wiki/Shipwrecked "Shipwrecked")*, [Crabbits](/wiki/Crabbit "Crabbit"), [Flups](/wiki/Flup "Flup") and [Snakes](/wiki/Snake "Snake") and can be caught using Traps. Placing a Trap on a Crabbit that buried itself will easily catch it.

While inside a *[Hamlet](/wiki/Hamlet "Hamlet")* world, Traps can be used to kill [Scorpions](/wiki/Scorpion "Scorpion"), [Pikos](/wiki/Piko "Piko"), [Poison Dartfrogs](/wiki/Poison_Dartfrog "Poison Dartfrog"), [Rabid Beetles](/wiki/Glowfly "Glowfly"), [Vipers](/wiki/Viper "Viper") and [Weevoles](/wiki/Weevole "Weevole"). They can be bought for 2 [Oincs](/wiki/Oinc "Oinc") at [The Boar's Tusk Weapon Shop](/wiki/The_Boar%27s_Tusk_Weapon_Shop "The Boar's Tusk Weapon Shop") in the [Pig City](/wiki/Pig_City "Pig City").

## Silver Bowl The Gorge[]

In [The Gorge](/wiki/The_Gorge "The Gorge") event for *[Don't Starve Together](/wiki/Don%27t_Starve_Together "Don't Starve Together")*, Traps function almost identically to the way they do in normal gameplay, other than the fact that they have no durability and can be used an infinite amount of times. They are used to catch [Rabbits](/wiki/Rabbit "Rabbit"). Traps can be purchased from [Billy](/wiki/Billy "Billy") for 4 Old Coins each.

## Prototype Tips[]

* Creatures that cannot be caught in the Trap can still eat food used as a bait in the Trap. Traps with baits should be placed away from [Pigs](/wiki/Pig "Pig") (omnivore) and [Gobblers](/wiki/Gobbler "Gobbler") (herbivore) in order for them to be more effective.
* [Birds](/wiki/Birds "Birds") will fly down to Traps baited with [Seeds](/wiki/Seeds "Seeds"), but they will not be caught. [Bird Traps](/wiki/Bird_Trap "Bird Trap") can be used to catch Birds.
* Traps can be used to kill Spiders and Frogs without aggroing any others in close proximity. This can turn events such as [Frog Rain](/wiki/Frog_Rain "Frog Rain") into a way to easily obtain food without the risk of getting mobbed.
* Caught Rabbits or Crabbits can be left to starve to death, either in the trap or in the inventory. This gives the same items as murdering them but without raising the player's Naughtiness level.
* Traps can also be used to mark notable areas on the [Map](/wiki/Map "Map"), as Trap icons appear wherever they are left.

## Mosquito Bugs[]

 https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/dont-starve-game/images/7/7c/Screen Shot 2013-07-24 at 10.01.08 AM.png/revision/latest?cb=20130728161954 https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/dont-starve-game/images/7/7c/Screen Shot 2013-07-24 at 10.01.08 AM.png/revision/latest?cb=20130728161954 



 

* Traps found near [Skeletons](/wiki/Skeleton "Skeleton") as part of [Set Pieces](/wiki/Set_Piece "Set Piece") have a random amount of [durability](/wiki/Durability "Durability"). Sometimes, a Trap may be at 0%; they can be used to trap animals infinitely and will never break, presumably because their durability cannot go down. Placing this in a chest or equivalent can cause bugs such as Chester being unopenable, a backpack disappearing, and crashing the server when returning from caves.
* Schrödinger's Rabbit: while a Rabbit is in the middle of springing a Trap, it is possible to kill the Rabbit, receiving a [Morsel](/wiki/Morsel "Morsel") and still obtaining a live Rabbit (although this is fairly hard to time correctly).
* When a Trap catches an animal and is picked up before it completely falls down, the prey will disappear and the Trap will lose durability as normal.
