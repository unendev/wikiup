**Tallbirds** are black, two-legged, flightless [Birds](/wiki/Category:Birds "Category:Birds") with tiny wings normally found in [Rockyland](/wiki/Rockyland "Rockyland") biomes. They guard their [Tallbird Nest](/wiki/Tallbird_Nest "Tallbird Nest"), attacking anything nearby. They are considered innocent creatures even though they are not passive or neutral, and killing one adds 2 points to the player's [naughtiness](/wiki/Naughtiness "Naughtiness") level. Tallbirds take three blasts from the [Ice Staff](/wiki/Ice_Staff "Ice Staff") to be frozen. If killed, they drop 2 [Meat](/wiki/Meat "Meat") and will respawn from their nest after 2.5 days (5 days in DST).

Tallbirds lay a speckled, pale-blue [Tallbird Egg](/wiki/Tallbird_Egg "Tallbird Egg"). Tallbird Eggs do not spoil and can be used in a [Crock Pot](/wiki/Crock_Pot "Crock Pot") or hatched into a Smallbird. If a Tallbird Egg is stolen, the Tallbird will chase the player for 3-5 screens. At night (and sometimes during the day), the Tallbird will curl up in its nest. If a Tallbird is attacked while it is asleep, it will remain curled up for a few seconds, allowing the player to land a few free hits before it can attack. Tallbirds can create new nests on rocky tiles or tiles that were dug up, allowing for Tallbirds to be relocated anywhere.

Tallbirds may be found clustered together in a [Set Piece](/wiki/Set_Piece#Tallfort "Set Piece") called *Tallfort*, where 20 or so Tallbird Nests are placed in a square around multiple [Boulders](/wiki/Boulder "Boulder").

**Smallbirds** are passive [mobs](/wiki/Mobs "Mobs") that have been hatched from a Tallbird Egg and can fly briefly, unlike teens and adults. A Smallbird will grow into a Teenbird (or Smallish Tallbird) after 10 full [days](/wiki/Day "Day"). They are considered innocent creatures and killing one adds 6 points to the player's [naughtiness](/wiki/Naughtiness "Naughtiness") level.

Smallbirds require 20 [hunger](/wiki/Hunger "Hunger") points per day and will demand food by chirping when hungry (Hunger < 50%). When starving (Hunger = 0), they will die after 4 game hours (2 minutes). They can be fed any type of [Seeds](/wiki/Seeds "Seeds"). They will eat seeds off the ground when they are hungry. Eating anything heals them to full health.

Smallbirds will follow the player and attack the player's targets. If the player moves far enough away from the Smallbird while it is sleeping at night, it will wake up to move closer to the player before falling asleep again. Since they have very small Health, players must be careful when fighting strong mobs or groups of them. Other birds won't run away from Smallbirds and can be a good way to get [Feathers](/wiki/Feathers "Feathers") and [Morsels](/wiki/Morsel "Morsel"). Note that Smallbirds can trigger [Tooth Traps](/wiki/Tooth_Traps "Tooth Traps") and [Bee Mines](/wiki/Bee_Mine "Bee Mine"), likely killing them.

When idle, a Smallbird will either bounce around or cock its head/body at an angle with a puzzled look.

Commonly known as **Teenbirds**, **Smallish Tallbirds** are neutral [Mobs](/wiki/Mob "Mob") that were once Smallbirds. After 18 full [days](/wiki/Day "Day"), Smallish Tallbird will grow into a Tallbird, becoming aggressive when approached.

Like Smallbirds, Smallish Tallbirds will follow the player from a distance, and they will also attack the player's targets or enemies that are nearby. They are considered innocent creatures and killing one adds 2 points to the player's naughtiness level.

Smallish Tallbirds require 60 [hunger](/wiki/Hunger "Hunger") points per day and will begin to chirp more and more until fed. When starving, they will peck the player (2 damage per hit), and will die after 8 segments (4 minutes) if not fed. Eating anything restores 1/3 of their maximum health. They will eat any [meats](/wiki/Meats "Meats"), [fruits](/wiki/Fruit "Fruit") or [vegetables](/wiki/Vegetable "Vegetable"), including [Glow Berries](/wiki/Glow_Berries "Glow Berries"), [Rotten Eggs](/wiki/Rotten_Egg "Rotten Egg"), and [Rot](/wiki/Rot "Rot"). Smallish Tallbirds will eat food left on the ground.

If the player is wearing a [Spiderhat](/wiki/Spiderhat "Spiderhat"), the Smallish Tallbird will attack until the hat is removed.

After turning into adult Tallbirds they will target the player.

## Spear Fighting Strategy[]

Tallbirds cannot be stun-locked, but can be kited. There is only a short interval between their attacks. The safe method is to dodge its attack, attack it once, then dodge again. It's possible to attack twice and run out of its pecking range after it scratches the ground. Note that Tallbirds can leap over small gaps between two landmasses.

Unlike many other creatures, Tallbirds will not defend each other. Tallbirds will only chase the target that aggroed them. They will not change targets even if something else attacks them. This can be exploited to kill them safely by making them aggro on [Chester](/wiki/Chester "Chester") or other followers and then attack the Tallbird without retaliation (however, this has been changed in *[Don't Starve Together](/wiki/Don%27t_Starve_Together "Don't Starve Together")*). A very easy way to kill Tallbirds is to lure them to a [Lureplant](/wiki/Meat_Bulb_(Lureplant) "Meat Bulb (Lureplant)")'s Eyeplants. As they ignore anything other than their target, they die quickly. Placing [Fleshy Bulbs](/wiki/Fleshy_Bulb "Fleshy Bulb") close to a large Tallbird nesting area is an easy way to clear it out. However, this method makes it difficult to obtain their Meat drops, since Lureplants will digest them in a short amount of time.

Tallbirds can be put to sleep with [Sleep Dart](/wiki/Sleep_Dart "Sleep Dart") or [Pan Flute](/wiki/Pan_Flute "Pan Flute"). They can also be set on fire using [Fire Dart](/wiki/Fire_Dart "Fire Dart") or a [Fire Staff](/wiki/Fire_Staff "Fire Staff"), causing it to panic. Usually, the fire will go out before doing enough damage to kill the bird. Note that they can spread fire to their nests, which will permanently stop it from respawning.

## Placeholder Trivia[]

* In previous versions, the Tallbird's chase timer would only reset after they attempted to attack. As they would outrun any character with normal movement speed, this resulted in them chasing the player indefinitely.
* [Maxwell](/wiki/Maxwell_(character) "Maxwell (character)") states that Tallbirds were a failed experiment upon examination.
* Although eggs hatched by the player create Smallbirds, eggs left in a nest without an adult Tallbird present will create an instant adult.
* Tallbirds may have been inspired by an illustration in Ralph Waldo Emerson's [*Nature*](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nature_(essay)#/media/File:Houghton_MS_Am_1506_(4)_-_Cranch,_edit.jpg).
