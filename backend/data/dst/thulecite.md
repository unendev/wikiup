**Thulecite** is a craftable [Item](/wiki/Item "Item") found in the [Ancient Tab](/wiki/Ancient_Tab "Ancient Tab"). It requires 6 [Thulecite Fragments](/wiki/Thulecite_Fragments "Thulecite Fragments") and a [Broken Ancient Pseudoscience Station](/wiki/Ancient_Pseudoscience_Station "Ancient Pseudoscience Station") to be crafted. Thulecite is generally acquired by destroying [Ancient Statues](/wiki/Ancient_Statue "Ancient Statue") and [Ruins Sinkholes](/wiki/Sinkhole "Sinkhole") with a [Pickaxe](/wiki/Pickaxe "Pickaxe"), [Gunpowder](/wiki/Gunpowder "Gunpowder") etc., but they also have a 10% chance to be found in [Ornate Chests](/wiki/Ornate_Chests "Ornate Chests") and a 75% chance in Large Ornate Chests. Thulecite also can randomly drop when destroying [Relics](/wiki/Relic "Relic") or damaging Ancient Pseudoscience Stations with a [Hammer](/wiki/Hammer "Hammer"), [Gunpowder](/wiki/Gunpowder "Gunpowder") etc. Like all items from the Ancient Tab, Thulecite cannot be prototyped and [Characters](/wiki/Characters "Characters") must stand near an Ancient Pseudoscience Station to craft them.

Its main use is as a crafting material for items in the Ancient Tab. It can also be used to repair a Broken Ancient Pseudoscience Station, opening up more crafting options.

## Reign of Giants icon Reign of Giants[]

In the *[Reign of Giants](/wiki/Reign_of_Giants "Reign of Giants")* DLC, the [Seed of Ruin](/wiki/Seed_of_Ruin "Seed of Ruin") can be used to regenerate the [Caves](/wiki/Caves "Caves"), and by extension, the [Ruins](/wiki/Ruins "Ruins"), therefore making Thulecite infinitely renewable. Additionally, players have a small chance of finding [Green Gems](/wiki/Green_Gem "Green Gem") inside [Tumbleweeds](/wiki/Tumbleweed "Tumbleweed"), thus making Thulecite renewable, since more Thulecite can be gained with the use of [Construction Amulets](/wiki/Construction_Amulet "Construction Amulet") and [Deconstruction Staffs](/wiki/Deconstruction_Staff "Deconstruction Staff") as explained below.

## Don't Starve Together icon Don't Starve Together[]

In *[Don't Starve Together](/wiki/Don%27t_Starve_Together "Don't Starve Together")*, Thulecite can be gathered in the [Caves](/wiki/Caves "Caves") from [Cave Holes](/wiki/Cave_Hole "Cave Hole") with the [The Lazy Forager](/wiki/The_Lazy_Forager "The Lazy Forager"). After killing the [Ancient Fuelweaver](/wiki/Ancient_Fuelweaver "Ancient Fuelweaver"), an event triggers that respawns the Ancient Statues in the Ruins, providing a renewable source of Thulecite and Gems. Players must wait 20 days until the Fuelweaver can be fought again. It is also possible to acquire Thulecite as an uncommon drop from [Sunken Chests](/wiki/Sunken_Chest "Sunken Chest").

With the Forgotten Knowledge update, Thulecite can also be gathered by utilizing the [Dust Moths](/wiki/Dust_Moth "Dust Moth") found in the [Ancient Archive](/wiki/Ancient_Archive "Ancient Archive"). After feeding one an [Amberosia](/wiki/Amberosia "Amberosia"), it will begin to ornate a nearby [Tidy Hidey-Hole](/wiki/Tidy_Hidey-Hole "Tidy Hidey-Hole") with mineable Thulecite Fragments. Because Amberosia is renewable, this provides a reliable (albeit slow) source of Thulecite alternative to killing the Ancient Fuelweaver.

## Tips[]

* Although Green Gems are somewhat rare, it is possible to use a Construction Amulet to craft 5 [Thulecite Suits](/wiki/Thulecite_Suit "Thulecite Suit") for 15 Thulecite, then use a [Deconstruction Staff](/wiki/Deconstruction_Staff "Deconstruction Staff") on those 5 Thulecite Suits to get 30 Thulecite back for a profit of 13 Thulecite (the Amulet costs 2 Thulecite). This practice makes it possible to collect big amounts of Thulecite from a few Green Gems.
* [Slurtles](/wiki/Slurtles "Slurtles"), [Snurtles](/wiki/Snurtles "Snurtles") and [Rock Lobsters](/wiki/Rock_Lobsters "Rock Lobsters") will attempt to eat dropped Thulecite, so players should be careful and collect all Thulecite before they successfully eat it. Slurtles will also attack a player holding Thulecite if there aren't any other minerals around for them to eat.
  + When a Slurtle or Snurtle has eaten 2 pieces of Thulecite they will produce [Slurtle Slime](/wiki/Slurtle_Slime "Slurtle Slime"). When players feed Thulecite to a Rock Lobster, they will become friendly and follow the player around for 3 minutes (6 [Clock](/wiki/Clock "Clock") segments).
* If you need to turn Thulecite into fragments, craft walls and hammer them. 1 Thulecite becomes 6 walls which yield 1 fragment each, making this process completely reversable at the cost of the hammer wear.

## Trivia[]

* Thulecite may have been based off of real-life mineral [thulite](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thulite "wikipedia:Thulite"), which is also a variety of [zoisite](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zoisite "wikipedia:Zoisite").
* Like much of the Ruins, Thulecite was inspired by the [Cthulhu Mythos](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cthulhu_Mythos "wikipedia:Cthulhu Mythos"), and may have derived its name from the name of Cthulhu, an ancient god-like being associated with insanity, magic, abnormal geometry, and tentacles.
  + A secondary inspiration for the name may have come from [Thule](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thule "wikipedia:Thule"), a northern region in ancient geography that is usually described as being the farthest north one can travel. Later, writers would use the term Ultima Thule to describe something far off and unattainable. This is more than likely inspiration for Thulecite's status as an endgame material.
* The material itself is quite similar in color and use to the [Dwarven Metal](https://elderscrolls.fandom.com/wiki/Dwarven_Metal_Ingot "morrowind:Dwarven Metal Ingot") that is found in the *[The Elder Scrolls](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Elder_Scrolls "wikipedia:The Elder Scrolls")* series, as both the Dwemer and the Ancient Civilization used their own material (Dwarven metal for the Dwemer, and Thulecite for the Ancients), and when they became extinct, their respective material were left behind and could be used by those who found their ruins. This is perhaps unsurprising, as it's a believable explanation for the rarity of the resources required for rare elite items.
