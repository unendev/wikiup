**Mants** are [Mobs](/wiki/Mob "Mob") exclusive to the *[Hamlet](/wiki/Hamlet "Hamlet")* DLC. They spawn from [Antcomb Homes](/wiki/Antcomb_Home "Antcomb Home") as Larvae, inside [Mant Hills](/wiki/Mant_Hill "Mant Hill"). Mants can mostly be found inside the Mant Hill, or wandering around it.

They are hostile Mobs, and will attack the player if they are too close. However, they will first warn the player to leave by glaring at them. Wearing a full set of Mant Armor as a disguise (i.e. [Mant Suit](/wiki/Mant_Suit "Mant Suit") and [Mant Mask](/wiki/Mant_Mask "Mant Mask")) will make them neutral to the player as long as the armor is worn. Their language is normally indecipherable but it can be understood with the [Pherostone](/wiki/Pherostone "Pherostone") in the player's inventory. However, owning one without the Mant disguise won't prevent them from being aggressive.

Mants can be befriended by using the Pherostone to determine which food they eat, and then feeding the corresponding meat, seeds, plants, or wood. Befriended Mants will assist the player in chopping down [trees](/wiki/Tree "Tree") and attacking [mobs](/wiki/Mob "Mob").

Upon death, they drop either one [Monster Meat](/wiki/Monster_Meat "Monster Meat") or one [Chitin](/wiki/Chitin "Chitin"). When examined by a character, the displayed name will be “Drone”, followed by a six-digit number.

Mants will consume any food left on the ground. If the player is holding a [Magnifying Glass](/wiki/Magnifying_Glass "Magnifying Glass"), nearby Mants will panic. During the [Aporkalypse](/wiki/Seasons/Aporkalypse "Seasons/Aporkalypse"), all Mants will transform into aggressive [Warrior Mants](/wiki/Warrior_Mant "Warrior Mant") wearing glowing warpaint.

Mants take two [Sleep Darts](/wiki/Sleep_Dart "Sleep Dart") to be put to sleep, and two hits from an [Ice Staff](/wiki/Ice_Staff "Ice Staff") to be frozen.

**Mant Warriors** are mobs exclusive to the *Hamlet* DLC. They are similar to regular Mants, but they are only encountered deep inside of the [Mant Hill](/wiki/Mant_Hill "Mant Hill") or during the [Aporkalypse](/wiki/Seasons/Aporkalypse "Seasons/Aporkalypse"), where they wear glowing war-paint and take the place of normal Mants. Compared to regular Mants, Mant Warriors are faster, more powerful, and always attack the player on sight. They are also not fooled by the [Mant Mask](/wiki/Mant_Mask "Mant Mask") and [Mant Suit](/wiki/Mant_Suit "Mant Suit") disguise.

They can be found in the room with the [Queen Womant](/wiki/Queen_Womant "Queen Womant") Den entrance, as well as all of the rooms leading to the [Queen Womant](/wiki/Queen_Womant "Queen Womant") boss fight. They will also spawn from [Queen Womant](/wiki/Queen_Womant "Queen Womant")'s eggs, which will spawn Warrior Mants 2 to 6 seconds after landing.

Upon death, they drop either one [Monster Meat](/wiki/Monster_Meat "Monster Meat") or one [Chitin](/wiki/Chitin "Chitin"). When examined by a character, the displayed name will be "Warrior", followed by a six-digit number.

Mant Warriors take one [Sleep Dart](/wiki/Sleep_Dart "Sleep Dart") to be put to sleep, and two hits from an [Ice Staff](/wiki/Ice_Staff "Ice Staff") to be frozen.

## Prototype Tips[]

* The player can tell if they are too close to a Mant by looking at its eyes. If it has an angry look (furrowed eyebrows), the player should probably keep back.

## Placeholder Trivia[]

* The name "Mant" is a portmanteau of the words "Man" and "Ant".
* The prefab name "antman" may be a reference to the [superhero](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ant-Man "wikipedia:Ant-Man") of the same name.
* There are art assets in the game files of a creature similar to the Mant, but with notable differences, including a mouth on the front of the face (above the mandibles, almost more human-like) and a lack of arms. One of the developers confirmed that this is an old design for the Mant.[[1]](#cite_note-1)
* The fact that Mants are afraid of a player holding a magnifying glass is a reference to the stereotypical action of young kids burning ants with focused light using a magnifying glass.
  + This is further supported with the fact that you can burn objects from a distance by using a magnifying glass in game.
* The fact that Mants produce [Honey](/wiki/Honey "Honey") in their Mant Hill instead of [Bees](/wiki/Bee "Bee") in the *Hamlet* DLC is likely because real life ants are close relatives of bees.
