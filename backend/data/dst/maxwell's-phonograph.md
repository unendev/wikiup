**Maxwell's Phonograph** (or *Gramaphone*) is a [Structure](/wiki/Structure "Structure") exclusive to *[Don't Starve](/wiki/Don%27t_Starve "Don't Starve")* that is seen in the Epilogue of [Adventure Mode](/wiki/Adventure_Mode "Adventure Mode"), nearby [Maxwell](/wiki/Maxwell_(NPC) "Maxwell (NPC)"), who is in the [Nightmare Throne](/wiki/Nightmare_Throne "Nightmare Throne"). It cannot be destroyed in any way, and it serves no practical function within the game. When Maxwell is first reached, Maxwell's Phonograph is playing the *[Ragtime](/wiki/Soundtrack "Soundtrack")* theme that is used in some update trailers.

Maxwell's Phonograph can be turned off and back on. Upon turning it off, Maxwell will say, "Thank you. I have been listening to that song for an eternity." When turned back on, Maxwell will say, "I guess I deserve that." Freeing Maxwell will automatically stop Maxwell's Phonograph from playing, but once the player character is in the throne, the Phonograph will play again, to taunt the player as it did to Maxwell.

## Trivia[]

* A bunch of pieces that appear to be components for Maxwell's Phonograph can be found in the game's files, but they cannot be acquired without cheating tools or code trickery and serve no purpose.
* In the animated short *[Taste of Home](/wiki/Animated_Shorts_and_Trailers#Taste_of_Home "Animated Shorts and Trailers")*, Warly is shown to have owned a Phonograph very similar to the one in-game. It played an odd song before Warly disappeared from the real world.
* Maxwell's Phonograph's music emanates from [Maxwell's Door](/wiki/Maxwell%27s_Door "Maxwell's Door") while the door is open.
* The sound file refers to this device as *Gram**a**phone*, while its real life counterpart is called *[Gram**o**phone](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gramophone "wikipedia:Gramophone")*. Whether it's a mistake or purposeful is not known.
* If the player spawns Maxwell's Phonograph using the [Console](/wiki/Console "Console") and is resurrected next to it, Maxwell's Phonograph will be playing.
* The [Chorusbox Circuit](/wiki/Chorusbox_Circuit "Chorusbox Circuit") plays a simpler, musicbox rendition of this song.
* In the game files, the song that Maxwell's Phonograph plays is called "d9ney."
* [Wagstaff](/wiki/Wagstaff "Wagstaff") identifies Maxwell's Phonograph at the end of Adventure Mode as a Gramophone ML-77 in his examination quote for it.
* In *Don't Starve Together*, Maxwell's Phonograph was introduced in the *[Host of Horrors](/wiki/Host_of_Horrors "Host of Horrors")* update as the "[Gramophone](/wiki/Gramophone "Gramophone")".
