**Honey** is a [Sweetener](/wiki/Sweetener "Sweetener") [Food](/wiki/Food "Food") [item](/wiki/Item "Item") created by [Bees](/wiki/Bee "Bee"). Honey may be obtained by killing a Bee or [Killer Bee](/wiki/Killer_Bee "Killer Bee"), by destroying a [Beehive](/wiki/Beehive "Beehive") or [Killer Bee Hive](/wiki/Killer_Bee_Hive "Killer Bee Hive"), or by harvesting a [Bee Box](/wiki/Bee_Box "Bee Box"). Honey provides 3 [health](/wiki/Health "Health") points and 9 [hunger](/wiki/Hunger "Hunger") points when consumed.

## Harvesting[]

*https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/dont-starve-game/images/c/cb/Glommer.png/revision/latest?cb=20231029201751 See also: [Honey Production](/wiki/Bee_Box#Honey_Production "Bee Box") and [Farming - Bees and Honey](/wiki/Farming#Bees_and_Honey "Farming")*

 https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/dont-starve-game/images/7/75/BeeKeeper Hat.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20121119174714 https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/dont-starve-game/images/7/75/BeeKeeper Hat.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20121119174714 

Adequate protection against hostile winged insects.

 

Typically, harvesting is a slow process with low risk. The [Beekeeper Hat](/wiki/Beekeeper_Hat "Beekeeper Hat") is a specialty item designed to reduce damage from Bees. The best source for Honey is Bee Boxes; however, early in the game when these are not available yet, Bees and Beehives are a good alternative.

Ideally, Honey should be collected when all the Bees are working outside - in this case they will ignore the player entirely. Bee Boxes contain 4 Bees. The first leaves immediately in the morning and one leaves every 37.5 seconds after. The best time to harvest then becomes about 4 hours into the day or right before dusk. Harvesting is perfectly safe any time during [Winter](/wiki/Winter "Winter"), since the Bees will not come out of their boxes.

Destroying Beehives is trickier because it is harder to find an opportune time when all the Bees are out. With 6 Bees and one leaving every 50 seconds, the last worker will exit the hive 8.3 game hours (250 seconds) into the day, but dusk usually comes before this can happen. However, one or two Bees can be safely ignored for the short time it takes to smash the hive if one uses protective gear.

Since Killer Bees do not leave their hive to collect nectar, there is no perfect time to attack a Killer Bee Hive. Getting close to a hive will cause all the Killer Bees to leave the hive and chase the player a short distance before retreating unless there is another [Mob](/wiki/Mob "Mob") nearby. If there is, the Killer Bees can be led into the other creature and, after they lose interest in the player, they will aggro the mob and attack. The player can then attack the Killer Bee Hive while the bees are in combat or have died.

[Wendy](/wiki/Wendy "Wendy") can use [Abigail](/wiki/Abigail "Abigail") to easily destroy hives: once Abigail is summoned, click to attack a Beehive and then cancel the attack by walking away. Abigail will still be offensive and attack and destroy the Beehive. She can also keep the Bees stun-locked, easily taking them out and allowing the player to collect the loot from the hive.

## Downloadable Content[]

In the *[Hamlet](/wiki/Don%27t_Starve:_Hamlet "Don't Starve: Hamlet")* DLC, Honey can be found in [Mant Hills](/wiki/Mant_Hill "Mant Hill"). [Honey Chests](/wiki/Honey_Chest "Honey Chest") contain Honey and can convert [Nectar](/wiki/Nectar "Nectar") into honey. [Lamps](/wiki/Lamp "Lamp") can be hammered and [Antcomb Homes](/wiki/Antcomb_Home "Antcomb Home") can be [mined](/wiki/Pickaxe "Pickaxe") to recieve Honey and [Honeycombs](/wiki/Honeycomb "Honeycomb"). The Queen Womant drops 5 Honey when killed.

## Trivia[]

* Honey will rot in-game, unlike in real life. However, in-game honey does have an insanely long spoil timer, likely in reference to the fact that real honey doesn't spoil.
* While Honey is not produced by Bees in the Hamlet DLC, [Mants](/wiki/Mant "Mant") appear to do so, this is a reference to the fact that real life ants are closely related to bees.
