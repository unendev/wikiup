**Ancient Pig Ruins** are areas introduced in the *[Hamlet](/wiki/Don%27t_Starve:_Hamlet "Don't Starve: Hamlet")* DLC. They can be accessed by entering a **Ruinous Entrance**, which can be found in the [Plains](/wiki/Plains "Plains"), [Rainforest](/wiki/Rainforest "Rainforest"), [Deep Rainforest](/wiki/Deep_Rainforest "Deep Rainforest") or [Gas Rainforest](/wiki/Gas_Rainforest "Gas Rainforest") [biomes](/wiki/Biomes "Biomes"). Clicking on the entrance will transport the player into an *Indiana Jones*-esque temple ruins containing many rooms connected to each other, complete with decorative wall art.

## Door (Ancient Pig Ruins Exit) Map Icon Interior[]

Rooms will often include an assortment of hostile mobs like [Batilisks](/wiki/Batilisks "Batilisks"), [Scorpions](/wiki/Scorpion "Scorpion"), or [Vipers](/wiki/Viper "Viper"), and many rooms will contain [Unimportant Pillars](/wiki/Unimportant_Pillar "Unimportant Pillar") or [Smashing Pots](/wiki/A_Smashing_Pot "A Smashing Pot"). Some rooms will contain a [Wishing Well](/wiki/Wishing_Well "Wishing Well") or [statues](/wiki/Ruined_Sculptures "Ruined Sculptures") that can be [chiseled](/wiki/Ball_Pein_Hammer "Ball Pein Hammer") for [Gold Nuggets](/wiki/Gold_Nugget "Gold Nugget") or [Lost Relics.](/wiki/Lost_Relics "Lost Relics")

Most rooms are completely dark, with the exception of light emitted from exits and ceiling gaps during [Day](/wiki/Day "Day") and [Dusk](/wiki/Dusk "Dusk"). Ceiling light is typically above an item surrounded by spike traps or above a room full of [Grass Tufts](/wiki/Grass_Tuft "Grass Tuft") and [Saplings](/wiki/Sapling "Sapling"). Some rooms contain [Wall Braziers](/wiki/Wall_Brazier "Wall Brazier") that can be [fueled](/wiki/Fuel "Fuel") like a [Fire Pit](/wiki/Fire_Pit "Fire Pit"), but are considered uncontrolled fires like a [Campfire](/wiki/Campfire "Campfire"), potentially burning nearby [flammables](/wiki/Category:Flammable_Objects "Category:Flammable Objects").

When being inside any Ancient Pig Ruins, there is no loss of [Sanity](/wiki/Sanity "Sanity") in darkness.

## Map Icon Connections to Islands[]

Entrances located in the Deep Rainforest and Gas Rainforest biomes will have [Creeping Vines](/wiki/Creeping_Vines "Creeping Vines") covering the entrance, which must be destroyed with a [Machete](/wiki/Machete "Machete") or [Shears](/wiki/Shears "Shears").

The ruins with a [Pig](/wiki/Pig "Pig") statue above the entrance will have a second exit leading to the island with the [Palace](/wiki/Palace "Palace").

The ruins with an artichoke above the entrance will have a second exit leading to the island with the [Fountain of Youth](/wiki/Fountain_of_Youth "Fountain of Youth").

The ruins with a [Mant](/wiki/Mant "Mant") statue above the entrance will have a [Pherostone](/wiki/Pherostone "Pherostone") located within them.

The ruins with the [Ancient Herald](/wiki/Ancient_Herald "Ancient Herald") statue above the entrance will have a second exit leading to the island with the [Aporkalypse Calendar](/wiki/Aporkalypse_Calendar "Aporkalypse Calendar") in the ruin located deeper in the island. This ruin is also where the [End's Well](/wiki/Wishing_Well "Wishing Well") can be found.

The Palace and Fountain ruin entrances are found in Deep Rainforest biomes on the first island and their respective islands.

The Pherostone and Aporkalypse ruin entrances are found in the Gas Rainforest biome on the first island.

The fifth island (with the Aporkalypse Calendar) has two ruins, both with the Ancient Herald statue. One connects to the first island; while the other contains the End's Well and Aporkalypse Calendar, and is surrounded by several [Snaptooth Flytraps](/wiki/Snaptooth_Flytrap "Snaptooth Flytrap") in a [Set Piece](/wiki/Set_Piece "Set Piece")-like fashion.

Many of the doorways within these ruins will also be covered by Creeping Vines, which, unlike the ones on the entrances, regrow upon next entering in the Ruins.

## Pressure Plates[]

Some rooms have Pressure Plates that can be triggered by players and other creatures (e.g. Royal Guards hired by [Security Contract](/wiki/Security_Contract "Security Contract")). Ghosts, followers (including [Abigail](/wiki/Abigail "Abigail")), and [Shadow Creatures](/wiki/Shadow_Creature "Shadow Creature") do not trigger the Pressure Plates. Upon activating, it will close any surrounding doors besides the exit. One or more of these plates must be weighted down with an [item](/wiki/Items "Items") to open the doors. Pressure plates may also activate [Striking Carvings](/wiki/Striking_Carving "Striking Carving").

* Pressure plates that activate doors are either lined up in long rows or in a pattern of five plates, one near each possible doorway and one in the center.
* Pressure plates that will activate Striking Carvings are typically in a pattern of nine, three equidistant rows of three.
* Some rooms contain [Nasty Spear Traps](/wiki/Nasty_Spear_Trap "Nasty Spear Trap"), which activate automatically or by proximity.

There are also [Striking Statues](/wiki/Striking_Statue "Striking Statue") that shoot darts while rotating clockwise in some treasure rooms, triggered by Pressure Plates or by chiseling the relics on the room. These are found in treasure rooms in the Palace and Fountain ruins, and in many normal rooms in the second Aporkalypse ruins.

Some rooms in the ruins may have a Suspicious Crack in the wall where a door would otherwise be. These cracks can be investigated by using a [Magnifying Glass](/wiki/Magnifying_Glass "Magnifying Glass") or [Spectoggles](/wiki/Spectoggles "Spectoggles"), after which the character will explain whether or not it is concealing a door. It is possible for suspicious cracks to lead to ordinary rooms that can already be accessed normally from another side.

The cracked area can be destroyed using [Gunpowder](/wiki/Gunpowder "Gunpowder"), [Coconades](/wiki/Coconade "Coconade"), [Slurtle Slime](/wiki/Slurtle_Slime "Slurtle Slime"), a [Weather Pain](/wiki/Weather_Pain "Weather Pain"), or by causing a cave-in with a nearby [Unimportant Pillar](/wiki/Unimportant_Pillar "Unimportant Pillar"). Upon being destroyed, if there is no door there will be a solid section of wall. If there is a door it will now show up as a pathway on the Ruins map.

[Aporkalypse Calendar](/wiki/Aporkalypse_Calendar "Aporkalypse Calendar") is always in a room accessible through Suspicious Crack.

## Bugs[]

* Dying in a different world (any of the three DLC realms, as well as [caves](/wiki/Caves "Caves"), [ruins](/wiki/Ruins "Ruins"), and the [volcano](/wiki/Volcano "Volcano")) and respawning from a [Meat Effigy](/wiki/Meat_Effigy "Meat Effigy") or [Magic Flower](/wiki/Magic_Flower "Magic Flower") that was placed inside any [Hamlet](/wiki/Don%27t_Starve:_Hamlet "Don't Starve: Hamlet") DLC interior ([Slanty Shanty](/wiki/Slanty_Shanty "Slanty Shanty") etc.) will cause the player to be respawned out in the void of the world holding the respawn structure. It is recommended to use the command c\_warp("*prefab*") to teleport the character back into the world, as walking back in can be problematic for world types such as [Shipwrecked](/wiki/Don%27t_Starve:_Shipwrecked "Don't Starve: Shipwrecked").

## Tips[]

* A good strategy for fighting enemies in the ruins involves using a [Torch](/wiki/Torch "Torch") as a weapon. Setting one on fire will cause it to panic and stop attacking the player, which is helpful due to the limited amount of space in any given room. Do not use this strategy if fighting in a room filled with Grass Tufts and Saplings, as this can quickly form an uncontrollable fire that can kill the player.
* [Disarming Tools](/wiki/Disarming_Tools "Disarming Tools") can be used on Striking Carvings to collect a [Blow Dart](/wiki/Blow_Dart "Blow Dart") from each one. Disarming all carvings in a room will allow the player to step on that room's pressure plates or chisel its Lost Relics off of their statues without worrying about taking damage.
* No room in the ruins will contain both dart-trap-triggering AND door-closing pressure plates.

## Trivia[]

* Suspicious Cracks used to be able to be opened by using an [Axe](/wiki/Axe "Axe") on them, but this feature was unintended[[1]](#cite_note-1) and removed on 12/20/2018 update.
