The **Catcoon** is a neutral [Mob](/wiki/Mob "Mob") in the *[Reign of Giants](/wiki/Don%27t_Starve:_Reign_of_Giants "Don't Starve: Reign of Giants")* DLC, spawning from a [Hollow Stump](/wiki/Hollow_Stump "Hollow Stump"). It will wander around, pouncing at small creatures and playing with various objects, sometimes destroying them. When killed, a Catcoon will drop [Meat](/wiki/Meat "Meat") and has a 33% chance of dropping a [Cat Tail](/wiki/Cat_Tail "Cat Tail"). It will also give the player 5 points of [Naughtiness](/wiki/Naughtiness "Naughtiness"), which may result in spawning Krampus.

When it sees a small [Bird](/wiki/Birds "Birds"), [Rabbit](/wiki/Rabbit "Rabbit"), [Moleworm](/wiki/Moleworm "Moleworm"), [Butterfly](/wiki/Butterfly "Butterfly"), or [Bee](/wiki/Bee "Bee") within 25[m](/wiki/Distance "Distance"), it will pounce on it and kill it. In addition to [Monsters](/wiki/Monsters "Monsters"), Catcoons are aggressive towards [Pengulls](/wiki/Pengull "Pengull") and [Grass Gekkos](/wiki/Grass_Gekko "Grass Gekko") and will attack them if they get close enough. This generally leads to the Catcoon being killed in retaliation. They will also attack other Catcoons, often committing an act of cannibalism when done. Catcoons will also attack a [Glommer](/wiki/Glommer "Glommer"), even if players have befriended both, so caution should be taken. Catcoons are hostile to [Webber](/wiki/Webber "Webber") and [Wortox](/wiki/Wortox "Wortox") since they are considered as [Monsters](/wiki/Monsters "Monsters").

Catcoons will attempt to pick up "toys" within 25m.

Whenever it starts [Raining](/wiki/Rain "Rain"), any Catcoons that are outside will try to go back to their Hollow Stump.

Catcoons can be befriended by giving them "toys" or [food](/wiki/Food "Food"), and they will give "gifts" to the player by coughing up items, such as [Rot](/wiki/Rot "Rot") and [Cut Grass](/wiki/Cut_Grass "Cut Grass"), as if it is a hairball. Each item given to the Catcoon will add a tenth of a [day](/wiki/Day "Day") (48 sec) to the time they spend following the player [[1]](#cite_note-1) (up to a maximum of 1 day [[2]](#cite_note-2)) and force the next hairball to be sooner.[[3]](#cite_note-3) The item that they give depends on the number of times they hack before coughing it up. Currently, there are two lists for neutral and friendly Catcoons.

## Don't Starve Together icon Don't Starve Together[]

In *[Don't Starve Together](/wiki/Don%27t_Starve_Together "Don't Starve Together")*, befriended Catcoons will follow the player for 48 second per "toys" or food given. If the Catcoon is able to reach the player before that time ends, it will spit out the gift and discontinue to follow. However, spitting out a gift does not mean it is no longer befriended, evident from its aggro to a wild Catcoon.

Catcoons have 4 inventory slots, items they pick up from the ground will be stored in their inventory. When their inventory slots are full, they would return to their Catcoon Den and store items in the den. They will drop all item in their inventory upon their death.

## Prototype Tips[]

* Catcoons will steal any [Trinkets](/wiki/Trinkets "Trinkets") and [Wanda](/wiki/Wanda "Wanda")'s Pocket Watches left on the ground among other things, therefore it's advisable to keep them in [Chests](/wiki/Chest "Chest") if any Catcoons live nearby.
* Many Catcoon gifts are also Catcoon toys. Players should make sure to pick up any wanted gifts as soon as they are coughed up to prevent Catcoon from getting it back.
* If [Wes](/wiki/Wes "Wes") uses [Pile o' Balloons](/wiki/Pile_o%27_Balloons "Pile o' Balloons") to befriend a Catcoon, the Catcoon will drop it on death.
* Catcoons will sometimes leave drops from the creatures they have killed. This is especially common in Worlds with Catcoons set to 'more' or 'lots' in [World Customization](/wiki/World_Customization "World Customization") and can be used to the Player's advantage.

## Mosquito Bugs[]

* [Feather Pencil](/wiki/Feather_Pencil "Feather Pencil") and [Dark Tatters](/wiki/Dark_Tatters "Dark Tatters") cannot be given Catcoon despite being its toys.
* In Hamlet, despite being irreplaceable items, the Catcoon will not drop The Blue Sow and The Jeweled Truffle on death.

## Placeholder Trivia[]

* The name "Catcoon" is a portmanteau of the words "Cat" and "Raccoon".
* [Hollow stumps](/wiki/Hollow_Stump "Hollow Stump") become deserted after spawning 9 Catcoons. This is a reference to the belief that cats have 9 lives. In DST an empty Hollow Stump will regenerate after 15-20 days, becoming an inhabited Hollow Stump.
* Catcoons may be a reference to a real-life cat breed known as the "Maine Coon", which were rumored to be a mix of cat and raccoon.
* Catcoons will occasionally attempt to climb trees, but they never succeed.
* The Catcoons' voice work was done by Kelly Graham.[[4]](#cite_note-4)
* Catcoons will occasionally be seen searching through [Chester](/wiki/Chester "Chester"), [Flowers](/wiki/Flowers "Flowers"), [Rabbit Holes](/wiki/Rabbit_Hole "Rabbit Hole"), Etc. Since Items in Chester will not go missing, this is simply a cosmetic effect.
