The **Piko** is a neutral [Mob](/wiki/Mob "Mob") exclusive to the *[Don't Starve: Hamlet](/wiki/Don%27t_Starve:_Hamlet "Don't Starve: Hamlet")* DLC. There is also a rarer orange variant called the **Orange Piko**.

Both Piko and Orange Piko spawn from [Tea Trees](/wiki/Tea_Tree "Tea Tree"), and seem more likely to spawn near clumps of several trees growing close together. Each Piko has a "home tree". They will only climb this tree, unless they are caught in a [trap](/wiki/Trap "Trap") and released. They will retreat to their home tree at dusk, when frightened, or after picking up an item. They will not leave the tree until next dawn or the player relogs. They do not seem to spawn from tea trees planted by the player, unless one is caught in a trap and put on the ground near that tree. After a few seconds, the Piko will run up the nearest tree, "infesting" it with Piko. Chopping down an infested tree returns some of the items stolen by its Piko. However, there is a bug which causes the infested [tree](/wiki/Tea_Tree "Tea Tree")'s stump to still spawn and harbor Pikos. Digging up the stump solves this. Player-infested trees behave identically to naturally spawned ones.

An infested tree always spawns the same color Piko each day, unless it's piko is killed or captured, in which case the color of the next piko is random.

If an item is dropped on the ground (either [edible](/wiki/Food "Food") or not) near a Piko or Orange Piko, there is a chance that it will stock it in its cheeks and flee with it. It is common when you chop a [Tea Tree](/wiki/Tea_Tree "Tea Tree") that Pikos may spawn or race in from nearby trees and steal one of your [Logs](/wiki/Logs "Logs"), [Twigs](/wiki/Twigs "Twigs"), or [Seed Pods](/wiki/Seed_Pod "Seed Pod"). Take caution not to drop stacks of an item, as one Piko is able to steal it all.

Pikos will run from the player, unless they are trying to steal an item on the ground or there is a [full moon](/wiki/Moon_Cycle "Moon Cycle")/[aporkalypse](/wiki/Aporkalypse "Aporkalypse") in progress.

During a [Full Moon](/wiki/Full_Moon "Full Moon") or [Aporkalypse](/wiki/Aporkalypse "Aporkalypse"), Pikos will glow, their eyes turn red and they become aggressive. In addition, an attack from a Piko or Orange Piko will, like frogs, cause the item in the leftmost slot of the inventory bar to drop from the player's inventory. After said item is on the ground, the Piko/Orange Piko prioritizes storing the item in its cheeks and retreating to its home [Tea Tree](/wiki/Tea_Tree "Tea Tree").

Pikos can be caught using a [Trap](/wiki/Trap "Trap"). During a [Full Moon](/wiki/Moon_Cycle "Moon Cycle"), carrying a captured Piko will cause the player to emit a reddish glow, similar to the effects of eating a [Glow Berry](/wiki/Glow_Berry "Glow Berry").

## Prototype Tips[]

* Be very careful when chopping [Tea Trees](/wiki/Tea_Tree "Tea Tree") at night with a [Lantern](/wiki/Lantern "Lantern") on the ground. Pikos might steal it, and without any other light sources you will be completely vulnerable to [Charlie](/wiki/Charlie "Charlie").
* Player can infest a tree with Pikos by releasing any captured piko nearby and letting it climb the tree.
* Using an item to bait a Piko is an easy way to kill them, as they prioritize retrieving the item and running away with it rather than running from players when approached. Players can get free hits on Pikos as they run toward the item. If the Piko picked up the item, the player can retrieve it by killing the Piko, as they run toward a given Tea Tree in a predictable straight line.
  + Pikos cannot pick up backpacks, but will relentlessly try to anyways, making for very effective bait.
* Interestingly, [Abigail](/wiki/Abigail "Abigail") hates Pikos and will attack them if they are too near. An easy way to farm them for morsels.
* Pikos can be used to obtain drops from pig guards without other nearby guards aggroing the player. If a piko picks up a guard drop then is killed it will drop what it picked up and the guards will no longer identify it as belonging to a guard and the player can pick it up without getting attacked.

## Mosquito Bugs[]

* Pikos can hide in Tree stumps as if the tree was still present and will thus run up an invisible tree and disappear.
* If dropped in a shop, game can crash.
* if it tries to get a wet sunken object, you hitting it makes it walk on water. once it is on water, it will try to get back to its tea tree, but you have to hit it again for it to get back on land. you cannot use this to retrieve wet sunken objects as if you do it will stay as a wet sunken object, but on land.
* Pikos will try to steal [Backpack](/wiki/Backpack "Backpack") and other things which add inventory slots.
* If you kill a Piko just before it gets trapped it provides you with a morsel and a trapped Piko.
* If you kill or capture an aggressive Piko on a full moon, another non-aggressive Piko will immediately spawn from it's tree.

## Placeholder Trivia[]

* The use of [Orange Pikos](/wiki/Orange_Piko "Orange Piko") in making Tea and Iced Tea is a pun on [orange pekoe tea](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tea_leaf_grading#Orange_pekoe "wikipedia:Tea leaf grading").
* The Piko may be loosely based off of the real-life [pika](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pika "wikipedia:Pika"), who are known to steal food from its own kind, and [pica](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pica_(disorder) "wikipedia:Pica (disorder)"), an eating disorder characterized by the consumption of inedible items.
