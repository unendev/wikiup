The **Boat Kit** is a craftable [Item](/wiki/Item "Item") in the [Seafaring Filter](/wiki/Seafaring_Filter "Seafaring Filter") exclusive to *[Don't Starve Together](/wiki/Don%27t_Starve_Together "Don't Starve Together")*, introduced in *[Return of Them](/wiki/Return_of_Them "Return of Them")*. It can be crafted with 4 [Boards](/wiki/Boards "Boards") and needs a [Think Tank](/wiki/Think_Tank "Think Tank") to prototype. When placed on the [Ocean](/wiki/Ocean "Ocean"), it will create a **Boat**. Compare: [Grass Raft](/wiki/Grass_Raft "Grass Raft") and [Archaic Boat](/wiki/Archaic_Boat "Archaic Boat").

Players can move a Boat by using an [Oar](/wiki/Oar "Oar"), a [Driftwood Oar](/wiki/Driftwood_Oar "Driftwood Oar"), [Battle Paddle](/wiki/Battle_Paddle "Battle Paddle"), or a [Malbatross Bill](/wiki/Malbatross_Bill "Malbatross Bill"). [Masts](/wiki/Mast "Mast") can be built on a Boat to obtain a constant thrust. The [Steering Wheel Kit](/wiki/Steering_Wheel_Kit "Steering Wheel Kit") is used to direct the thrust of all equipped masts. An [Anchor Kit](/wiki/Anchor_Kit "Anchor Kit") can be built on a Boat to slow down or stop.

A Boat starts with 200 health, represented by the icon Boat. It takes damage by colliding with certain objects in the water such as [Sea Stacks](/wiki/Sea_Stack "Sea Stack") or [Salt Formations](/wiki/Salt_Formation "Salt Formation"), coasts, or the border of the world. Taking damage can create a leak in the boat that does constant damage (-0.5 Boat/s per small leak and -1 Boat/s per large leak) until a [Boat Patch](/wiki/Boat_Patch "Boat Patch"), [Trusty Tape](/wiki/Trusty_Tape "Trusty Tape") or [Tree Jam](/wiki/Tree_Jam "Tree Jam") is used on the leak. Boats can be repaired by using wood material.

Leaks can also be closed temporarily with any items by dropping them onto the leak. The item will remain there for a period of time (depending on the weight of the item) before it gets thrown up into the air. Even players can close the leak by standing on it, but will receive [Wetness](/wiki/Wetness "Wetness") over time.

Being made of wood, the boat itself is flammable and can catch fire (either from placing an open [Campfire](/wiki/Campfire "Campfire") on it, or having the fire spread to other onboard flammable items), causing heavy damage to the boat depending on how many current segments are currently in flames (there are 5 segments total, the center, and 4 on each side). Contained fires, such as a [Firepit](/wiki/Firepit "Firepit") or [Scaled Furnace](/wiki/Scaled_Furnace "Scaled Furnace"), can be safely placed and used on the boat.

In addition to sailing, a boat can be used to bridge together small gaps between land branches. This allows players to speed up their travel at little cost.

When Boat reaches 0 the boat sinks and all players [drown](#Drowning_Efects). The boat leaves 3 [Boards](/wiki/Boards "Boards") and half of the materials used to create all the structures that were build on the boat.

Each Boat has one plank, which can be used to abandon ship with the same effects as drowning.

## Drowning Effects[]

When [Characters](/wiki/Characters "Characters") drown, they reappear on the nearest coast with 25% less max health (similar to when being ressurected by [Telltale Heart](/wiki/Telltale_Heart "Telltale Heart")), 25 less [Hunger](/wiki/Hunger "Hunger"), 25 less [Sanity](/wiki/Sanity "Sanity"), 100% [Wetness](/wiki/Wetness "Wetness") and with half of their inventory slots dropped into the water where they have drowned. [WX-78](/wiki/WX-78 "WX-78") loses 40% of their max health and 50 sanity. [Woodie](/wiki/Woodie "Woodie") loses 50 hunger in normal form and 100 wereness when he is in his [Werebeaver](/wiki/Werebeaver "Werebeaver") or [Weremoose](/wiki/Weremoose "Weremoose") form. [Wurt](/wiki/Wurt "Wurt") does not lose any stats, does not drop items, and does not receive health penalty, but gets 50 wetness.

## Tips[]

* Boats are perfect for bridging small gaps in between stretches of land, thus creating shortcuts to desired locations.
* Players can put [Houndius Shootius](/wiki/Houndius_Shootius "Houndius Shootius") on the boat and anchor the boat offshore to cheese mobs and [Bosses](/wiki/Boss_Monsters "Boss Monsters") on land. This method does not work on the [Hounds](/wiki/Hound "Hound") though because they can swim to the boat. [Deerclops](/wiki/Deerclops "Deerclops") and [Bearger](/wiki/Bearger "Bearger") will move out of the [Houndius Shootius](/wiki/Houndius_Shootius "Houndius Shootius")'s range if they are hit and cannot reach [Houndius Shootius](/wiki/Houndius_Shootius "Houndius Shootius") to hit it back.
* A few mobs can actually jump onto your boat, including [Pigs](/wiki/Pig "Pig"), [Hounds](/wiki/Hound "Hound"), etc. You could use this to your advantage.
* [Deerclops](/wiki/Deerclops "Deerclops"), [Moose/Goose](/wiki/Moose/Goose "Moose/Goose"), [Klaus](/wiki/Klaus "Klaus") (including his [Gem Deer](/wiki/Gem_Deer "Gem Deer"), red Gem Deer's fire geyser does NOT ignite the boat on fire) are unable to damage the boat by any means, [Bearger](/wiki/Bearger "Bearger") can damage and push the boat with his shockwave attack. This can be used to "cheese" certain bosses, as jumping on the boat in the right time allows the player to avoid the damage and effect caused by the boss.
* [Lureplants](/wiki/Meat_Bulb_(Lureplant) "Meat Bulb (Lureplant)") don't spawn Eyeplants when on a boat. You could use this to easily get Leafy Meat.
