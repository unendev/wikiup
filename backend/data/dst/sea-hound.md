A **Sea Hound** is a hostile [Mob](/wiki/Mob "Mob") found in the *[Shipwrecked](/wiki/Don%27t_Starve:_Shipwrecked "Don't Starve: Shipwrecked")* DLC. They may be encountered alone at sea, or attack the player in large packs while they are sailing.

[Meats](/wiki/Meats "Meats") dropped into any part of the [Ocean](/wiki/Ocean "Ocean") have a chance to spawn either one [Crocodog](/wiki/Crocodog "Crocodog") or 2-4 Sea Hounds (60% chance of a Crocodog and 40% chance of Sea Hounds). The meat does not have to be dropped by the player themselves. The probability of an attack is based on the hunger value of the item dropped into the sea, and can be calculated by dividing the hunger value of a single item in the stack by 200. This means that the size of the stack does not increase the odds of an attack, but a large number of separate stacks of meat (such as those dropped by puncturing a bloated [Whale](/wiki/Whale "Whale") carcass) can trigger a massive feeding frenzy. [Monster Food](/wiki/Monster_Food "Monster Food"), [Roe](/wiki/Roe "Roe"), and [Shark Fins](/wiki/Shark_Fin "Shark Fin") will not trigger an attack.

Because they lose interest in the player easily, Sea Hounds may get distributed in the ocean and later be re-encountered.

## Prototype Tips[]

* Unlike Crocodogs, Sea Hounds cannot be stun-locked or set on fire. Their fast attack speed and spawning in groups also makes kiting and avoiding damage very difficult. Their only weaknesses are their relatively low health and inability to move onto land.
* The [Quackering Ram](/wiki/Quackering_Ram "Quackering Ram") and the [Harpoon](/wiki/Harpoon "Harpoon") are perfect to use against them as Sea Hounds will die in one hit to these weapons and the player is able to easily avoid damage. A [Trident](/wiki/Trident "Trident") is also very effective if the player can dodge the Sea Hound’s first attack since this weapon will kill it before it can make a second attack.
* Using poisoned, ranged weapons like the [Poison Spear](/wiki/Poison_Spear "Poison Spear") + [Speargun](/wiki/Speargun "Speargun") or [Poison Dart](/wiki/Poison_Dart "Poison Dart") against Sea Hounds can make kiting significantly easier as it slows their movement and attacks to more manageable speeds.

## Placeholder Trivia[]

* Sea Hounds don't attack [Sea Walls](/wiki/Sea_Wall "Sea Wall").
* Sea Hounds were once used to replace [Hounds](/wiki/Hound "Hound") in their periodic attacks, if the player was at sea. This feature was removed in the *[Home Sea Home](/wiki/Don%27t_Starve:_Shipwrecked#November_21st,_2017_-_Home_Sea_Home_Update "Don't Starve: Shipwrecked")* update, adding [Crocodogs](/wiki/Crocodog "Crocodog") which can traverse both land and water. While Hounds can no longer be encountered in the DLC at all (except via the [Slot Machine](/wiki/Slot_Machine "Slot Machine")), Sea Hounds were accidentally removed from the game, but were later added back in their current functionality.
* A [Cargo Boat](/wiki/Cargo_Boat "Cargo Boat") may randomly be found at sea containing some loot, but jumping on the boat will trigger a Sea Hound attack.
* When Sea Hounds are approaching, Wilson may comment with "I'm going to need a bigger boat!". This is a reference to the movie *[Jaws](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jaws_(film) "wikipedia:Jaws (film)")*, in which a lead character says "We're gonna need a bigger boat!", while being stalked by a shark.
* According to Wickerbottom, the scientific name for Sea Hound is *Houndus Selachii*. "*Houndus*" is a play on the word "Hound", while *[Selachii](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Shark "wikipedia:Shark")* is the order of sharks. Interestingly enough, sharks in real life were historically called "sea dogs" by sailors.
