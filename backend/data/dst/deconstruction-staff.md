The **Deconstruction Staff** is a craftable [Item](/wiki/Items "Items") found in the [Ancient Tab](/wiki/Ancient_Tab "Ancient Tab"). It requires at least a [Broken Pseudoscience Station](/wiki/Broken_Pseudoscience_Station "Broken Pseudoscience Station") to craft, and costs 4 [Nightmare Fuel](/wiki/Nightmare_Fuel "Nightmare Fuel"), 2 [Living Logs](/wiki/Living_Log "Living Log"), and 2 [Green Gems](/wiki/Green_Gem "Green Gem") to craft. It also can randomly drop from an Ancient Pseudoscience Station when the latter is damaged with a [Hammer](/wiki/Hammer "Hammer"), [Gunpowder](/wiki/Gunpowder "Gunpowder") etc. Like all items from the Ancient Tab, Deconstruction Staves cannot be prototyped and can only be crafted within range of an [Ancient Pseudoscience Station](/wiki/Ancient_Pseudoscience_Station "Ancient Pseudoscience Station").

When used on a craftable [Structure](/wiki/Structure "Structure"), the Deconstruction Staff returns 100% of the ingredients needed to craft it. However, when used on an Item, the Staff will return a percentage of the materials equal to the remaining durability, rounded up. If [Gems](/wiki/Gems "Gems") were used in the crafting process, they will not be returned and will always "shatter" instead. With each use of the Staff, [Characters](/wiki/Characters "Characters") will lose 20 Sanity and the Staff will lose 20% of its [Durability](/wiki/Durability "Durability").

Using it on a [Pig House](/wiki/Pig_House "Pig House"), [Rabbit Hutch](/wiki/Rabbit_Hutch "Rabbit Hutch"), [Bird Cage](/wiki/Bird_Cage "Bird Cage"), or [Bird Trap](/wiki/Bird_Trap "Bird Trap") will get rid of any inhabitants still inside it. When used on a [Meat Effigy](/wiki/Meat_Effigy "Meat Effigy"), it will not restore the 30 Health (40 Health in *[Don't Starve Together](/wiki/Don%27t_Starve_Together "Don't Starve Together")*) until the user dies or saves and quits.

When deconstructing items produced with living mobs like bees or rabbits, you may wish to be prepared to catch those animals again once they are released from the item.

[Green Gems](/wiki/Green_Gem "Green Gem") are rare and limit the number of Deconstruction Staffs. A [Construction Amulet](/wiki/Construction_Amulet "Construction Amulet") is always worth using when crafting this staff and this staff is the only item it is always worth using for.

## Deconstructing Craftables[]

### Craftables Created with a Construction Amulet[]

Any [craftable](/wiki/Crafting "Crafting") object can be deconstructed, but most are not worth the cost (thulecite is rare, green gems are rarer). The following table lists some commonly deconstructed items. It assumes the item was crafted with a [Construction Amulet](/wiki/Construction_Amulet "Construction Amulet") and shows net cost of the construction and deconstruction and net gain. Items produced without a construction amulet are covered in separate tables below.

#### Explanation[]

Assuming the construction amulet was used to craft the staff, the effective cost of the staff is Green Gem×1.2 Thulecite×0.4 Living Log×1 Nightmare Fuel×2.6. The staff can be used 5 times, so dividing the cost by 5 yields the cost per use: Green Gem×0.24 Thulecite×0.08 Living Log×0.2 Nightmare Fuel×0.52

The best candidates for construction/deconstruction are items that use (even) multiples of a rare ingredient and no gems (except the [Iridescent Gem](/wiki/Iridescent_Gem "Iridescent Gem") - all other gems are destroyed during deconstruction).

### Craftables Created WITHOUT a Construction Amulet[]

[Scaled Flooring](/wiki/Scaled_Flooring "Scaled Flooring") is worth deconstructing even when produced without the construction amulet (scales are probably rarer than green gems).

### Deconstruction to renew durability[]

When using deconstruction to renew durability, the profit is the extended use of the item and/or components, not (only) a resource that can be used to create other items. Deconstructing a heavily used [Pan Flute](/wiki/Pan_Flute "Pan Flute") preserves the mandrake of which there may be only ever be 3 or 4 in a world. Deconstructing a [Pick/Axe](/wiki/Pick/Axe "Pick/Axe"), yields a brand new Opulent Pickaxe and Luxury Axe, which can be used down to 3% durability before constructing a new Pick/Axe with them.

Deconstructing [Scalemail](/wiki/Scalemail "Scalemail") directly yields 1 scales as opposed to the 0.75 scales created when deconstructing a single Scaled Floring. This is a good way to change your mind about Scalemail, but Scaled Flooring is the way to multiply scales.

## Prototype Tips[]

* Since Green Gems are so rare, the best use for Deconstruction Staffs is on Items or Structures that require very rare or non-renewable materials (e.g. items from the Ancient or [Magic Tab](/wiki/Magic_Tab "Magic Tab")).
* Some items that are created using a single non-renewable ingredient (e.g. [Pan Flute](/wiki/Pan_Flute "Pan Flute")) can be deconstructed and crafted again to extend their usage, since the Staff will always return at least one of the non-gem materials used.
* The Deconstruction Staff cannot be used on [Walls](/wiki/Wall "Wall") or a [Houndius Shootius](/wiki/Houndius_Shootius "Houndius Shootius") that have been placed.
* In the *Reign of Giants* DLC, using the Deconstruction Staff on burnt Structures will still return 100% of their materials.
* Using the Deconstruction Staff on a single item from a recipe that gives multiple copies of said item (e.g. [Turfs](/wiki/Turfs "Turfs") or [Walls](/wiki/Wall "Wall")) will return the main item + part of the recipe. For example, deconstructing 1 [Scaled Flooring](/wiki/Scaled_Flooring "Scaled Flooring") will return 1 [Scales](/wiki/Scales "Scales") and 1 [Cut Stone](/wiki/Cut_Stone "Cut Stone").
