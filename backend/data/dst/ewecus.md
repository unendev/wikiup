**Ewecus** is an aggressive [Mob](/wiki/Mob "Mob") that may spawn at the end of a series of [Suspicious Dirt Piles](/wiki/Suspicious_Dirt_Pile "Suspicious Dirt Pile"). Upon death, it drops 4 [Meat](/wiki/Meat "Meat"), 2-3 [Steel Wool](/wiki/Steel_Wool "Steel Wool"), & 1-2 [Phlegm](/wiki/Phlegm "Phlegm"). If left alive, it drops [Manure](/wiki/Manure "Manure") periodically, similar to [Beefalo](/wiki/Beefalo "Beefalo") and [Koalefants](/wiki/Koalefant "Koalefant").

It utilizes the same increased spawning chance as a [Varg](/wiki/Varg "Varg"), starting at 5% on [Day](/wiki/Day "Day") 1 and gradually increasing to 33% by Day 100 (these values will be halved for finding Ewecus specifically). This makes hunting for Koalefants increasingly risky.

## Icon Fight Combat[]

Ewecus has a short-ranged melee attack, when it will attempt to [kite](/wiki/Glossary#kite "Glossary") the player, and a long-ranged projectile attack, during which it spits mucus at the player; it will halt the player's [movement](/wiki/Player_Speed "Player Speed") on impact, similar to being frozen in the *[Reign of Giants](/wiki/Reign_of_Giants "Reign of Giants")* [DLC](/wiki/DLC "DLC"). The effect will fade over time; but befriended [Bunnymen](/wiki/Bunnyman "Bunnyman") Don't Starve Together, [Mants](/wiki/Mant "Mant") Hamlet, [Pigs](/wiki/Pig "Pig"), [Royal Guards](/wiki/Royal_Guard "Royal Guard") Hamlet, or other players Don't Starve Together can free the stuck player immediately. Ewecus won't use ranged mucus attack if player is sitting on [Beefalo](/wiki/Beefalo "Beefalo") or [Woby](/wiki/Woby "Woby").

Ewecus will typically only use their melee attack on a player who is stuck from its mucus attack.

## Spear Hunting[]

It is possible to dodge the projectile attack by running into the Ewecus when it is about to spit. When the spit animation starts stop your attack and run into the Ewecus thereby pushing it. This will dodge the attack and avoids getting immobilized.[[1]](#cite_note-1)[[2]](#cite_note-2)

Another strategy is to either use darts or a group of allies; it cannot be effectively kited solo without using a mount. Alternatively, it can be kited while sitting on beefalo; however, precise timing might be necessary since attack window is very small (smaller than for [Pig](/wiki/Pig "Pig")).

## Placeholder Trivia[]

* Ewecus was introduced to *[Don't Starve Together](/wiki/Don%27t_Starve_Together "Don't Starve Together")* in the *[Stuck in the Middle with Ewe](/wiki/Don%27t_Starve_Together/Version_History#December_2nd,_2014_-_Stuck_in_the_Middle_with_Ewe "Don't Starve Together/Version History")* update, and to *[Don't Starve (DS)](/wiki/Don%27t_Starve "Don't Starve")* in the *[Quality of Life](/wiki/Don%27t_Starve/Version_History "Don't Starve/Version History")* update.
* Ewecus is considered part of *[A New Reign](/wiki/A_New_Reign "A New Reign")*, even though it was introduced before the official start of the beta branch.
* The name "Ewecus" is a portmanteau of the words "Ewe" (a female sheep) and "Mucus".
* If one manages to herd a Ewecus in a pen with second tier [Walls](/wiki/Wall "Wall"), they can keep it as a pet without danger, since the Walls intercept its projectile attacks.
* [Wickerbottom](/wiki/Wickerbottom "Wickerbottom")'s description of the Ewecus (*Ovis chalybs*) is Latin for "steel sheep".
* In the game's files, there are a few unused files of Ewecus' body with most of its wool shaved off, originally meant for shaving it with a [Razor](/wiki/Razor "Razor") for Steel Wool.

## Mosquito Bugs[]

* In *DS*, befriended Bunnymen cannot help a stuck player.
* In DST, occasionally Ewecus will randomly become neutral and run from the player, similar to a Koalefant, though they will revert to normal upon being attacked
