The **Axe** is a [Tool](/wiki/Tool "Tool") that can be used to chop down [Trees](/wiki/Tree "Tree"). It can be crafted at the beginning of the game with 1 [Twig](/wiki/Twig "Twig") and 1 [Flint](/wiki/Flint "Flint"). An Axe can also be used as a weapon, dealing 27.2 Health damage on each hit. With either usage, an Axe has enough durability for 100 swings. When used to chop down Trees, Axe requires 5 chops for small, 10 for medium, and 15 for large Trees. Dead or burnt Trees require only one swing. Currently, the amount of chopping required is independent of the type of the Tree.

The **Luxury Axe** is a version of the normal Axe that has four times as much durability and requires [Gold Nuggets](/wiki/Gold_Nugget "Gold Nugget") instead of Flint, making it easily renewable. It requires 4 Twigs and 2 Gold Nuggets to craft, and an [Alchemy Engine](/wiki/Alchemy_Engine "Alchemy Engine") to prototype. It has 400 uses, 4 times as many as the regular Axe. When used as a weapon it deals the same amount of damage as a regular Axe. It also has the same efficiency as the regular Axe, requiring 5, 10, and 15 chops to fell trees depending on their size.

The **Moon Glass Axe** is a version of the normal **Axe** that is exclusive to *[Don't Starve Together](/wiki/Don%27t_Starve_Together "Don't Starve Together")*, introduced in *[Return of Them](/wiki/Return_of_Them "Return of Them")*. It can be crafted with 2 [Twigs](/wiki/Twigs "Twigs") and 3 [Moon Shards](/wiki/Moon_Shard "Moon Shard"), however doing so requires that the player to be close to the [Celestial Altar](/wiki/Celestial_Altar "Celestial Altar"). It is 2.5 times more efficient than the normal Axe. This means that Small Trees will take 2 hits, medium Trees will take 4 hits, and large Trees will take 6 hits to be chopped down. When used to attack [shadow creatures](/wiki/Shadow_Creature "Shadow Creature"), [shadow pieces](/wiki/Shadow_Pieces "Shadow Pieces") or [Reanimated Skeletons](/wiki/Reanimated_Skeleton "Reanimated Skeleton") it will lose less durability, and viceversa, it will lose more applications if used to attack any other unit.

The Moon Glass Axe was introduced in the *[Turn of Tides](/wiki/Return_of_Them#Turn_of_Tides "Return of Them")* update.

The **Obsidian Axe** is another version of Axe exclusive to the *[Shipwrecked](/wiki/Don%27t_Starve:_Shipwrecked "Don't Starve: Shipwrecked")* DLC. It requires 1 Axe, 1 [Dragoon Heart](/wiki/Dragoon_Heart "Dragoon Heart"), and 2 [Obsidian](/wiki/Obsidian "Obsidian") to craft. The Obsidian Axe is also a possible reward from [X Marks the Spot](/wiki/X_Marks_the_Spot "X Marks the Spot") treasure chests. Like all items in the [Volcanic Tab](/wiki/Volcanic_Tab "Volcanic Tab"), it cannot be prototyped and the player must be wearing a [Brain of Thought](/wiki/Brain_of_Thought "Brain of Thought") or be near an [Obsidian Workbench](/wiki/Obsidian_Workbench "Obsidian Workbench") to craft it again.

The Obsidian Axe can be used to chop down [Trees](/wiki/Tree "Tree") with 2.5 times the efficiency of the standard Axe, in addition to having 250 uses, which is 2.5 times of the uses for the standard version. Small Trees will take 2 hits, medium Trees will take 4 hits, and large Trees will take 6 hits to chop down.

As the Obsidian Axe is used (either as a tool or a weapon), its charge level will increase. As the charge level increases, the Obsidian Axe will produce more heat and light, which protects the players from [Charlie](/wiki/Charlie "Charlie"), but can cause [Overheating](/wiki/Overheating "Overheating"). It can also be used as a weapon, initially dealing the same damage as the standard Axe per hit, but after gaining 75 charges, it will start igniting any target struck and deal up to 2x damage based on the number of charges. The charge level decreases over time as long as the axe isn't used and dropping it into the [Ocean](/wiki/Ocean "Ocean") will completely remove its charge. An Obsidian Axe will not gain any charges during [Hurricane Season](/wiki/Hurricane_Season "Hurricane Season"). Similar to [Thermal Stone](/wiki/Thermal_Stone "Thermal Stone"), the Axe will start glowing as it gains charges.

Players should take caution when using an Obsidian Axe with high levels of charge, as any Tree ignited by the Obsidian Axe can potentially ignite other nearby trees, resources or [Structures](/wiki/Structure "Structure"). Burnt Trees can be chopped down with a single strike with any axe to obtain [Charcoal](/wiki/Charcoal "Charcoal"), and the Obsidian Axe will not reignite the burnt tree or the Charcoal. When using this item as a weapon, players should remember that the Mobs that are killed while on fire will drop [cooked](/wiki/Cooking "Cooking") variants of the [food](/wiki/Food "Food") items and ashes for flammable resources they would otherwise drop. Some Mobs will also panic when on fire, running in random directions, potentially igniting other mobs, resources or Structures around them.

## Downloadable Content[]

In the *[Shipwrecked](/wiki/Don%27t_Starve:_Shipwrecked "Don't Starve: Shipwrecked")* DLC, Axes can be obtained from the [Slot Machine](/wiki/Slot_Machine "Slot Machine") and [Steamer Trunk](/wiki/Steamer_Trunk "Steamer Trunk"). Luxury Axes can be obtained from the Slot Machine and [X Marks the Spot](/wiki/X_Marks_the_Spot "X Marks the Spot") and by using a [Trawl Net](/wiki/Trawl_Net "Trawl Net") in the [Ocean](/wiki/Ocean "Ocean").

In the *[Hamlet](/wiki/Don%27t_Starve:_Hamlet "Don't Starve: Hamlet")* DLC, Axes can be bought for 5 [Oincs](/wiki/Oinc "Oinc") at the [Pigg and Pigglet's General Store](/wiki/Pigg_and_Pigglet%27s_General_Store "Pigg and Pigglet's General Store") in the [Pig City](/wiki/Pig_City "Pig City"). Three of them are also required to craft the Pigg and Pigglet's General Store using the [Key to the City](/wiki/Key_to_the_City "Key to the City").

## Usage[]

To chop down a Tree with the Axe, players can right-click it from the inventory to equip it and left-click the Tree (Or press "X" in the console edition). Once the player begins to chop the Tree, holding down the LMB or Spacebar will have the player character keep chopping until the Tree falls. Alternatively, one can chop faster by repeatedly clicking LMB with specific timing.

In addition to chopping down trees, Axes are used in the following crafting recipes.

Outside of chopping trees, the Axe can be a cheap tool to be used as a weapon for early game survival.

## Silver Bowl The Gorge[]

In [The Gorge](/wiki/The_Gorge "The Gorge") [event](/wiki/Category:Events "Category:Events") for *[Don't Starve Together](/wiki/Don%27t_Starve_Together "Don't Starve Together")*, an Axe can be found between two [Fire Pits](/wiki/Fire_Pit "Fire Pit") near the [Mossy Gateway](/wiki/Ancient_Gateway#The_Gorge "Ancient Gateway"), next to two Logs. More Axes can be purchased from the [Swamp Pig Elder](/wiki/Swamp_Pig_Elder "Swamp Pig Elder") for five Logs. Axes in The Gorge have infinite durability and have a Victorian-inspired appearance. This new appearance is available as a [Skin](/wiki/Skins "Skins").

## Trivia[]

* The Luxury Axe was added in the [October 2, 2012](/wiki/New_Features#October_2.2C_2012.5B18.5D "New Features") update.
* The Axe used to have a value of 1-10 [Science Points](/wiki/Research_points_list "Research points list") depending on its durability when used on a [Science Machine](/wiki/Science_Machine "Science Machine"), while the Luxury Axe gave 1-35.
* [Lucy the Axe](/wiki/Lucy_the_Axe "Lucy the Axe"), a character-specific item, is given to [Woodie](/wiki/Woodie "Woodie") upon spawn. It acts as an infinite durability Axe that chops Trees much faster but does less damage.
* When the Luxury Axe is equipped, a 'ding' sound can be heard.
* In reality, gold is not a practical metal to be used in tools or weapons as it is a soft metal; it would easily deform and break. This is often remarked when [examined](/wiki/Character_quotes "Character quotes") by some [Characters](/wiki/Characters "Characters").
* Axes deal the same amount of damage as [Boomerangs](/wiki/Boomerang "Boomerang").

* A charged Obsidian Axe placed inside [Chester](/wiki/Chester "Chester"), [Packim Baggims](/wiki/Packim_Baggims "Packim Baggims"), or [Ro Bin](/wiki/Ro_Bin "Ro Bin") will cause the follower to glow until the charge fades.

## Bugs[]

* When playing as [Woodie](/wiki/Woodie "Woodie") in *Shipwrecked*, the Obsidian Axe can talk like [Lucy](/wiki/Lucy "Lucy").
