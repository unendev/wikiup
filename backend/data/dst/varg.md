The **Varg**, also known as the **Warg**, is an aggressive Mob in the *[Reign of Giants](/wiki/Reign_of_Giants "Reign of Giants")* DLC. When tracking [Suspicious Dirt Piles](/wiki/Suspicious_Dirt_Pile "Suspicious Dirt Pile"), there is a chance for a Varg to be found instead of a [Koalefant](/wiki/Koalefant "Koalefant") or an [Ewecus](/wiki/Ewecus "Ewecus"). This chance starts at 2.5% on [Day](/wiki/Day "Day") one, and gradually increases to 16.5% chance on Day 100. There is also a chance that a Varg can spawn in place of a hound attack in [Don't Starve Together](/wiki/Don%27t_Starve_Together "Don't Starve Together"). When killed, the Varg drops around 4-6 [Monster Meat](/wiki/Monster_Meat "Monster Meat") and 1-3 [Hound's Tooth](/wiki/Hound%27s_Tooth "Hound's Tooth").

The Varg takes 3 hits with the [Ice Staff](/wiki/Ice_Staff "Ice Staff") to be frozen, and 1 [Sleep Dart](/wiki/Sleep_Dart "Sleep Dart") to be put to sleep.

## Attack Pattern[]

The Varg will howl, calling forth two [Hounds](/wiki/Hounds "Hounds") to the battle. Vargs are able to summon both normal and special Hounds (based on the [season](/wiki/Season "Season")), which is decided at random. It should be noted that [Blue Hounds](/wiki/Blue_Hound "Blue Hound") can appear in [Winter](/wiki/Winter "Winter") and [Spring](/wiki/Spring "Spring") while [Red Hounds](/wiki/Red_Hound "Red Hound") can appear in [Summer](/wiki/Summer "Summer") and [Autumn](/wiki/Autumn "Autumn").

A Varg's Hounds are bound to it, and will immediately aggro on whatever the Varg itself was targeting when it summoned them. Summoned Hounds will also return to their Varg if they lose interest in their current target. If engaged for long enough, the Varg will howl again to summon more Hounds.

At close range it attacks by biting, with a two-hit delay. It moves slowly, but attacks quickly and has a large range for its jaws.

## Icon Tools Usage[]

The Varg can effectively be used as a way to spawn Hounds if there are no nearby [Hound Mounds](/wiki/Hound_Mounds "Hound Mounds") (or all the Hound Mounds in the current world are destroyed).

One way to do this is by leading the Varg into a pen with a large gap in the side. The player should run into the pen and put the Varg to sleep once it follows inside. The player must then exit the pen and close the gap. The Varg will still summon Hounds if the player wanders too close, but it will not attack any of the walls that confine it.

## Prototype Tips[]

* A Varg has a limit of 6-8 Hound followers, after which it will stop summoning them. If any of its spawn dies, or moves too far away from the Varg, it will attempt to summon more.
* Sleeping Vargs may be woken up by the barking of any nearby Hounds. They may also awaken if the player stands too close to them for too long, similar to [Beefalo](/wiki/Beefalo "Beefalo") in heat.
* The Varg will always howl to summon Hounds when it can do so. It is even able to do so for a brief moment after it has died after its death animation.
* Vargs can potentially be very dangerous and are usually a threat to even the most well-armed of players. Besides their ability to be a hound farm, Vargs have little usefulness as their drops are easily obtainable by other means. As such, it is recommended to simply stay away from them, as they drop minimal loot and are extremely dangerous.

## Placeholder Trivia[]

* The Warg is a wolf-like creature from Norse mythology. The term was used to refer to [Fenrir](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fenrir "wikipedia:Fenrir") and his offspring.
  + "Varg," as it is referred to in-game, is also the Swedish word for "wolf."
* [J. R. R. Tolkien](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/J._R._R._Tolkien "wikipedia:J. R. R. Tolkien") popularized Wargs in his fantasy novels set in Middle Earth, like *[The Hobbit](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Hobbit "wikipedia:The Hobbit")* and *[The Lord of the Rings](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Lord_Of_The_Rings "wikipedia:The Lord Of The Rings")*. They are also popular in *[Dungeons & Dragons](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dungeons_%26_Dragons "wikipedia:Dungeons & Dragons")*, where they are known as "Worgs" or "Dire Wolves".
* If a Varg is spawned in the [Caves](/wiki/Caves "Caves") or [Ruins](/wiki/Ruins "Ruins"), it will summon [Depths Worms](/wiki/Depths_Worm "Depths Worm") instead of Hounds.
* Out of all the creatures that drop Monster Meat, a Varg can drop the second highest number, with a maximum of six, beaten only by the [Pugalisk](/wiki/Pugalisk "Pugalisk"), which can drop up to 16.
* Vargs were added to *[Don't Starve Together](/wiki/Don%27t_Starve_Together "Don't Starve Together")* before the rest of the *Reign of Giants* DLC in the *[Stuck in the Middle with Ewe](/wiki/Don%27t_Starve_Together_Version_History "Don't Starve Together Version History")* update.
* Vargs were featured during the *[Year of the Varg](/wiki/Year_of_the_Varg "Year of the Varg")* [event](/wiki/Category:Events "Category:Events") for *[Don't Starve Together](/wiki/Don%27t_Starve_Together "Don't Starve Together")* in early 2018.
