The **Deadly Brightshade** is a [Mob](/wiki/Mob "Mob") exclusive to *[Don't Starve Together](/wiki/Don%27t_Starve_Together "Don't Starve Together"),* introduced within the *[From Beyond](/wiki/From_Beyond "From Beyond")* updates. They appear when [Brightshade Gestalts](/wiki/Brightshade_Gestalt "Brightshade Gestalt") take over [Plants](/wiki/Plants "Plants"), which appear from [Rifts](/wiki/Rift "Rift").

Deadly Brightshades attack by spawning a single Tunneling Vine, which will weave in and out of the ground towards nearby players or other targets. While the Tunneling Vine is alive, the main body of the plant will retaliate upon taking damage, dealing 100 standard damage and 30 [Planar Damage](/wiki/Planar_Damage "Planar Damage") to anything close to it and cause a large [Knockback](/wiki/Knockback "Knockback"), (except for other Brightshades and Tunneling Vines) that also dismounts any player riding a beefalo.

Tunneling Vines have 200 HP. When tunneling, they will sometimes leave behind a much larger segment of vine that cannot attack, but can be damaged. When the Tunneling Vine is destroyed, its corresponding Brightshade's bud will open and it will not retaliate for 9 seconds. The Brightshade will begin shaking after roughly 5 seconds have passed. After 9 seconds have passed, the Brightshade will immediately retaliate, close (and thus retaliate if attacked) and spawn another Tunneling Vine.

Both Deadly Brightshades and their Tunneling Vines have [Planar Entity Protection](/wiki/Planar_Entity_Protection "Planar Entity Protection"), and thus physical damage are less effective against them. In contrast, the planar damage and other damage sources are fully effective. For example, a [Brightshade Sword](/wiki/Brightshade_Sword "Brightshade Sword") will deal 32.99 physical damage and 30 planar damage to them instead of 38 and 30 respectively.

When killed, a Deadly Brightshade drops 2 [Brightshade Husk](/wiki/Brightshade_Husk "Brightshade Husk") and 2 [Leafy Meat](/wiki/Leafy_Meat "Leafy Meat").

## Tips[]

* Neither the Deadly Brightshade nor the Tunneling Vine will drop [Souls](/wiki/Soul "Soul") for Wortox.
* Because the retaliation effect has a short range, ranged weapons such as [Blow Darts](/wiki/Blow_Dart "Blow Dart"), [Walter](/wiki/Walter "Walter")'s [Trusty Slingshot](/wiki/Trusty_Slingshot "Trusty Slingshot"), or [Howlitzer](/wiki/Howlitzer "Howlitzer") can damage the main plant without fearing of retaliation.
  + The range of long-reach melee weapons, such as [Tail o' Three Cats](/wiki/Tail_o%27_Three_Cats "Tail o' Three Cats") and [Alarming Clock](/wiki/Alarming_Clock "Alarming Clock"), is not far enough to safely stay out of the Brightshade's retaliation range.
* The retaliation effect will damage any mob nearby, potentially turning aggressive mobs against the Deadly Brightshade. Large numbers of mobs with weak individual attacks such as [Bees](/wiki/Bee "Bee") will die quickly, but large mobs with slow high-damage attacks such as [Treeguards](/wiki/Treeguard "Treeguard") can fight effectively against the Brightshade if the player pits them against each other.
* Because the Brightshade deals a large amount of Planar Damage, care should be taken when fighting the Brightshade, especially characters with low HP, such as [Maxwell](/wiki/Maxwell "Maxwell") or an old [Wanda](/wiki/Wanda "Wanda").
* [Brightshade Armor](/wiki/Brightshade_Armor "Brightshade Armor") set obtained after defeating a few Deadly Brightshades can drastically reduce the damage Deadly Brightshades do, while the [Brightshade Sword](/wiki/Brightshade_Sword "Brightshade Sword") allow partially piercing their planar entity protection.
  + [Shadow Reaper](/wiki/Shadow_Reaper "Shadow Reaper") will (While wearing [Void Cowl](/wiki/Void_Cowl "Void Cowl")) fully stack its additional damage while killing the Tunneling Vine, after which it will remain fully stacked against the Brightshade, meaning that players with any damage amp or allies attacking the Brightshade are capable of killing it after killing just one Tunneling Vine. The Reaper will remain stacked against other vines as well, which makes it a perfect weapon for dispatching groups of Brightshades.
* Because planar entity protection increases in effectiveness the stronger a hit is, a full stack (40) of [Gunpowder](/wiki/Gunpowder "Gunpowder") is not enough to instantly kill a Deadly Brightshade.
