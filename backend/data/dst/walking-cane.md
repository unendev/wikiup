The **Walking Cane** is a [Dress Tab](/wiki/Dress_Tab "Dress Tab") [Item](/wiki/Item "Item"), which can also be used as a [Weapon](/wiki/Weapon "Weapon"). It requires 1 [Walrus Tusk](/wiki/Walrus_Tusk "Walrus Tusk"), 2 [Gold Nuggets](/wiki/Gold_Nugget "Gold Nugget"), and 4 [Twigs](/wiki/Twigs "Twigs") to craft, and an [Alchemy Engine](/wiki/Alchemy_Engine "Alchemy Engine") to prototype. It increases the character's movement [speed](/wiki/Player_Speed "Player Speed") by 25% while equipped. The Walking Cane can be used to craft the [Lazy Explorer](/wiki/The_Lazy_Explorer "The Lazy Explorer").

## Gift Icon Downloadable Content[]

In the *[Shipwrecked](/wiki/Don%27t_Starve:_Shipwrecked "Don't Starve: Shipwrecked")* DLC, the Walking Cane cannot be crafted since [MacTusk](/wiki/MacTusk "MacTusk") is not present. However, it can be obtained by using the [Slot Machine](/wiki/Slot_Machine "Slot Machine"), or as possible loot from the [Quacken](/wiki/Quacken "Quacken").

In the *[Hamlet](/wiki/Don%27t_Starve:_Hamlet "Don't Starve: Hamlet")* DLC, the Walking Cane crafting recipe is unavailable, however, a [Blueprint](/wiki/Blueprint "Blueprint") for it can be bought with 500 [Oincs](/wiki/Oinc "Oinc") from [The Tinkerer's Tower](/wiki/The_Tinkerer%27s_Tower "The Tinkerer's Tower"). The Walrus Tusk is available at ['The Sty' Oddities Emporium](/wiki/%27The_Sty%27_Oddities_Emporium "'The Sty' Oddities Emporium").

In both these DLCs, the Walking Cane can also be used while sailing on [Boats](/wiki/Boats "Boats"), providing the same speed boost.

## Prototype Tips[]

* The Walking Cane can also be used as a weapon, although it is amongst the weakest in the game, as it only deals half the damage of a [Spear](/wiki/Spear "Spear") when used as a weapon. However, it has an infinite durability, so it can be swung indefinitely.
* The Walking Cane's speed boost puts the [Character](/wiki/Character "Character") on the same level as many other creatures who could otherwise outrun them, including [Followers](/wiki/Followers "Followers") like [Chester](/wiki/Chester "Chester"). This is amplified by both [natural](/wiki/Trail "Trail") and [Cobblestone](/wiki/Cobblestones "Cobblestones") roads, for a combined increase of movement speed by 55% (62.5% in DST), allowing players to outrun most mobs in the game.
* The Walking Cane is essential in some fights, as it allows the player to often pull out a single additional hit before needing to dodge the attack. For example, the bonus movement speed allows the player to deal more damage to [Treeguards](/wiki/Treeguard "Treeguard") and all of seasonal giants, assuming the player is used to changing the weapon and the Walking Cane during the fight.
  + Thanks to its utility during fights, PC players are recommended to switch their weapon to the Walking Cane using the numbers on their keyboards when kiting (more preferably 1, 2 or 3 since they are close to the WASD keys), and then switch back to the weapon when hitting.
* A Walking Cane's infinite durability and high attack speed makes it a decent weapon against some low HP and easy to stunlock mobs, such as [Rabbits](/wiki/Rabbit "Rabbit"), [Spiders](/wiki/Spiders "Spiders"), [Bees](/wiki/Bee "Bee"), larger mobs that do not fight back such as the [Lureplants](/wiki/Meat_Bulb_(Lureplant) "Meat Bulb (Lureplant)") or some very fragile entities such [Ancient Fuelweaver's](/wiki/Reanimated_Skeleton "Reanimated Skeleton") Woven Shadows.
* One can stack the speed bonus with the [Magiluminescence](/wiki/Magiluminescence "Magiluminescence") to have a total increase of 45% (50% in DST) movement speed while both items are worn, which means that while on a road, the player will have a 75% (95% in DST) speed boost. This can further be increased with [WX-78](/wiki/WX-78 "WX-78")'s overload(DS) and Speed circuit module (DST)
  + In the *Shipwrecked* DLC, speed can further be increased by eating [Coffee](/wiki/Coffee "Coffee") and/or [Tropical Bouillabaisse](/wiki/Tropical_Bouillabaisse "Tropical Bouillabaisse") and by playing as [Wilbur](/wiki/Wilbur "Wilbur") while on land.
  + In the *Hamlet* DLC, speed can further be increased by eating [Iced Tea](/wiki/Iced_Tea "Iced Tea") and/or [Tea](/wiki/Tea "Tea") and by playing as [Wheeler](/wiki/Wheeler "Wheeler") or [Wormwood](/wiki/Wormwood "Wormwood") during blooming state.
  + In *[Don't Starve Together](/wiki/Don%27t_Starve_Together "Don't Starve Together")*, the speed multiplier is product of all multiplier of equipped items and roads and characters.
* The Walking Cane can be found on a [Set Piece](/wiki/Set_Piece "Set Piece").

## Placeholder Trivia[]

* The Walking Cane was added in *[The End Is Nigh](/wiki/Version_History#The_End_is_Nigh "Version History")* update.
* Walani's examine text for the Walking Cane is a reference to the Latin phrase "Veni, vidi, vici", which translates to "I came, I saw, I conquered".
