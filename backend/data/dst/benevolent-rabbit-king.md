The **Benevolent Rabbit King** is a passive [mob](/wiki/Mob "Mob") exclusive to *[Don't Starve Together](/wiki/Don%27t_Starve_Together "Don't Starve Together")*, introduced in the *[From Beyond](/wiki/From_Beyond "From Beyond")* updates. It can be summoned by giving 12 to 16 [Carrots](/wiki/Carrot "Carrot") to a [Rabbit Hole](/wiki/Rabbit_Hole "Rabbit Hole") or by catching a [Fortuitous Rabbit](#Fortuitous_Rabbit).

Standing next to the Benevolent Rabbit King grants access to the [Trading Hutch Filter](/wiki/Trading_Hutch_Filter "Trading Hutch Filter").

Unlike regular Rabbits, the Benevolent Rabbit King remains above ground during [Dusk](/wiki/Dusk "Dusk") and [Night](/wiki/Night "Night"). When leaving the area, the Benevolent Rabbit King will despawn and cannot be summoned for the next 5 minutes.

When the Benevolent Rabbit King is attacked, it will turn it into the [Wrathful Rabbit King](#Wrathful_Rabbit_King). This also happens when it is approached with [Meat](/wiki/Meats "Meats") items in the inventory; wearing a [Coat of Carrots](/wiki/Coat_of_Carrots "Coat of Carrots") will prevent this.

The **Fortuitous Rabbit** is a passive mob exclusive to *Don't Starve Together*, introduced in the *From Beyond* updates. Every time a [Rabbit](/wiki/Rabbit "Rabbit") emerges from a [Rabbit Hole](/wiki/Rabbit_Hole "Rabbit Hole"), there is a 1% chance a [Fortuitous Rabbit](#Fortuitous_Rabbit) will spawn instead. There is also a small chance for a Fortuitous Rabbit to fall during an [Earthquake](/wiki/Earthquake "Earthquake") in the [Caves](/wiki/Caves "Caves") instead of a regular Rabbit.

When caught, the Fortuitous Rabbit turns into a Benevolent Rabbit King. It cannot be caught with a [Trap](/wiki/Trap "Trap"), but can be chased down due to its slower runspeed.

When the Fortuitous Rabbit is attacked, it will turn it into the [Wrathful Rabbit King](#Wrathful_Rabbit_King).

The **Wrathful Rabbit King** is a hostile [mob](/wiki/Mob "Mob") exclusive to *[Don't Starve Together](/wiki/Don%27t_Starve_Together "Don't Starve Together")*, introduced in the *[From Beyond](/wiki/From_Beyond "From Beyond")* updates. It spawns when a Benevolent Rabbit King or Fortuitous Rabbit is attacked, a Benevolent Rabbit King is approached with [Meat](/wiki/Meats "Meats") items in the inventory, or when killing too many [Rabbits](/wiki/Rabbit "Rabbit") or [Bunnymen](/wiki/Bunnyman "Bunnyman"). There is a hidden rabbit naughtiness meter similar to the regular [Naughtiness](/wiki/Naughtiness "Naughtiness") meter; killing a Rabbit adds 1 point to the rabbit naughtiness meter, killing a Bunnyman 3 points. Once the meter reaches the limit (random threshold from 20 to 30), the Wrathful Rabbit King spawns.

Upon death, it drops 1 [Monster Meat](/wiki/Monster_Meat "Monster Meat"), 2 [Beard Hair](/wiki/Beard_Hair "Beard Hair"), and 1 [Rabbit King Cudgel](/wiki/Rabbit_King_Cudgel "Rabbit King Cudgel").

### Behavior[]

Once spawned, the Wrathful Rabbit King will summon 3 [Royal Rabbit Enforcers](/wiki/Royal_Rabbit_Enforcer "Royal Rabbit Enforcer") to protect it. The Wrathful Rabbit King can perform a kick attack that can knockback players. When dodging the kick, the King will land on its head and be stuck with its antlers to the ground for a few seconds, making it vulnerable to damage. When killing the Royal Rabbit Enforcers, the Wrathful Rabbit King will attempt to summon new ones.

### Strategy[]

The Wrathful Rabbit King can be put to sleep with a [Pan Flute](/wiki/Pan_Flute "Pan Flute") or a [Sleep Dart](/wiki/Sleep_Dart "Sleep Dart"). When it sleeps, the Royal Rabbit Enforcers will become passive, stopping chasing and attacking the player.
