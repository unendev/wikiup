The **BFB** is a massive [Bird](/wiki/Category:Birds "Category:Birds") exclusive to the *[Hamlet](/wiki/Don%27t_Starve:_Hamlet "Don't Starve: Hamlet")* DLC. It appears every 10-24 days, heralded by a loud cawing, and then a large shadow flying above the player. It is an extremely large bird creature that has foliage growing on it, with two smaller "inner" heads inside its beak. It is a source of [Dung Piles](/wiki/Dung_Pile "Dung Pile"), dropping a few as it flies around. The Dung Piles do NOT deal damage to the player, despite showing a visible shockwave-Like effect.

## Behavior[]

The BFB starts as a small bird shaped shadow above the player, that will bounce between the edges of the players view distance dropping [Dung Piles](/wiki/Dung_Pile "Dung Pile") periodically. It's shadow will progressively grow larger before stopping at about the point it's wing span takes up nearly all of the players vision radius. When the shadow reaches its maximum size, the BFB will immediately land as long as the player is not on a blacklisted [turf](/wiki/Turf "Turf"). The BFB will wait in this flying above the player while they are indoors or standing on blacklisted [turfs](/wiki/Turf "Turf"). Going [indoors](/wiki/Category:Interiors "Category:Interiors") will not cause the BFB to leave while in the flying state, but the arrival of [night](/wiki/Day-Night_Cycle "Day-Night Cycle") will.

The Blacklisted Turfs:

* Flat Stone Turf [Flat Stone Turf](/wiki/Flat_Stone_Turf "Flat Stone Turf")
* Stone Road Turf [Stone Road Turf](/wiki/Stone_Road_Turf "Stone Road Turf")
* Lawn Turf [Lawn Turf](/wiki/Lawn_Turf "Lawn Turf")
* Deep Rainforest [Deep Rainforest Turf](/wiki/Deep_Rainforest "Deep Rainforest")
* [Gas Rainforest Turf](/wiki/Gas_Rainforest "Gas Rainforest")
* Any turf next to [water](/wiki/Lily_Pond "Lily Pond") or the edge of the map (About 2-3 Away)

Upon landing, the head will start chasing the player directly while 2 legs and tail follow behind. Its legs, similar to [Bigfoot](/wiki/Bigfoot "Bigfoot"), will deal 1000 damage to anything they land on, while cutting down any trees, and will destroying any buildings. The head of the BFB will temporarily stop chasing if it sees any Structures (or [Gnat Mounds](/wiki/Gnat_Mound "Gnat Mound")), and will move to destroy any in sight. Once landed the BFB will leave if the player goes [indoors](/wiki/Category:Interiors "Category:Interiors") or on blacklisted [Turf](/wiki/Turf "Turf").

## Tips[]

* The BFB's head moves about as fast as a normal player character, making outrunning it possible with speed boosts like a [Stalking Stick](/wiki/Stalking_Stick "Stalking Stick"), a [Walking Cane](/wiki/Walking_Cane "Walking Cane") or [Coffee](/wiki/Coffee "Coffee"). If the BFB head makes it over the player, they will be swallowed and transported to the [Nest](/wiki/BFB%27s_Nest "BFB's Nest") on the [Pinacle](/wiki/Pinacle "Pinacle"). One of the things in the nest is a [Stone Egg](/wiki/Stone_Egg "Stone Egg") which hatches into a [Ro Bin](/wiki/Ro_Bin "Ro Bin").
* The BFB can also be summoned at will by using the [Bird Whistle](/wiki/Bird_Whistle "Bird Whistle"). Making using it for tree cutting possible much like the [Old Bell](/wiki/Old_Bell "Old Bell").
* The BFB will not spawn during [Night](/wiki/Night "Night"), [Full Moon](/wiki/Full_Moon "Full Moon"), or the [Aporkalypse](/wiki/Aporkalypse "Aporkalypse"), and will fly away immediately after landing if summoned with the Bird Whistle or in the air.

## Trivia[]

* It was added in the Early Access of *Hamlet*. It was not fully implemented in the closed beta, but it could be debugspawned.
* At the end of the *Hamlet* E3 trailer, a large bird-like silhouette that slightly resembles the BFB is shown flying in the distance.
* The name "BFB" may be a reference to the Roald Dahl book *The BFG*, which stands for "big friendly giant". "BFB" may then stand for "big friendly bird".
  + The fact that the BFB takes the character to its nest also may refer to *The BFG*, in which the giant takes a girl to his home in order to keep her from telling other humans about the existence of giants.
  + The name may also be a reference to the BFG, a large weapon in the *Doom* video game franchise. This would mean it stands for "big f\*\*king bird".
  + It is also entirely probable that it stands for "big flying bird", as it is basicly a gigantic bird.
* If you turn off BFB and then in game use bird whistle, he will still spawn.
* The BFB's original name, (as seen in the *Hamlet* early access announcements) was "The Roc". Its prefab name also remained "roc". A [Roc](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Roc_(mythology) "wikipedia:Roc (mythology)") is a giant legendary bird from Middle Eastern mythology and appears the epic *Arabian Nights*, in which the main protagonist is abducted and taken back to it's nest in much the same way the player is.
* According to Wickerbottom, the BFB is in the family Accipitridae, containing birds such as hawks, eagles, and Old World vultures.
* The BFB's legs act quite similar to [Biiigfoot](/wiki/Old_Bell#Bigfoot "Old Bell") - with the same amount of damage.
* Its design may be based off of the shoebill stork. Having a visually similar head, legs, and posture.
* When raining, the BFB's shadow will provide shelter, granting 35% [Wetness](/wiki/Wetness "Wetness") resistance, and preventing held [Torches](/wiki/Torch "Torch") from being affected by [Rain](/wiki/Rain "Rain").
* The BFB will still do the flyover sequence when spawned with the console.
