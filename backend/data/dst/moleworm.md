The **Moleworm** is a passive [Mob](/wiki/Mob "Mob") in the *[Reign of Giants](/wiki/Reign_of_Giants "Reign of Giants")* DLC. They are indigenous to the [Grasslands](/wiki/Grasslands "Grasslands") and [Deciduous Forest](/wiki/Deciduous_Forest "Deciduous Forest") [Biomes](/wiki/Biome "Biome"). Moleworms live in a [Burrow](/wiki/Burrow "Burrow"), underground, but can occasionally spawn from [Tumbleweeds](/wiki/Tumbleweed "Tumbleweed"), Catcoons, and/or [Earthquakes](/wiki/Earthquake "Earthquake").

Caught Moleworms are required to craft [Moggles](/wiki/Moggles "Moggles") or [Rain Hats](/wiki/Rain_Hat "Rain Hat") and can be used in a [Crock Pot](/wiki/Crock_Pot "Crock Pot") to make [Guacamole](/wiki/Guacamole "Guacamole").

## Behavior[]

Moleworms travel underground similar to a [Depths Worm](/wiki/Depths_Worm "Depths Worm"). They will seek out and collect [items](/wiki/Items "Items") that are lying on the ground, including [Minerals](/wiki/Minerals "Minerals"), [Thermal Stones](/wiki/Thermal_Stone "Thermal Stone"), [Charcoal](/wiki/Charcoal "Charcoal"), [Ashes](/wiki/Ashes "Ashes"), [Gunpowder](/wiki/Gunpowder "Gunpowder"), [Ice](/wiki/Ice "Ice"), [Old Bells](/wiki/Old_Bell "Old Bell"), most [Turfs](/wiki/Turfs "Turfs"), [Gears](/wiki/Gears "Gears") and most [Gravedigger Items](/wiki/Gravedigger_Items "Gravedigger Items"). After collecting 2 stacks of items, they will return to their [Burrow](/wiki/Burrow "Burrow") to drop them off. When a Moleworm takes Gunpowder, it triggers an explosion; killing the Moleworm and leaving a small fire where it died. It will drop a [Cooked Morsel](/wiki/Cooked_Morsel "Cooked Morsel"). When a Moleworm takes an [Old Bell](/wiki/Old_Bell "Old Bell"), it will trigger one use and summon [Bigfoot](/wiki/Bigfoot "Bigfoot").

Moleworms cannot be trapped. In addition, they activate [Traps](/wiki/Trap "Trap") that have been laid down.

Moleworms are not able to pass [Walls](/wiki/Wall "Wall"), despite moving underground.

Moleworms trigger [Spider](/wiki/Spider "Spider") webbing, although spawned Spiders will ignore them.

If a Burrow is destroyed without killing the Moleworm, the Moleworm will wander off to eventually make a new burrow. If the Moleworm is killed but the burrow untouched, a new Moleworm will respawn in 2.5 days(1200 seconds). If the Moleworm is killed and the burrow dug, neither will re-spawn.

Moleworms population can be grown by delinking the Moleworm from its original [molehill](/wiki/Burrow "Burrow") and releasing the moleworm to make more molehill. The moleworm can be delinked by keeping in inventory and relogging, storing them in [Bunding Wrap](/wiki/Bundling_Wrap "Bundling Wrap") , or moving them across shard, like from surface to cave.

Moleworms in the inventory will starve in 3 days(1440 seconds), if not fed minerals. Each mineral fed also heals the Moleworm 10 Health.

## Hunting[]

A live Moleworm can be obtained by either attacking it (one time) with a [Hammer](/wiki/Hammer "Hammer") or [Executive Hammer](/wiki/Executive_Hammer "Executive Hammer") after it surfaces, or by digging up its Burrow by a [Shovel](/wiki/Shovel "Shovel") during the day (destroying the Burrow). The Moleworm can be baited to surface by dropping baits such as a [Rock](/wiki/Rocks "Rocks") or charcoal. A dug Burrow yields a mineral, plus any items that a Moleworm has stashed inside it.

Hitting the Moleworm with a Hammer is a regular attack, despite the special stun effect. This considers the Moleworm as an aggressive target for [Followers](/wiki/Followers "Followers"), for the followers to kill Moleworms. Moleworms are hurt with each hammering and can be instantly killed by Hammer if hit by a character with a high damage modifier (e.g. [Wolfgang](/wiki/Wolfgang "Wolfgang") in mighty form). The Moleworm's current health is recorded despite moving between mob, stunned state, and inventory; if the same Moleworm is hit multiple times with a Hammer, despite being transferred into inventory, the Moleworm will be killed.

## Hamlet Hamlet[]

In the *[Hamlet](/wiki/Hamlet "Hamlet")* DLC Moleworms can't be found on the surface, but can fall from the ceiling during [Earthquakes](/wiki/Earthquake "Earthquake") when mining an [Unimportant Pillar](/wiki/Unimportant_Pillar "Unimportant Pillar") in the [Ancient Pig Ruins](/wiki/Ancient_Pig_Ruins "Ancient Pig Ruins") or a [Cave Cleft](/wiki/Cave_Cleft "Cave Cleft"). They can also be obtained by using a [Deconstruction Staff](/wiki/Deconstruction_Staff "Deconstruction Staff") on [Moggles](/wiki/Moggles "Moggles"), which can be bought at [The Sow's Ear Hat Shop](/wiki/The_Sow%27s_Ear_Hat_Shop "The Sow's Ear Hat Shop"), though Moggles can't be crafted in a Hamlet world.

## Tips[]

* Moleworms can be farmed to provide [morsels](/wiki/Morsel "Morsel"), [naughtiness](/wiki/Krampus "Krampus") and [souls](/wiki/Soul "Soul").
* [Molehills](/wiki/Burrow "Burrow") can be a renewable source of rocks and other minerals, especially if most [Boulders](/wiki/Boulder "Boulder") have already been mined.
* Healing the Moleworm, to reduce Moleworms dying from hammer, can speed up the construction of Moleworm/Molehill farm.
* Moleworms can be used to consolidate minerals or turf, like the [Gold Nuggets](/wiki/Gold_Nugget "Gold Nugget") from [Pig King](/wiki/Pig_King "Pig King"), Rocks from [exploded](/wiki/Weather_Pain "Weather Pain") [Stone Fruit](/wiki/Stone_Fruit "Stone Fruit") or minerals from [petrified forest](/wiki/Petrified_Tree "Petrified Tree").

## Trivia[]

* Moleworms lack hind legs. Instead, they have a segmented fleshy tail like a worm, explaining their name. This can be more easily seen when the Moleworms are in the inventory.
* The ability to knock a Moleworm out with a Hammer is a reference to the popular game, [Whac-A-Mole](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Whac-A-Mole "wikipedia:Whac-A-Mole").
* The Moleworm's appearance is based on the [Star-Nosed Mole](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/star-nosed_mole "wikipedia:star-nosed mole").
* Creatures like the Moleworm began to starve in the inventory after *[A Feet of Strength](/wiki/Reign_of_Giants#March_12th_-_.22A_Feet_of_Strength.22_Forums_Beta_update: "Reign of Giants")* update.

## Bugs[]

* Relogging next to an empty burrow will cause a Moleworm to immediately spawn. This occurs even when the previous Moleworm has just been killed or transferred to the player's inventory.
* When playing as [Wortox](/wiki/Wortox "Wortox"), a burrowing Moleworm triggering a trap will produce a soul despite nothing dying or any drops coming out of the Moleworm.
