The **Drying Rack** is a craftable [Food Tab](/wiki/Food_Tab "Food Tab") [Structure](/wiki/Structure "Structure"). It requires 3 [Twigs](/wiki/Twigs "Twigs"), 2 [Charcoal](/wiki/Charcoal "Charcoal"), and 3 [Rope](/wiki/Rope "Rope") to craft and a [Science Machine](/wiki/Science_Machine "Science Machine") to prototype.

The Drying Rack can dry most [Meats](/wiki/Meats "Meats") and most [Fish](/wiki/Fishes "Fishes") into [Jerky](/wiki/Jerky "Jerky"), [Small Jerky](/wiki/Small_Jerky "Small Jerky"), or [Monster Jerky](/wiki/Monster_Jerky "Monster Jerky"). Dried food items take significantly longer to spoil and often provide better [Health](/wiki/Health "Health") and [Sanity](/wiki/Sanity "Sanity") compared to raw food used in the rack or their cooked versions. In *Don't Starve*, [Meat](/wiki/Meat "Meat"), [Monster Meat](/wiki/Monster_Meat "Monster Meat"), [Morsel](/wiki/Morsel "Morsel"), [Frog Legs](/wiki/Frog_Legs "Frog Legs"), [Fish](/wiki/Fish "Fish"), [Eel](/wiki/Eel "Eel"), [Drumstick](/wiki/Drumstick "Drumstick") and [Batilisk Wing](/wiki/Batilisk_Wing "Batilisk Wing") can all be dried on the Drying Rack if they are uncooked.

Unlike the results of [Cooking](/wiki/Cooking "Cooking"), the dried food created will always be completely fresh. This means a spoiled Morsel can be dried to get a completely fresh piece of Small Jerky. Dried food left on a Drying Rack does not spoil until collected.

## Downloadable Content[]

In all [DLCs](/wiki/DLC "DLC") (including DST), dried food left on the Drying Rack will spoil over time. [Rain](/wiki/Rain "Rain") will prevent any items on the Drying Rack from drying, making it less effective in [Spring](/wiki/Spring "Spring") in *[Reign of Giants](/wiki/Don%27t_Starve:_Reign_of_Giants "Don't Starve: Reign of Giants")* (and the derived DST), [Hurricane Season](/wiki/Hurricane_Season "Hurricane Season") and [Monsoon Season](/wiki/Monsoon_Season "Monsoon Season") in *[Shipwrecked](/wiki/Don%27t_Starve:_Shipwrecked "Don't Starve: Shipwrecked")*, and [Humid Season](/wiki/Seasons/Humid "Seasons/Humid") in *[Hamlet](/wiki/Don%27t_Starve:_Hamlet "Don't Starve: Hamlet")*.

In *Shipwrecked*, [Fish Morsels](/wiki/Fish_Morsel "Fish Morsel"), [Dead Dogfish](/wiki/Dogfish "Dogfish"), [Dead Swordfish](/wiki/Swordfish "Swordfish"), [Tropical Fish](/wiki/Tropical_Fish "Tropical Fish") and [Raw Fish](/wiki/Raw_Fish "Raw Fish") can be dried on the Drying Rack to acquire Jerky. Additionally, [Seaweed](/wiki/Seaweed "Seaweed") can be dried into [Dried Seaweed](/wiki/Dried_Seaweed "Dried Seaweed"), while [Dead Jellyfish](/wiki/Dead_Jellyfish "Dead Jellyfish") and [Dead Rainbow Jellyfish](/wiki/Dead_Rainbow_Jellyfish "Dead Rainbow Jellyfish") can be dried into [Dried Jellyfish](/wiki/Dried_Jellyfish "Dried Jellyfish").

In *Hamlet*, the [Flytrap Stalk](/wiki/Flytrap_Stalk "Flytrap Stalk") can be dried to create a [Stalking Stick](/wiki/Stalking_Stick "Stalking Stick"). [Poison Dartfrog Legs](/wiki/Poison_Dartfrog_Legs "Poison Dartfrog Legs") can be dried into Small Jerky.

## Don't Starve Together Don't Starve Together[]

In *[Don't Starve Together](/wiki/Don%27t_Starve_Together "Don't Starve Together")*, [Kelp Fronds](/wiki/Kelp_Fronds "Kelp Fronds") can be dried on the Drying Rack to obtain [Dried Kelp Fronds](/wiki/Dried_Kelp_Fronds "Dried Kelp Fronds").

The Hermit **Drying Rack** is a naturally spawning object exclusive to *[Don't Starve Together](/wiki/Don%27t_Starve_Together "Don't Starve Together")*, introduced in *[Return of Them](/wiki/Return_of_Them "Return of Them")*. Six of them can be found on the [Hermit Island](/wiki/Hermit_Island "Hermit Island").

The Hermit Drying Rack works just like a drying rack, except that it cannot be destroyed or burned.

## Tips[]

* Drying Racks are very useful for storing Meats. Jerky and Small Jerky take 20 days to completely spoil, while Meat and Morsel will spoil in 6 days, and cooked Meat/Morsel will spoil in 10 days. Storing any kind of jerky in the [Ice Box](/wiki/Ice_Box "Ice Box") will give 40 days before they spoil, allowing the player to focus on concerns other than gathering fresh food.
* It is a good idea to build some racks early on (i.e. before the first Winter). The player may then store meat on the Drying Racks ready for the Winter, which will easily last throughout Winter once picked. Additionally, the [Jerky](/wiki/Jerky "Jerky") versions provide improved stats over regular and cooked Meats.
* Drying a Fish, Frog Legs, or Drumstick will turn them into Small Jerky, which prevents it from being used as a specific ingredient for certain [Crock Pot](/wiki/Crock_Pot "Crock Pot") meals, such as [Fish Sticks](/wiki/Fish_Sticks "Fish Sticks") (which need fish) or [Froggle Bunwich](/wiki/Froggle_Bunwich "Froggle Bunwich") (which need frog legs).
* All the meat listed above can still be dried during winter. This is a very useful method for restoring health and sanity.
* Due to the fact that the food items prepared on a Drying Rack are still technically uncooked, [Wagstaff](/wiki/Wagstaff "Wagstaff") still takes the -3 health penalties eating dried food items, dampening their healing effects.
* One can dry a pair of Poison Dartfrog Legs on a Drying Rack to remove their poisoning effects and gain the benefits from a Small Jerky, while it can also used as a cooking ingredient in a [Crock Pot](/wiki/Crock_Pot "Crock Pot").
* In the *Hamlet* DLC, [indoor](/wiki/Category:Interiors "Category:Interiors") Drying Racks are not affected by Rain.

## Trivia[]

* Even though the [Koalefant Trunk](/wiki/Koalefant_Trunk "Koalefant Trunk") counts as meat, it cannot be hung on the Drying Rack.
* The Hermit Drying Rack was introduced to *Don't Starve Together* in the *[She Sells Sea Shells](/wiki/Return_of_Them#She_Sells_Sea_Shells "Return of Them")* update.

## Bugs[]

* In *Reign of Giants*, Jerky left on the Drying Racks in the [Caves](/wiki/Caves "Caves") may not continue to spoil while the player is in the surface world.
