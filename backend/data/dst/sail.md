*Exclusive to:* Shipwrecked

*This article is about the Sails in [Shipwrecked](/wiki/Shipwrecked "Shipwrecked") and [Hamlet](/wiki/Hamlet "Hamlet"). For the sails seen in [Don't Starve Together](/wiki/Don%27t_Starve_Together "Don't Starve Together"), see [Mast](/wiki/Mast "Mast").*

**Sails** are craftable [Nautical](/wiki/Nautical_Tab "Nautical Tab") [items](/wiki/Items "Items") introduced in the *[Shipwrecked](/wiki/Don%27t_Starve:_Shipwrecked "Don't Starve: Shipwrecked")* DLC. Their purpose is to give a speed and acceleration bonus to [Boats](/wiki/Boats "Boats") and keep players' hands free while sailing, allowing them to hold items like [Tools](/wiki/Tools "Tools"), [Weapons](/wiki/Weapon "Weapon"), and hand-held [Light](/wiki/Light_Tab "Light Tab") sources. Sails lose durability over time when moving on water, but using a [Sewing Kit](/wiki/Sewing_Kit "Sewing Kit") on any variety of Sail repairs 100% of its durability, except the Iron Wind, which requires [Gears](/wiki/Gears "Gears") to repair. The [Trawl Net](/wiki/Trawl_Net "Trawl Net") takes the place of a sail, but functions differently. Boats without equipment slots ([Log Raft](/wiki/Log_Raft "Log Raft"), [Raft](/wiki/Raft "Raft"), [Sea Legs](/wiki/Sea_Legs "Sea Legs"), and [Surfboard](/wiki/Surfboard "Surfboard")) cannot have sails.

The **Thatch Sail** gives a small speed bonus to the player while on a [Boat](/wiki/Boats "Boats"). It is crafted from 2 [Bamboo](/wiki/Bamboo "Bamboo"), 2 [Vines](/wiki/Vine "Vine"), and 4 [Palm Leaves](/wiki/Palm_Leaf "Palm Leaf") and is prototyped at a [Science Machine](/wiki/Science_Machine "Science Machine"). It is also a reward from the [Yaarctopus](/wiki/Yaarctopus "Yaarctopus") in exchange for [California Rolls](/wiki/California_Roll "California Roll"). Compared to other sails, it has the lowest speed boost at 20% and the lowest durability. However, it can be crafted at a Science Machine (unlike the Snakeskin Sail and Cloth Sail, which require an [Alchemy Engine](/wiki/Alchemy_Engine "Alchemy Engine")), and is relatively inexpensive compared to the others. Once an Alchemy Engine is available it is usually desirable to move on to the other choices available.

The **Cloth Sail** gives a moderate speed bonus to the player while on a Boat. It is crafted from 2 [Cloth](/wiki/Cloth "Cloth"), 2 Bamboo, and 2 [Rope](/wiki/Rope "Rope") and is prototyped at an Alchemy Engine. It is also a reward from the [Yaarctopus](/wiki/Yaarctopus "Yaarctopus") in exchange for [Seafood Gumbo](/wiki/Seafood_Gumbo "Seafood Gumbo"). Compared to the other sails, it offers a relatively high speed boost of 30% and has middling durability. It is made out of ingredients that are easy to acquire and its overall cost is relatively cheap. However, it doesn't have the high speed of the Feather Lite Sail or the high durability of the Snakeskin Sail. Simply put, it is a jack of all trades but master of none.

It is crafted from 4 [Logs](/wiki/Log "Log"), 2 Rope, and 2 [Snakeskin](/wiki/Snakeskin "Snakeskin") and is prototyped at an Alchemy Engine. It has the highest durability out of all five of the sails, tied with the Iron Wind. However, its speed is middling between the Thatch Sail and the Cloth Sail. It is best suited for long voyages at sea where the player will have limited access to supplies.

The Snakeskin Sail is also included in the *[Hamlet](/wiki/Don%27t_Starve:_Hamlet "Don't Starve: Hamlet")* DLC, however, it has a very different appearance as Snakeskin is obtained from [Vipers](/wiki/Viper "Viper").

It is crafted from 2 Bamboo, 2 Rope, and 4 [Doydoy Feathers](/wiki/Doydoy_Feather "Doydoy Feather") and is prototyped at a Science Machine (unlike the Cloth Sail and the Snakeskin Sail). Compared to other sails, it has the second highest speed boost at 40% but shares the lowest durability with the Thatch Sail - lasting only 2 days. As such, it is recommended to breed the two [Doydoys](/wiki/Doydoy "Doydoy") found in the world before killing any to make this item. The only way to make them efficient is to breed the Doydoys and collect the feathers, as otherwise, only one can be made, requiring constant repairs with a [Sewing Kit](/wiki/Sewing_Kit "Sewing Kit").

It can only be repaired with [Gears](/wiki/Gear "Gear"). Each set of Gears restores 25% of its durability.
