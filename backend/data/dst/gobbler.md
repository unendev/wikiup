**Gobblers** are passive [Mobs](/wiki/Mobs "Mobs") that feed on [Berries](/wiki/Berries "Berries") and all [Fruits](/wiki/Fruits "Fruits") and [Vegetables](/wiki/Vegetables "Vegetables"), including [Mandrakes](/wiki/Mandrake "Mandrake"). Gobblers have a 10% chance to appear by leaping out of recently picked [Berry Bushes](/wiki/Berry_Bush "Berry Bush"), including [underground](/wiki/Caves "Caves") bushes. Gobblers drop two [Drumsticks](/wiki/Drumsticks "Drumsticks") when killed. Despite being passive, killing Gobblers has no effect on [Naughtiness](/wiki/Naughtiness "Naughtiness").

## Brain Behavior[]

Gobblers will run toward and attempt to eat from any nearby unharvested Berry Bushes, as well as any dropped Fruits or Vegetables. They can quickly devastate berry farms if the bushes are not protected. Gobblers will eat picked [Mushroom](/wiki/Mushroom "Mushroom") caps, but will not eat Mushrooms that are still in the ground. After eating a Mandrake, the Gobbler will fall [asleep](/wiki/Asleep "Asleep"). The Gobbler has no eating limit and will consume as much [Food](/wiki/Food "Food") as possible until killed.

Gobblers will run away when approached by the player or certain mobs. They are much faster than the player, making them nearly impossible to kill with a melee weapon unless cornered or attacked while it's eating. When left alone, they will slowly wander around searching for food. During [dusk and night](/wiki/Day-Night_Cycle "Day-Night Cycle"), Gobblers will panic and run to a nearby [Berry Bush](/wiki/Berry_Bush "Berry Bush") for cover to sleep. If, after a certain amount of time, the Gobbler doesn't find cover, they will go to sleep on the ground, though it will wake up if attacked or if a mob comes too close.

Gobblers will continue to spawn at dusk or night, but they will run straight to a bush to hide in instead of eating anything. Because of this, it is advised to harvest berries during this time. Gobblers will exhibit this behavior if there is no natural light source, so in Caves they will flee to hide in a bush regardless of the time of day, unless they are located under a natural shaft of light.

Don't Starve **Don't Starve**

In *[Don't Starve](/wiki/Don%27t_Starve "Don't Starve")*, Gobblers will eat [Mandrakes](/wiki/Mandrake "Mandrake") even if they are still planted.

If brought from a *[Shipwrecked](/wiki/Shipwrecked "Shipwrecked")* world, Gobblers can also hide in a planted [Coffee Plant](/wiki/Coffee_Plant "Coffee Plant").

Don't Starve Together **Don't Starve Together**

In *[Don't Starve Together](/wiki/Don%27t_Starve_Together "Don't Starve Together")*, Gobblers will also hide in [Juicy Berry Bushes](/wiki/Juicy_Berry_Bush "Juicy Berry Bush") and hit them to eat the [Juicy Berries](/wiki/Juicy_Berries "Juicy Berries").

## Spear Hunting[]

 https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/dont-starve-game/images/1/13/PoisonedPerd.png/revision/latest?cb=20130726092248 https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/dont-starve-game/images/1/13/PoisonedPerd.png/revision/latest?cb=20130726092248 

[Woodie](/wiki/Woodie "Woodie") luring the gobbler with 3 poisonous Red Caps.

 

Gobblers will eat most vegetable and fruit [Food](/wiki/Food "Food") items (like [Carrots](/wiki/Carrot "Carrot"), [Corn](/wiki/Corn "Corn"), [Berries](/wiki/Berries "Berries"), or [Powdercake](/wiki/Powdercake "Powdercake")) left on the ground. When such food is placed near a Gobbler, it will slowly approach the food while completely ignoring the player. As long as the Gobbler doesn't make it to the bait before being attacked, it will not flee like normal. If it reaches the bait, the Gobbler will eat it instantly and begin fleeing again.

Gobblers are not picky and will also eat poisonous [Red Caps](/wiki/Red_Cap "Red Cap") left on the ground. If the Gobbler eats three, it will die, as Red Mushrooms reduce Health by 20 points when eaten.

Gobblers can be killed by the [Boomerang](/wiki/Boomerang "Boomerang") in 2 hits by most characters. [Wendy](/wiki/Wendy "Wendy"), [Wes](/wiki/Wes "Wes"), and wimpy [Wolfgang](/wiki/Wolfgang "Wolfgang") will take 3 hits and Mighty Form Wolfgang will kill a Gobbler with a single boomerang hit. After the first hit, Gobblers will not run away; this makes Gobblers easy targets for a second (and third) strike.

Placing Tooth Traps close to Berry Bushes will allow for the farming of both Berries and Drumsticks. With the right camera orientation (with the player standing in front of the bush), [Tooth Traps](/wiki/Tooth_Trap "Tooth Trap") can be placed directly underneath Berry Bushes, causing any spawned Gobbler to land on top of and trigger the trap, dying instantly. Adding walls around the farm will help to keep Gobblers from escaping. Gobblers can also be chased into Tooth Traps if they escape. This makes Tooth Traps an essential element of any berry farm.

Alternatively, a [Houndius Shootius](/wiki/Houndius_Shootius "Houndius Shootius") turret can be utilized to farm Gobblers next to a patch of Berry Bushes. The turret deals enough damage to kill the Gobbler in one hit, although the player must target the Gobbler to be attacked for the Houndius Shootius to do so too. This is a good method of farming Gobblers for late-game bases without the use of Boomerangs, Tooth Traps, or any other weapon. This method is particularly effective when used with a very large berry bush farm (50+ bushes) as the player can collect 10 or more Drumsticks every few days simply from harvesting the berry bushes.

If none of these options are available, Gobblers can be chased into confined areas such as walled pens where they will be forced into the range of melee weapons or into either wild [Marshes](/wiki/Marsh "Marsh") where they can be finished off by the resident *[Tentacles](/wiki/Tentacle "Tentacle")* or towards a pond where [Frogs](/wiki/Frog "Frog") are wandering around. Due to their aggressive nature, the frogs will immediately attack the Gobblers. Gobblers take five hits to be killed by Frogs and, as with ranged weapons, Gobblers do not respond when attacked by frogs. One should beware of angering any Frogs when collecting loot. If the frogs are too much of a problem, the player could simply wait until dusk, when the frogs retreat to their pond, to pick up the drops.

Gobblers can be hunted at night if they did not despawn at a Berry Bush. It should be noted, however, that they will wake up and run away after a single melee strike. This makes landing the second melee strike difficult if one isn't using a [Tentacle Spike](/wiki/Tentacle_Spike "Tentacle Spike") or similarly powerful weapon.

In the *[Shipwrecked](/wiki/Shipwrecked "Shipwrecked")* DLC, [Elephant Cacti](/wiki/Elephant_Cactus "Elephant Cactus") can be brought over from the [Volcano](/wiki/Volcano "Volcano") and placed near Berry Bushes to kill any Gobblers that come out of them.

If you create a fenced-in area near your berry farm, then put a food item inside the area, Gobblers will constantly go after the food. However, since the food is fenced in, the Gobblers can't reach it. Therefore, they will constantly run into the fence, trying to get the food. This allows the player to kill the Gobblers with ease, as the Gobblers will not acknowledge the player whilst trying to get the food item.

If all else fails, one can corner the Gobbler at the edges of the island where the ocean meets the land or at a gap in the land where there is water. This solution is not always efficient as Gobblers that are killed near edges of the land may drop their Drumsticks into the ocean, losing them permanently. In rare occasions, these drumsticks will drop out of the players reach, floating above the ocean and attracting various carnivorous monsters until it rots. The only real way to fix this bug is to set the drumstick on fire with a large fire or a [Fire Staff](/wiki/Fire_Staff "Fire Staff").

## Placeholder Trivia[]

* The Gobbler is voiced by Matt, the lead sound artist at Klei Entertainment.
* In the "customize world" screen, the Gobbler is called "Perd". According to the programmer, Kevin, it originally stood for 'pig bird'.[[1]](#cite_note-1)
* Gobblers were added in the *[Enter the Gobbler](/wiki/Version_History#November_13.2C_2012_.E2.80.93_Enter_the_Gobbler.5B17.5D "Version History")* update.
* Prior to the *[Six Feet Under](/wiki/Version_History#October_1.2C_2013_-_Six_Feet_Under "Version History")* update, the Gobbler dropped one Drumstick and one [Morsel](/wiki/Morsel "Morsel").
* Despite being passive creatures, they have a damage value of 20 in the game code. It also has an attack speed set to 3.
* Gobblers share the same "hunchback" model as mobs such as [Pigs](/wiki/Pig "Pig"), [Merms](/wiki/Merm "Merm"), etc. This is especially evident in a few of their animations.
* The name "Gobbler" is a pun on the "gobble" sounds turkeys make and the way the animal "gobbles" its food down.
* Gobblers were featured in the 2017 [event](/wiki/Category:Events "Category:Events") *[Year of the Gobbler](/wiki/Year_of_the_Gobbler "Year of the Gobbler")*.
* Maxwell's examination quote for the gobblers reveals that they are addicted to fermented berries, which explains their behavior and may imply that they're drunk.
