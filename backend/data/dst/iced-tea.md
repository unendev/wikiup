**Iced Tea** is a [Food](/wiki/Food "Food") [Item](/wiki/Item "Item") exclusive to the *[Hamlet](/wiki/Don%27t_Starve:_Hamlet "Don't Starve: Hamlet")* DLC. It can be cooked in the [Crock Pot](/wiki/Crock_Pot "Crock Pot") by combining two [Orange Pikos](/wiki/Piko "Piko"), a [Sweetener](/wiki/Sweetener "Sweetener"), and 1 [Ice](/wiki/Ice "Ice"). It takes 10 seconds too cook and 6 [Days](/wiki/Day-Night_Cycle "Day-Night Cycle") to [spoil](/wiki/Spoil "Spoil"). Iced Tea also can be obtained by cooking [Tea](/wiki/Tea "Tea") in the Crock Pot, and waiting a [Day](/wiki/Day "Day") for it to cool down.

When consumed, it restores 3 Health, 12.5 Hunger and 33 Sanity, lowers body temperature by 10 and increases Walking [Speed](/wiki/Speed "Speed") by 1.67 (1/3 as much as [Coffee](/wiki/Coffee "Coffee")) for 80 seconds.

## Tips[]

* If opting for the second solution (to make normal Tea and wait a day), players should avoid putting the Tea in an [Ice Box](/wiki/Ice_Box "Ice Box"), since it will, paradoxically, make the cooling time twice as long.

## Trivia[]

* During the [Pig Fiesta](/wiki/Pig_Fiesta "Pig Fiesta") event, Iced Tea takes on a more festive appearance, looking like green tea.
* The use of [Orange Pikos](/wiki/Orange_Piko "Orange Piko") in making Tea and Iced Tea is a pun on [orange pekoe tea](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tea_leaf_grading#Orange_pekoe "wikipedia:Tea leaf grading").
* Tea and Icedtea overrides the effect of coffee and each other respectively.
