**Tropical Fish** is a type of [Fish](/wiki/Fishes "Fishes") [Food](/wiki/Food "Food") introduced in the *[Shipwrecked](/wiki/Don%27t_Starve:_Shipwrecked "Don't Starve: Shipwrecked")* DLC.

They can be obtained by fishing in a [Shoal](/wiki/Shoal "Shoal") or [Tidal Pool](/wiki/Tidal_Pool "Tidal Pool") with a [Fishing Rod](/wiki/Fishing_Rod "Fishing Rod") or rowing over it with [Boats](/wiki/Boats "Boats") with an attached [Trawl Net](/wiki/Trawl_Net "Trawl Net"). [Fish Farms](/wiki/Fish_Farm "Fish Farm") that have been stocked with [Roe](/wiki/Roe "Roe") have a chance to continually produce Tropical Fish.

[Merms](/wiki/Merm "Merm") and [Fishermerms](/wiki/Fishermerm "Fishermerm") will drop Tropical Fish when killed. [Hammering](/wiki/Hammer "Hammer") down [Merm Huts](/wiki/Merm_Hut "Merm Hut") and [Fishermerm's Huts](/wiki/Fishermerm%27s_Hut "Fishermerm's Hut") will also yield Tropical Fish, however these structures are not renewable and destroying them will also remove the source of Merm and Fishermerms respectively. Fishermerms will fish in Tidal Pools on their own for Tropical Fish, but they will not eat what they catch, which then can then be collected by the player. Except for Fish Farms and Shoals, which are commonly found in [Coral Reef](/wiki/Coral_Reef "Coral Reef") and [Mangrove](/wiki/Mangrove "Mangrove") biomes, all other sources of Tropical Fish can only be found in [Tidal Marsh](/wiki/Tidal_Marsh "Tidal Marsh") biomes.

Tropical Fish can be used in [Crock Pot](/wiki/Crock_Pot "Crock Pot"), and counts as 0.5 units [Meats](/wiki/Meats "Meats") and 1 units of [Fishes](/wiki/Fishes "Fishes"). Cooking Tropical Fish on a [Fire](/wiki/Fire "Fire") will yield a [Cooked Fish Morsel](/wiki/Fish_Morsel "Fish Morsel"). It can also be [dried](/wiki/Drying_Rack "Drying Rack") to get [Small Jerky](/wiki/Small_Jerky "Small Jerky"). It can be traded to [Yaarctopus](/wiki/Yaarctopus "Yaarctopus") for [Dubloons](/wiki/Dubloons "Dubloons").

## Prototype Tips[]

* Tropical Fish has more meat and fish value than Cooked Fish Morsel obtained by cooking it on fire, so it is not recommended to cook Tropical Fishes if they are going to be used in the Crock Pot. Drying them also has a similar effect, but it may be more desirable since Small Jerky can be used in recipes that Tropical Fish cannot be, due to many Fish based recipes taking precedence over Meat based ones.
* Fish Farms that are stocking Tropical Fish are marked with a small sign showing a blue fish, after being harvested at least once.

## Placeholder Trivia[]

* Tropical Fish resemble the real-life angelfish.
