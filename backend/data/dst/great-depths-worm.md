The **Great Depths Worm** is a [Boss](/wiki/Boss "Boss") [Monster](/wiki/Monster "Monster") exclusive to *[Don't Starve Together](/wiki/Don%27t_Starve_Together "Don't Starve Together")*, introduced in the *[From Beyond](/wiki/From_Beyond "From Beyond")* expansion. It can spawn in the [Caves](/wiki/Caves "Caves") instead of the wave of [Depths Worms](/wiki/Depths_Worm "Depths Worm"). The chance for this increases with every wave; after the
Great Depths Worm spawned, this chance will reset.

The Great Depths Worm will not spawn in the first 25 days.

## Behavior[]

The Great Depths Worm acts similar to the [Pugalisk](/wiki/Pugalisk "Pugalisk") from the *[Hamlet](/wiki/Hamlet "Hamlet")* DLC for *[Don't Starve](/wiki/Don%27t_Starve "Don't Starve")*. It will chase [characters](/wiki/Characters "Characters") and [mobs](/wiki/Mobs "Mobs") by jumping in and out of the ground, destroying [structures](/wiki/Structures "Structures") and other objects like [trees](/wiki/Trees "Trees"), and uprooting up [Grass Tufts](/wiki/Grass_Tufts "Grass Tufts") and [Saplings](/wiki/Sapling "Sapling"). When hit by the moving body parts of the worm, the characters will receive 14 damage and be knocked back every 3 hits. When hit by a jump of the worm, the characters will receive 32 damage.

When the worm finds anything or anyone to eat in a range of 2[m](/wiki/M "M"), it will swallow it and stop moving. When players or mobs get eaten, they will be digested, taking 560 damage. While Characters will be thrown out from the tail of the worm with at least 1 [Health](/wiki/Health "Health"), bigger mobs like [Pigs](/wiki/Pig "Pig"), [Merms](/wiki/Merm "Merm") and [Bunnymen](/wiki/Bunnymen "Bunnymen") will be digested completely to 3 [Manure](/wiki/Manure "Manure"). This action will take some seconds, making the worm remain at place. When the worm eats an item, it will stop only for a very short period of time.

If the Great Depths Worm tries to eat irreplaceable items like the [Star-Sky](/wiki/Star-Sky "Star-Sky") or [Pearl's Pearl](/wiki/Pearl%27s_Pearl "Pearl's Pearl"), it will throw them up.

## Strategy[]

As the Great Depths Worm counts as a [wet](/wiki/Wetness "Wetness") creature, [weapons](/wiki/Weapons "Weapons") with electric damage like the [Morning Star](/wiki/Morning_Star "Morning Star") or [Elding Spear](/wiki/Elding_Spear "Elding Spear") will deal 2.5 times more damage to it.

Dropping items on the ground in front of the worm will make the worm remain at place for a few seconds, giving enough time for a few hits on its body.

A dangerous strategy one may use is playing as WX-78, using 3 Electrification Circuits and getting intentionally digested. It will take 3 digestions (34 hits) to kill the the boss. It is possible that it could go wrong and get killed in the process when thrown up from the tail to the body parts when it starts to move.

It cannot be put to sleep, frozen or getting imprisoned by the [Shadow Prison](/wiki/Shadow_Traps#Shadow_Prison "Shadow Traps") or Wormwood's [Lunar Guardian I](/wiki/Wormwood/Don%27t_Starve_Together#Lunar_Guardian_I "Wormwood/Don't Starve Together") Skill.

## Trivia[]

* The Great Depths Worm was added in the *[Depths of Duplicity](/wiki/Depths_of_Duplicity "Depths of Duplicity")* update.
* With the introduction of the Great Depths Worm, [Fossil Fragments](/wiki/Fossil_Fragment "Fossil Fragment") became renewable.
* The worm can theoretically eat certain NPCs, such as the Crabby Hermit, Corvus Goodfeather, and Wagstaff.
* Wendy's and Maxwell's quotes, as well as the Scrapbook entry, hint at it possibly being an extinct species that was brought back to life.
