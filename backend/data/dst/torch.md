A **Torch** is a portable [Light](/wiki/Light_Tab "Light Tab") [Item](/wiki/Item "Item") in *[Don't Starve](/wiki/Don%27t_Starve "Don't Starve")*. It can be crafted with 2 [Cut Grass](/wiki/Cut_Grass "Cut Grass") and 2 [Twigs](/wiki/Twigs "Twigs"), and is available from the start of the game.

Torches [burn](/wiki/Fire#Fuel_Values "Fire") (and as a result loses durability) while equipped, providing a source of light for the player. A Torch will last for 75 seconds (2.5 in-game clock segments). In [Rain](/wiki/Rain "Rain"), a Torch may burn up to 150% faster depending on *precipitation rate*. It also provides a small amount of Warmth (to slow Freezing).

## Gift Icon Downloadable Content[]

In the *[Reign of Giants](/wiki/Reign_of_Giants "Reign of Giants")* DLC, Torches will not be affected by Rain if the player is sheltered. It no longer provides warmth.

In the *[Shipwrecked](/wiki/Don%27t_Starve:_Shipwrecked "Don't Starve: Shipwrecked")* DLC, Torches are affected by [Strong Winds](/wiki/Strong_Winds "Strong Winds"), causing their durability to deplete much faster than normal. In addition, Torches can be used to make [Boat Torches](/wiki/Boat_Torch "Boat Torch").

## Don't Starve Together Don't Starve Together[]

In *[Don't Starve Together](/wiki/Don%27t_Starve_Together "Don't Starve Together")*, Torches provide 20% [Wetness](/wiki/Wetness "Wetness") resistance when equipped.

## Icon Tools Usage[]

To equip and use a Torch, right-click it from the inventory. As long as the player is holding the Torch, it will burn with a consistent intensity and illuminate a small area around the player.

Dragging a Torch over objects such as [Trees](/wiki/Tree "Tree"), [Spider Dens](/wiki/Spider_Den "Spider Den"), or [Beehives](/wiki/Beehive "Beehive") and right clicking, or equipping a Torch and right clicking on such objects, will light them on fire. If the burning object contains creatures like a Beehive does, they will exit the structure when it is set ablaze. Burning objects will ignite other nearby objects (or creatures), possibly spreading to cause massive fires.

## Prototype Tips[]

* If the player does not have enough Torches to keep [Charlie](/wiki/Charlie "Charlie") away for a whole night, they can light a tree or other burnable object on fire and then unequip the Torch until the object has burnt out, extending the lifetime of the Torch's fire.
* Another option is to "flash" the Torch (equip the Torch and unequip it immediately) every several seconds right before Charlie attacks to keep her at bay. However, [Sanity](/wiki/Sanity "Sanity") drains faster in complete darkness and the Torch loses durability faster, so this trick is not free to use and not recommended in place of a real fire.
* If a newly crafted Torch is held in brief intervals, it will stay at 100% durability. If held too long, the torch will drop to 99% at which point the trick cannot be done. (Note that this trick does not work in Don't Starve Together)

## Placeholder Trivia[]

* The game files have contained code for a Torch's capability to give off warmth ever since [Winter](/wiki/Winter "Winter") was implemented, however this code has never actually been used.

## Mosquito Bugs[]

* The sound which plays while holding a Torch (the fire whooshing sound as the player moves around) may continue even after unequipping the Torch. Saving, exiting, and reloading the game seems to fix this.
* If the night is short enough, it is possible to hold a Torch and go to sleep in a [Bed Roll](/wiki/Bed_Roll "Bed Roll"); when the player wakes up, the Torch will not lose durability until it is re-equipped.
* If the player uses a Bed Roll while holding a Torch, the sleeping animation will be normal, but the Torch will disappear and the flame will float over the player's head, absent of the stick.
* Due to a code error, striking a [Mob](/wiki/Mob "Mob") with a Torch won't set it on fire. If *Shipwrecked* or *Hamlet* DLC is enabled, this problem can be fixed. In *Don't Starve Together* this has already been fixed.
