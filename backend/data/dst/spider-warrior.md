**Spider Warriors** are dangerous protectors of [Spider Dens](/wiki/Spider_Den "Spider Den"). They are stronger and slightly larger than [Common Spiders](/wiki/Spider/Normal "Spider/Normal"), able to leap a considerable distance to attack threats. They are only ever seen when a medium- or large-size [Spider Den](/wiki/Spider_Den "Spider Den") is attacked, or when a [Spider Queen](/wiki/Spider_Queen "Spider Queen") detects a threat nearby.

## Brain Behavior[]

When a Spider Den is attacked directly, or when it senses that a nearby Spider was attacked, several Spiders and Spider Warriors come out growling and chase the threat away. (In the *[Reign of Giants](/wiki/Reign_of_Giants "Reign of Giants")* DLC, a "nearby Spider" is one just on the sticky webbing.) After chasing the offending monster, player, or pig too far from their den, Spiders will prefer to return home, though Spider Warriors are willing to chase things farther and to even stand guard outside until the next morning.

Angered Spider Warriors attack by snapping at their targets or even by leaping at them from considerable distances.

Sleeping Spider Warriors can usually sense a threat from farther away than other sleeping Spiders.

### Webber Webber[]

Spider Warriors can be valuable allies to [Webber](/wiki/Webber "Webber"), as they attack both monsters and animals while completely regarding him as one of their own. He can lead angry masses of foes to a Spider Den and the spiders will fight at his back (or he can watch the two sides destroy each other). Either way, he should collect drops quickly before the spiders eat them, and he should be careful not to hit them.

Webber can build his camp with Spider Dens nearby as extra protection from [Hound](/wiki/Hound "Hound") and [Batilisk](/wiki/Batilisk "Batilisk") attacks.

Spider warrior's leaping attack is particularly useful for killing things that tend to run from the player and spiders, having one as a follower will pick off butterflies, having two will allow you to kill rabbits, and having three will let you easily kill gobblers, these can be done without wasting any resources other than a bit of meat, which can be a good way to gather more meat and butterfly drops while on the go as long as you pick up the meat quickly.

## Spear Hunting[]

Spider Warriors are faster than common Spiders, have twice as much health, and cannot be stunned. It usually takes a half-dozen or more hits to kill one, and it takes fast dodges and rapid strikes to kill them without being wounded. Fighting on a [Road](/wiki/Road "Road") or carrying a [Walking Cane](/wiki/Walking_Cane "Walking Cane") is recommended. Spiders usually can't outrun a target in full flight beyond their den's sticky webbing.

Spider Warriors' leaps can be dodged by Players who are quick enough to get out of the way. After a Spider Warrior has attacked, it cannot strike again for a moment, creating an opportune time to strike 7 or 8 times.

As long as the player is within the melee range of the Spider Warrior, it will not use its jump attack and use its normal bite attack.

Spider Warriors are vulnerable to [Traps](/wiki/Trap "Trap"), even getting caught in them as they leap through.

## Usage & Farming[]

Although Spiders are a dangerous foe early on and a constant nuisance later on, a properly tended-to Spider Den can become a useful source of [Spider Glands](/wiki/Spider_Gland "Spider Gland"), [Monster Meat](/wiki/Monster_Meat "Monster Meat"), and [Silk](/wiki/Silk "Silk"). Spider Glands are an important healing ingredient, used in [Healing Salves](/wiki/Healing_Salve "Healing Salve"), [Telltale Hearts](/wiki/Telltale_Heart "Telltale Heart"), and, [Wendy](/wiki/Wendy "Wendy")'s [Revenant Restorative](/wiki/Ghostly_Elixir#Revenant_Restorative "Ghostly Elixir"). [Monster Meat](/wiki/Monster_Meat "Monster Meat"), used sparingly, can be a meaty ingredient in [Crock Pot](/wiki/Crock_Pot "Crock Pot") recipes. Silk is a vitally important resource for surviving the cold [Winter](/wiki/Winter "Winter"), but it's also necessary for [fishing](/wiki/Fishing_Rod "Fishing Rod"), [beekeeping](/wiki/Bug_Net "Bug Net"), [birdkeeping](/wiki/Bird_Trap "Bird Trap"), and (by extension) [controlled agriculture](/wiki/Birdcage "Birdcage"). All of these things make them a prime candidate for farming, which can be done fairly easily.

A word of caution to the unwary: A den mustn't be left unattended for too long, or it might *grow legs and walk off*. A mature, overgrowing Spider Den can turn into a [Spider Queen](/wiki/Spider_Queen "Spider Queen"), who walks away leaving the old den behind, and plant itself in a new location as a fresh den. The queen is very dangerous, and she can overrun an entire base. Mature Spider Dens should be knocked down and replanted to avoid the risk of ever seeing a queen.

[Spider Eggs](/wiki/Spider_Eggs "Spider Eggs") can be recovered from mature Spider Dens when destroyed. Simply planting one or two of these inside enclosed walls will usually be sufficient containment, as Spiders will not often try to break walls. (If they do, they usually won't succeed before night falls.)

With an enclosure established, a method of killing is needed. One way is to set the enclosure around a [Tentacle](/wiki/Tentacle "Tentacle"), something deadly that Spiders will quickly go to attack after they emerge. Spiders can be sent wave after wave against a Tentacle with little to no effect, and they'll disregard drops from slain Spiders. However, the Player should beware of the Tentacle when picking up the drops.

## Shipwrecked Shipwrecked[]

Spider Warriors in the *Shipwrecked* DLC are larger, greener, and venomous, able to [Poison](/wiki/Poison "Poison") things that threaten their dens. Poison can be cured with [Anti Venom](/wiki/Anti_Venom "Anti Venom"), or preemptively prevented by wearing a [Seashell Suit](/wiki/Seashell_Suit "Seashell Suit") or a [Horned Helmet](/wiki/Horned_Helmet "Horned Helmet").

  + Another spider family is the 2 [Cave Spiders](/wiki/Spider/Cave_Spider "Spider/Cave Spider"), one of which [spits webbing](/wiki/Spider/Spitter "Spider/Spitter"). They can be found sharing the same [Spilagmite](/wiki/Spilagmite "Spilagmite").
  + The [Dangling Depth Dwellers](/wiki/Dangling_Depth_Dweller "Dangling Depth Dweller") found in the [Ruins](/wiki/Ruins "Ruins") are never seen with the other spider varieties.
  + The [Shattered Spiders](/wiki/Spider/Shattered_Spider "Spider/Shattered Spider"), found on the [Lunar Island](/wiki/Lunar_Island "Lunar Island"), are never encountered sharing their [Holes](/wiki/Shattered_Spider_Hole "Shattered Spider Hole") with other spider varieties.
* All varieties of spiders in-game have six legs instead of eight, unlike real spiders.
* Spider Warriors are visually based on jumping spiders, but are brightly colored like many venomous spider. Jumping spiders are not venomous.
* [Pigs](/wiki/Pigs "Pigs") used to eat dropped meats as fast as the Spiders, but now they have a delay doing so.
* It's safe to walk on the very edge of a Spider Den's sticky web. This usually works best if the webs come up to a coastline and there is no way around the web from the other side. This knowledge can be very handy to avoid fights.
