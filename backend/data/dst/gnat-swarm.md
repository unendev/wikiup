The **Gnat Swarm** is a [Mob](/wiki/Mob "Mob") exclusive to the *[Hamlet](/wiki/Don%27t_Starve:_Hamlet "Don't Starve: Hamlet")* DLC, which spawns from [Gnat Mounds](/wiki/Gnat_Mound "Gnat Mound") in the [Painted](/wiki/Painted "Painted") [Biome](/wiki/Biome "Biome").

## Brain Behavior[]

It takes the form of a swarm of bugs that will chase and attach itself to the player character's head. While attached, it will decrease the player's 12 5 [Sanity](/wiki/Sanity "Sanity") per min and deal 1 Health damage every 10 seconds, starting 30 seconds after being attached. It will follow the player until it finds a non-[fire](/wiki/Fire "Fire") light source to attach to, either at [Dusk](/wiki/Dusk "Dusk") or Night (such as [Thunderbirds](/wiki/Thunderbird "Thunderbird"), [Town Houses](/wiki/Town_House "Town House"), or [Glowflies](/wiki/Glowfly "Glowfly")).

The swarm isn't a status effect, but is an actual entity that is flying around the player. When force attacking, the swarm can only be hit if there are no other targets around. Attacking them directly will neither deal any damage, nor decrease durability of the weapon, possibly because they are not a single entity but a swarm and therefore can dodge attacks quickly.

However, they can be killed by other means:

* Spraying a cloud of [Bug B'Gone](/wiki/Bug_B%27Gone "Bug B'Gone")
* Freezing them with an [Ice Staff](/wiki/Ice_Staff "Ice Staff") (this action does not decrease durability of the Staff but still drains sanity as usual) or [Chilled Amulet](/wiki/Chilled_Amulet "Chilled Amulet"), and then attacking them normally
* Leading them into the [Gas Rainforest](/wiki/Gas_Rainforest "Gas Rainforest") [biome](/wiki/Biome "Biome")

If a Gnat Mound is destroyed, the swarm which comes from it will spawn a new mound after some time, similar to [Moleworms](/wiki/Moleworm "Moleworm").

The swarm still attaches to the player even when your character dies and resurrects with the power of a [Meat Effigy](/wiki/Meat_Effigy "Meat Effigy"), [Touch Stone](/wiki/Touch_Stone "Touch Stone") or a [Life Giving Amulet](/wiki/Life_Giving_Amulet "Life Giving Amulet"). It's possible to carry a Gnat Swarm to connected worlds by using the [Seaworthy](/wiki/Seaworthy "Seaworthy") or [Skyworthy](/wiki/Skyworthy "Skyworthy").

## Prototype Tips[]

* Overcharged [WX-78](/wiki/WX-78 "WX-78") players should be cautious, as they shine brighter than the other light sources, and therefore the Gnat Swarm will never leave them on its own.
* When playing as [Wendy](/wiki/Wendy "Wendy"), [Abigail](/wiki/Abigail "Abigail") will instantly attack Gnat Swarms and won't stop doing so, unless the player goes into a [cave](/wiki/Cave_Cleft "Cave Cleft") in which Abigail is going to follow the player. After that, she follows the player as normal. Abigail is not able to kill the Gnat Swarm, however. At night, the swarm may attach itself to Abigail as she is a bright, non-fire light source.
* Strangely, using a [Telelocator Staff](/wiki/Telelocator_Staff "Telelocator Staff") and a stocked [Telelocator Focus](/wiki/Telelocator_Focus "Telelocator Focus") on a attaching swarm result in completely destroying it (whether a mob or a player being attached), while using on a wandering swarm result in teleport it to the Telelocator Focus. Making this an expensive way to kill Gnat Swarm.
* [Iron Ore](/wiki/Iron_Ore "Iron Ore") can be renewable outside of a *Hamlet* world by carrying a Gnat Swarm then using an [Ice Staff](/wiki/Ice_Staff "Ice Staff") to freeze it when a desired location is reached.
* [Maxwell](/wiki/Maxwell/Character "Maxwell/Character") does not have the sanity loss from Gnat Swarm as a result of his dapperness.
* Gnat swarms also detach as soon as a new day starts, allowing a brief period to break the lock Gnat swarms have on the player.
* Attacking Gnat swarms is a very good way to charge [Obsidian Spear](/wiki/Obsidian_Spear "Obsidian Spear"), [Obsidian Axe](/wiki/Obsidian_Axe "Obsidian Axe") and [Obsidian Machete](/wiki/Obsidian_Machete "Obsidian Machete"), doing so also does not reduce any durability.

## Placeholder Trivia[]

* Wickerbottom refers to the gnats as "Culicidae Drosophila". Neither of these taxonomical terms refer to gnats, "Culicidae" is the scientific name for the mosquito family, and "Drosophila" is the genus that includes fruit and vinegar flies. Both of these two insect groups are colloquially grouped with gnats, however.
