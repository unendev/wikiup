The **Life Giving Amulet** is a craftable [item](/wiki/Items "Items") found in the [Magic](/wiki/Magic "Magic") tab. It requires 3 [Gold Nuggets](/wiki/Gold_Nugget "Gold Nugget"), 2 [Nightmare Fuel](/wiki/Nightmare_Fuel "Nightmare Fuel"), and 1 [Red Gem](/wiki/Red_Gem "Red Gem") to craft and a [Prestihatitator](/wiki/Prestihatitator "Prestihatitator") to prototype. It can also be found when a [Grave](/wiki/Grave "Grave") is [dug](/wiki/Shovel "Shovel") up, it can randomly drop from a [Slot Machine](/wiki/Slot_Machine "Slot Machine") or be dropped by a [Ancient Pseudoscience Station](/wiki/Ancient_Pseudoscience_Station "Ancient Pseudoscience Station") when it is damaged with a [Hammer](/wiki/Hammer "Hammer"), [Gunpowder](/wiki/Gunpowder "Gunpowder") etc.

If the player [dies](/wiki/Death "Death") while having a Life Giving Amulet equipped in the torso slot, they will be [resurrected](/wiki/Death#Resurrection "Death") at the beginning of the next day, with an immediate time skip through the [night](/wiki/Night "Night"). After resurrection, nearby mobs are put to [sleep](/wiki/Sleep "Sleep") and the amulet is destroyed.

Resurrection Effects:

* Health is set to 50
* Hunger is set to 2/3 of maximum
* Sanity is set to 1/2 of maximum
* Empty inventory (items can be found at place of death)

When hurt, the Life Giving Amulet will recover 5 Health at the cost of 5 Hunger every 30 seconds when it is worn until fully healed. This reduces the durability of the item by 5% every time the trade-off happens, so it is possible for the amulet to break without reviving the player. Equipped amulets also restore +2 Sanity per minute with no effect on durability.

## https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/dont-starve-game/images/d/d8/Gift Icon.png/revision/latest?cb=20151222151842 Downloadable Content[]

In the *[Don't Starve: Shipwrecked](/wiki/Don%27t_Starve:_Shipwrecked "Don't Starve: Shipwrecked")* DLC, Life Giving Amulets also function as a [Life Jacket](/wiki/Life_Jacket "Life Jacket") and save the player from drowning in the [Ocean](/wiki/Ocean "Ocean") when their [Boat](/wiki/Boats "Boats") sinks.

In *[Don't Starve: Hamlet](/wiki/Don%27t_Starve:_Hamlet "Don't Starve: Hamlet")*, Life Giving Amulets can be bought for 50 [Oincs](/wiki/Oinc "Oinc") at [The Flying Pig Arcane Shop](/wiki/The_Flying_Pig_Arcane_Shop "The Flying Pig Arcane Shop") in the [Pig City](/wiki/Pig_City "Pig City").

## Don't Starve Together icon Don't Starve Together[]

In *[Don't Starve Together](/wiki/Don%27t_Starve_Together "Don't Starve Together")*, the Life Giving Amulet doesn't resurrect its wearer automatically anymore. [Ghost Characters](/wiki/Ghost_Characters "Ghost Characters") instead have to haunt a dropped amulet to use it. Unlike [Telltale Hearts](/wiki/Telltale_Heart "Telltale Heart"), using the amulet does not result in max health penalty.

Unlike in the singleplayer version of the game, a dropped amulet will sink to the bottom of the [Ocean](/wiki/Ocean "Ocean") to prevent resurrecting over the water.

## Prototype Tips[]

* All [bosses](/wiki/Category:Boss_Monsters "Category:Boss Monsters") and some enemies, such as the [Ancient Guardian](/wiki/Ancient_Guardian "Ancient Guardian") and [Clockwork Bishops](/wiki/Clockwork_Bishop "Clockwork Bishop"), are immune to the sleep effect, so the player may get attacked and die again. It is better to flee from these fights when the player is not in a favorable position.
* Should the player die, resurrection will occur regardless of how much [durability](/wiki/Durability "Durability") is left, as long as it's above zero. So there is no reason to not use the Amulet to heal wounds until it reaches 5% durability.
* This item is especially useful when playing as [Maxwell](/wiki/Maxwell "Maxwell"). Because of his low maximum Health, [Meat Effigies](/wiki/Meat_Effigy "Meat Effigy") are not recommended, leaving only the Amulet as a way of resurrecting the player when [Touch Stones](/wiki/Touch_Stone "Touch Stone") are not available. The amulet also is useful for healing after using the [Codex Umbra](/wiki/Codex_Umbra "Codex Umbra").
* In *Don't Starve Together* it is not a good idea to store Life Giving Amulets in a [Backpack](/wiki/Backpack "Backpack") because they cannot be used to resurrect unless they're laying on the ground.

## Placeholder Trivia[]

* The Life Giving Amulet was added in *[The Birds and the Bees & Dogs That Want to Kill You](/wiki/Don%27t_Starve/Version_History#November_27,_2012_–_The_Birds_and_the_Bees_&_Dogs_That_Want_to_Kill_You "Don't Starve/Version History")* update.
* The Life Giving Amulet used to be called just "Amulet" before other amulets, such as the [Nightmare Amulet](/wiki/Nightmare_Amulet "Nightmare Amulet"), were added. It lost its old texture when it became the Life Giving Amulet, which was similar to the one used for the now defunct [Gemology](/wiki/Gemology "Gemology") crafting tab.
  + Before the *[Strange New Powers](/wiki/Version_History#July_2.2C_2013_-_Strange_New_Powers "Version History")* update, these amulets could be stacked.  As a result of this change, any stacks of amulets previously stored in chests were turned into just one amulet.
  + Before the *Strange New Powers* update, they required twice the amount of materials to craft.
* In *Don't Starve Together*, [Klaus](/wiki/Klaus "Klaus") uses the older version of the Life Giving Amulet to revive himself.
* The Life Giving Amulet shares the same in-game model with the [Construction Amulet](/wiki/Construction_Amulet "Construction Amulet"), but its gem is red instead of green.
* Ironically, though this amulet resurrects the dead, it can still be found in graves.
* This item could be a reference to the Amulet of Life Saving from *NetHack*.
