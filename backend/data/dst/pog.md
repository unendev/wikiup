**Pogs** are neutral [Mobs](/wiki/Mobs "Mobs") exclusive to the *[Hamlet](/wiki/Don%27t_Starve:_Hamlet "Don't Starve: Hamlet")* DLC. These curious and hungry critters tend to spawn in small herds in the [Wild Plains](/wiki/Wild_Plains "Wild Plains") [Biomes](/wiki/Biomes "Biomes"). A Pog drops a single [Morsel](/wiki/Morsel "Morsel") when killed.

They are considered innocent creatures and killing one adds 2 points to the player's Naughtiness level, which can cause [Krampus](/wiki/Krampus "Krampus") to appear.

## Behavior[]

They will eat any [Food](/wiki/Food "Food") [Item](/wiki/Item "Item") left on the ground (including buried [Mandrakes](/wiki/Mandrake "Mandrake"), but not poisonous food such as [Tubers](/wiki/Tuber "Tuber") and [Poison Dartfrog Legs](/wiki/Poison_Dartfrog_Legs "Poison Dartfrog Legs")).

They will also attack small creatures in the world like [Butterflies](/wiki/Butterfly "Butterfly"), [Glowflies](/wiki/Glowfly "Glowfly"), [Pikos](/wiki/Piko "Piko") or [Peagawks](/wiki/Peagawk "Peagawk"), and eat their edible loot, leaving anything non-edible behind for the player to collect.

The player may feed them any food item to make them a companion for a short amount of time. This can be helpful as they will fight aggressive mobs, distracting them and even defeating smaller mobs such as [Rabid Beetles](/wiki/Rabid_Beetle "Rabid Beetle"), [Weevoles](/wiki/Weevole "Weevole"), and [Poison Dartfrogs](/wiki/Poison_Dartfrog "Poison Dartfrog"). A befriended Pog will follow the player into buildings and beg at food displays in [Pig Shops](/wiki/Pig_Shops "Pig Shops").

If brought back to a base, Pogs, like [Splumonkeys](/wiki/Splumonkey "Splumonkey"), will open [Chests](/wiki/Chest "Chest"), [Ice Boxes](/wiki/Ice_Box "Ice Box"), [Scaled Chests](/wiki/Scaled_Chest "Scaled Chest") and [Root Trunks](/wiki/Root_Trunk "Root Trunk"), throwing aside items in search of food. They will eat any kind of food they find. They cannot open [Cork Barrels](/wiki/Cork_Barrel "Cork Barrel"), however, and if the player cooks items in a [Crock Pot](/wiki/Crock_Pot "Crock Pot"), a Pog will be distracted from ransacking the base for a period of time, but the best way to stop this behavior is to lead them away from the base or kill them.

During the [Aporkalypse](/wiki/Aporkalypse "Aporkalypse"), Pog will turn aggressive towards the player and all other mobs excluding their own kind. In addition, they can no longer be befriended by the player while in this state.

## Trivia[]

* According to Wickerbottom, the scientific name of the Pog is *Otocyon Canis*. This breaks the rules of the [binomial nomenclature](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Binomial_nomenclature "wikipedia:Binomial nomenclature"), as it is a combination of two genera. The *Otocyon* genus refers to the [bat-eared fox](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bat-eared_fox "wikipedia:Bat-eared fox"), whereas *Canis* is the genus containing dogs, wolves, etc.
* According to *Rhymes with Play* #213, the word "Pog" is not a portmanteau for anything. (The truth is, it's the word "Dog" with an attempt to make it a cuter word by starting with a "P" instead of a "D".)
* Because of the name resemblance and the similar color scheme, it's possible that Pog is a reference to the Porgs of the *[Star Wars](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Star_Wars "wikipedia:Star Wars")* universe. It would seem to be a reference to the common internet phrase of the same name. Due to the time that Hamlet was released, this is impossible but an interesting coincidence nonetheless.
* During the Closed Beta phase of *Hamlet*, a Pog used to drop a [Meat](/wiki/Meat "Meat").
* When left in an underground area for some time (such as the [Ancient Pig Ruins](/wiki/Ancient_Pig_Ruins "Ancient Pig Ruins")), Pogs obtain their [Aporkalypse](/wiki/Aporkalypse "Aporkalypse") look without turning aggressive towards the player or other mobs. This is caused by a coding mistake where the feral look is set when its not the [Aporkalypse](/wiki/Aporkalypse "Aporkalypse") instead of the other way around.
