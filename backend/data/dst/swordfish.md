**Swordfish** are [hostile](/wiki/Category:Hostile_Creatures "Category:Hostile Creatures") [Mobs](/wiki/Mobs "Mobs") found in the *[Shipwrecked](/wiki/Shipwrecked "Shipwrecked")* DLC. They spawn in the [Deep Ocean](/wiki/Ocean#Shipwrecked "Ocean") from an invisible spawner. One will spawn in the same spot every 60–120 seconds, as long as the player is nearby.

Despite being hostile, they are considered innocent creatures and killing one adds 4 points to the Player's [naughtiness](/wiki/Guides/Managing_Naughtiness "Guides/Managing Naughtiness") level, which can cause [Krampus](/wiki/Krampus "Krampus") to appear.

When a Swordfish is killed it becomes a **Dead Swordfish**. It can be eaten to restore 1 Health and 25 Hunger. If cooked at a [Campfire](/wiki/Campfire "Campfire"), [Fire Pit](/wiki/Fire_Pit "Fire Pit"), [Chiminea](/wiki/Chiminea "Chiminea"), or [Dwarf Star](/wiki/Star_Caller%27s_Staff "Star Caller's Staff"), it will produce [Fish Steak](/wiki/Fish_Steak "Fish Steak"). Dead Swordfish can be used to craft the [Cutlass Supreme](/wiki/Cutlass_Supreme "Cutlass Supreme"). It can also be [dried](/wiki/Drying_Rack "Drying Rack") to get [Jerky](/wiki/Jerky "Jerky"), or be traded to [Yaarctopus](/wiki/Yaarctopus "Yaarctopus") for [Dubloons](/wiki/Dubloons "Dubloons"). A Dead Swordfish cannot be used to cook in the [Crock Pot](/wiki/Crock_Pot "Crock Pot").

When a Dead Swordfish is left to spoil, it will turn into a [Spoiled Fish](/wiki/Spoiled_Fish "Spoiled Fish").

## Spear Fighting Strategy[]

To fight Swordfish, boat speed of 7 or above is recommended, adding both [Boat](/wiki/Boats "Boats") and the [Sail](/wiki/Sail "Sail") speed. Sailing close to the Swordfish will cause it to attack, which can be dodged by sailing in the opposite direction after the Swordfish pauses before the attack. Then players will be able to land 1 to 2 hits with a melee weapon depending on boat speed. Swordfish cannot be stunlocked.

The player can also dock near a herd of [Water Beefalo](/wiki/Water_Beefalo "Water Beefalo") and let the Swordfish attack the Beefalo. The Beefalo, fighting in groups, will usually win, resulting in the Swordfish's death. However, the player needs to actually get on land for this strategy to work, as the Swordfish will not attack other mobs while chasing the player.

If playing as [Wendy](/wiki/Wendy "Wendy"), one can let [Abigail](/wiki/Abigail "Abigail") attack the Swordfish and even persist until it dies, if given enough time. Note that the Swordfish doesn't change target when fighting, so the player must be wary and ensure to continuously fleeing while Abigail finishes the job. This, on the other hand, can make the process of killing a Swordfish completely safe.

## Prototype Tips[]

* Once the location of the spawner is found, it can be marked by [Items](/wiki/Item "Item") that have [Map](/wiki/Map "Map") markers, like any one of the [Backpacks](/wiki/Backpack "Backpack"), [Traps](/wiki/Trap "Trap"), [Sea Traps](/wiki/Sea_Trap "Sea Trap"), or [Boats](/wiki/Boats "Boats") as well as a [Buoy](/wiki/Buoy "Buoy").
* It'd be wise to save the Swordfish for crafting the Cutlass Supreme as the weapon can serve as a sturdier and saner alternative to the [Dark Sword](/wiki/Dark_Sword "Dark Sword").

## Placeholder Trivia[]

* There is code in the game files that would allow using alive Swordfish in the Crock Pot, counting as 1 unit of [Fish](/wiki/Fishes "Fishes") and 0.5 units of [Meat](/wiki/Meats "Meats"). However, there is no method implemented that would make capturing Swordfish alive possible.
