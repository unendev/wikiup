A **Blueprint** is an [Item](/wiki/Item "Item") that lets the player learn a craftable recipe without having to prototype it first. When used, it restores 15 Sanity. Blueprints themselves do not actually create an item; they only allow the player to craft the item away from the station normally used to prototype it.

Blueprints can be found in some [Set Pieces](/wiki/Set_Pieces "Set Pieces") and at the beginning of each world in [Adventure Mode](/wiki/Adventure_Mode "Adventure Mode"). The player can find more than one Blueprint that unlocks the same recipe, and all Blueprints will restore Sanity when used, even if the player has already researched the item.

The [Bundling Wrap](/wiki/Bundling_Wrap "Bundling Wrap") recipe can only be obtained from a Blueprint dropped by a [Bee](/wiki/Bee "Bee") or [Killer Bee](/wiki/Bee#Killer_Bee "Bee").

### Gift Icon Downloadable Content[]

In the *[Reign of Giants](/wiki/Reign_of_Giants "Reign of Giants")* DLC, Blueprints can also be obtained by destroying [Tumbleweeds](/wiki/Tumbleweed "Tumbleweed") in [Sandbox Mode](/wiki/Sandbox_Mode "Sandbox Mode"). It is possible to find Blueprints for items in the [Ancient Tab](/wiki/Ancient_Tab "Ancient Tab"), but an [Ancient Pseudoscience Station](/wiki/Ancient_Pseudoscience_Station "Ancient Pseudoscience Station") will still be needed to actually craft the item. Ancient Tab Blueprints do not restore any Sanity. The [Old Bell](/wiki/Old_Bell "Old Bell") recipe can only be obtained from a Blueprint dropped by a mined [Glommer's Statue](/wiki/Glommer%27s_Statue "Glommer's Statue").

In the *[Shipwrecked](/wiki/Shipwrecked "Shipwrecked")* DLC, Blueprints can be obtained from [X Marks the Spot](/wiki/X_Marks_the_Spot "X Marks the Spot") treasure chests.

In the *[Hamlet](/wiki/Hamlet "Hamlet")* DLC, both the [Vortex Cloak](/wiki/Vortex_Cloak "Vortex Cloak") and [Living Artifact](/wiki/Living_Artifact "Living Artifact") recipes can only be obtained from Blueprints which drop from the [Ancient Herald](/wiki/Ancient_Herald "Ancient Herald") and the [Large Iron Hulk](/wiki/Large_Iron_Hulk "Large Iron Hulk"), respectively.

In *[Don't Starve Together](/wiki/Don%27t_Starve_Together "Don't Starve Together")*, Blueprints for recipes that are already unlocked will no longer be able to be learned again. Instead, players can recycle them for [Papyrus](/wiki/Papyrus "Papyrus") at the [Cartographer's Desk](/wiki/Cartographer%27s_Desk "Cartographer's Desk").

In *[A New Reign](/wiki/A_New_Reign "A New Reign")*, **Rare Blueprints** were introduced for crafting recipes which cannot be prototyped or crafted at a [station](/wiki/Category:Prototypers "Category:Prototypers"). Many of these Blueprints drop from [Boss Monsters](/wiki/Category:Boss_Monsters "Category:Boss Monsters"), providing incentive for players to fight them. Generally, the recipe from the Blueprint will use the unique drop from the boss.

Other sources of rare Blueprints are the [Stagehand](/wiki/Stagehand "Stagehand") (provides Blueprint for [End Table](/wiki/End_Table "End Table")), [Crumpled Packages](/wiki/Crumpled_Package "Crumpled Package") (provide Blueprints for [Desert Goggles](/wiki/Goggles#Desert_Goggles "Goggles")), the [Ancient Chest](/wiki/Ancient_Chest "Ancient Chest"), [Distilled Knowledge](/wiki/Distilled_Knowledge "Distilled Knowledge"), and the [Loot Stash](/wiki/Loot_Stash "Loot Stash") (can provide some rare Blueprint).

* There are different console commands for each prototypical item in the game. e.g., to spawn a Blueprint for a [Spear](/wiki/Spear "Spear"), the following command should be used: `c_spawn("spear_blueprint")`.
* Adverts were introduced in the *[Hook, Line, and Inker](/wiki/Return_of_Them#Hook,_Line,_and_Inker "Return of Them")* update.
* Adverts were changed to be learnable like blueprints instead of having to be placed into the Tackle Receptacle in the [March 2022 QoL update](/wiki/Don%27t_Starve_Together/Version_History#March_24,_2022_-_March_2022_QoL_Update "Don't Starve Together/Version History"). They were also removed from the drops of [Birds](/wiki/Birds "Birds"), [Moose/Goose](/wiki/Moose/Goose "Moose/Goose"), and [Malbatross](/wiki/Malbatross "Malbatross")
* As of the [March 2022 QoL update](/wiki/Don%27t_Starve_Together/Version_History#March_24,_2022_-_March_2022_QoL_Update "Don't Starve Together/Version History"), the [Feathery Canvas](/wiki/Feathery_Canvas "Feathery Canvas"), [Winged Sail](/wiki/Mast#Winged_Sail "Mast"), and [Waterfowl Can](/wiki/Watering_Can#Waterfowl_Can "Watering Can") no longer require a Blueprint from [Malbatross](/wiki/Malbatross "Malbatross") in order to craft them.
