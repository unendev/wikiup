A **Campfire** is a key to basic survival in the world. It provides [Light](/wiki/Light "Light"), Heat and allows [Food](/wiki/Food "Food") to be [cooked](/wiki/Cooked "Cooked").

It requires 3 [Cut Grass](/wiki/Cut_Grass "Cut Grass") and 2 [Logs](/wiki/Log "Log") to create and is available from the beginning of the game. It is cheaper than a Fire Pit in terms of resources, but also less efficient, as it can not be re-lit. The [fuel](/wiki/Fuel_Values "Fuel Values") will burn twice as fast, it is more susceptible to [Rain](/wiki/Rain#Negative_Effects "Rain"), and it is less safe, as it may set nearby [flammable objects](/wiki/Category:Flammable_Objects "Category:Flammable Objects") on fire. When burnt out, the Campfire drops 1 [Ash](/wiki/Ashes "Ashes") or 1 Charcoal.

One [Charcoal](/wiki/Charcoal "Charcoal") will be dropped by a Campfire or Fire Pit that has reached at least 3/4 of the way to maximum fuel (or 3 logs) when it goes out.

### Icon Tools Usage[]

Generally, campfires are built when lacking resources to make a Fire Pit or when camping in a location that is not meant to be permanent, e.g. wandering away from the main base once it is established.

Once built, the Campfire lasts 2 minutes and 15 seconds unless more fuel is added. Additional fuel increases fire duration linearly up to a maximum of 4.5 minutes while heat and light emitted goes up in four discrete steps: 70 / 120 / 180 / 220 degrees and 2 / 3 / 4 / 5 units of distance, respectively. The different stages can be observed by the growing flames and by examining the fire.

Adding too much fuel to a Campfire can cause nearby [Trees](/wiki/Tree "Tree"), [Grass](/wiki/Grass "Grass"), [Saplings](/wiki/Sapling "Sapling"), or any other flammable objects to catch [fire](/wiki/Fire "Fire"), which can start large forest fires in a [Forest](/wiki/Forest "Forest") [biome](/wiki/Biome "Biome"). This danger can be averted by building away from other flammable objects or opting for the Fire Pit. During [Winter](/wiki/Winter "Winter"), Campfires provide enough warmth to keep a character alive without any additional clothing, provided that the character stays nearby and keeps adding fuel.

It is handy to have one ready at all times just in case the player can't get to their base in time.

In rain, a campfire may burn its fuel up to 250% faster depending on precipitation rate.

A **Fire Pit** is the center of most base camps. It provides light and heat, allows [food](/wiki/Food "Food") to be cooked, and is a contained, safer fire than a Campfire, since it will not set surrounding objects on fire.

It requires 2 [Logs](/wiki/Logs "Logs") and 12 [Rocks](/wiki/Rocks "Rocks") to craft and, like the Campfire, is available from the beginning of the game. It is a persistent structure, unlike Campfires, and can be re-lit by putting fuel in it. A Fire Pit is also more efficient than a Campfire, as the fuel will last 2 times longer, and it is less susceptible to Rain. It drops no Ashes when it burns out, but destroying it will drop the materials needed to build it.

### Icon Tools Usage[]

Fire Pits provide a great deal of light and heat to the surrounding area. Once placed, they cannot be moved unless destroyed by a [Hammer](/wiki/Hammer "Hammer") or [Deconstruction Staff](/wiki/Deconstruction_Staff "Deconstruction Staff"); however, they can be re-lit with any acceptable fuel.

Once built, the Fire Pit lasts 3 minutes unless more fuel is added. Additional fuel increases fire duration linearly up to a maximum of 6 minutes while heat and light emitted goes up in four discrete steps: 70 / 120 / 180 / 220 degrees and 2 / 3 / 4 / 5 units of distance, respectively. The different stages can be observed through the size of the flames and left-click feedback.

Fire Pits also show up as an icon on the [Map](/wiki/Map "Map"). Like the campfire, Fire Pits provide enough warmth during Winter to keep the player alive without any additional clothing, provided that they stay nearby. If the character sleeps the night, the fire will be out when they awake the next day.

In rain, a Fire Pit may burn its fuel up to 200% faster (depending on [precipitation rate](/wiki/Rain "Rain")).

*For fuel values, please see [Fuel Values](/wiki/Fire#Fuel_Values "Fire").*

## Don't Starve Together Don't Starve Together[]

In *[Don't Starve Together](/wiki/Don%27t_Starve_Together "Don't Starve Together")*, when playing as [Walter](/wiki/Walter "Walter"), players can interact with a Campfire during night to make him tell a story, which will restore the [sanity](/wiki/Sanity "Sanity") of other nearby players.

## Placeholder Trivia[]

* Because the fire layers over the rocks surrounding the pit, a player or any mob standing in front of or close enough to the side of the object will appear to be in front of it but behind the fire.
* The Fire Pit resembles a fire ring rather than an actual fire pit.
* The flame sprite is reused in *[The Screecher](/wiki/Screecher "Screecher")*.
* Before Ash was added to the game, the fire pit was left as a pile of burnt logs before disappearing about a minute later, this can still briefly be seen after a fire pit goes out.
