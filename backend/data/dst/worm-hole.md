A **Worm Hole** is a living tunnel, which warps the player to a different area of the map. Worm Holes appear as a mouth in the ground, and will open when approached by the player. When the option ‘Jump’ is selected, the character will leap inside to be spit out the other end after a brief delay. There are multiple Worm Holes in most worlds and they always connect the same two places on the map. Often they will end in two completely different areas with different resources, such as a [Forest](/wiki/Forest "Forest") (with lots of [Trees](/wiki/Tree "Tree")) and a [Savanna](/wiki/Savanna "Savanna") (with lots of [Grass](/wiki/Grass "Grass")).

Traveling through a Worm Hole costs 15 Sanity. [Sanity](/wiki/Sanity "Sanity") can be quickly drained by repeatedly jumping into them. [Chester](/wiki/Chester "Chester") and other [followers](/wiki/Category:Followers "Category:Followers") will follow the player through Worm Holes. Tradable [items](/wiki/Items "Items"), such as [Food](/wiki/Food "Food"), [Gravedigger Items](/wiki/Gravedigger_Items "Gravedigger Items"), and [Wooden Thing](/wiki/Wooden_Thing "Wooden Thing") components can be dropped into a Worm Hole to be spit out at the other end.

A **Sick Worm Hole** is a dying Worm Hole found in the "*Two Worlds*" chapter of [Adventure Mode](/wiki/Adventure_Mode "Adventure Mode"). Sick Worm Holes can only be used for one-way travel, after which their "exit" shrivels up and dies. They look similar to their healthy counterparts, but their "skin" and mouth have a yellowish/greenish tint, instead of being brown; the color of a normal Worm Hole.

## Downloadable Content[]

In the *[Shipwrecked](/wiki/Shipwrecked "Shipwrecked")* DLC, Worm Holes do not exist. However, the [Electric Isosceles](/wiki/Electric_Isosceles "Electric Isosceles") acts in a similar manner.

In the *[Hamlet](/wiki/Hamlet "Hamlet")* DLC, Worm Holes are used to connect unreachable islands.

## Don't Starve Together Don't Starve Together[]

In *[Don't Starve Together](/wiki/Don%27t_Starve_Together "Don't Starve Together")*, items that are not tradable can be dropped into a Worm Hole as well. Players no longer do a sleeping animation after leaving the Worm Hole. Using a [Clean Sweeper](/wiki/Clean_Sweeper "Clean Sweeper"), the Worm Hole skin can be changed. This change will also appear on the map for both ends of the Worm Hole.

## Tips[]

* Worm Holes can be used to escape dangerous enemies, such as [Deerclops](/wiki/Deerclops "Deerclops"), [Hounds](/wiki/Hounds "Hounds"), or [MacTusk](/wiki/MacTusk "MacTusk"), but they will be waiting at the Worm Hole when it is used to return. This can be a good way to temporarily escape tough mobs when unprepared to deal with them at the moment.
  + Worm Hole camps can be built, in order to have a backup base, with [Fire Pits](/wiki/Campfire "Campfire"), [Science Machines](/wiki/Science_Machine "Science Machine"), and other structures on both sides, in case tough enemies show up.
  + The player should check both entrances before seasons such as [Winter](/wiki/Winter "Winter") in case stationary monsters show up, such as MacTusk or [Tallbirds](/wiki/Tallbirds "Tallbirds"), and prepare to battle them as soon the player emerges from the Worm Hole.
* Building a settlement next to a Worm Hole offers quick travel to another biome/area.
* It's a good idea to note the positions and links between Worm Holes, to avoid confusion/wasted time. A good way to do this is by building Structures, dropping items or planting [Plants](/wiki/Plants "Plants") of the same type and have [Map](/wiki/Map "Map") icons close to two linked Wormholes.
* A Worm Hole can be used as a way to move items without worrying about inventory slots and Sanity loss.
* Jumping through a pair of wormholes can allow a player to quickly become [Insane](/wiki/Insane "Insane").

## Trivia[]

* The Sick Worm Hole was added in the *[Doorway To Adventure](/wiki/Don%27t_Starve/Version_History#Doorway_to_Adventure "Don't Starve/Version History")* update.
* [Wickerbottom](/wiki/Wickerbottom "Wickerbottom") refers to the Worm Hole as "*Megadrilacea oraduos*". Megadrilacea is an order of earthworms, and oraduos means two mouths or openings.
* A Worm Hole spawned via DebugSpawn will only open its mouth when the character walks away from it and can only be examined, unless done as described in the [Console](/wiki/Console "Console") page.
* A Worm Hole makes breathing noises when its "mouth" is open.
* Removing one side of a Worm Hole will crash the game upon traveling via the other side.
* The name "Worm Hole" is a pun, referencing the concept of a hypothetical connection between two points in distant space-time, or in other words; an opening that allows traveling between two places almost instantly.

## Bugs[]

* Sometimes Worm Holes can spawn in [Caves](/wiki/Caves "Caves"), though they can't be used.
* Rarely, Worm Holes can spawn on the ocean. If a player goes through the wormhole that connects with this wormhole on the water, they will be able to walk on water. However, they will be unable to travel back on land unless one goes into the wormhole again.
* Jumping into a Worm Hole and then interrupting the action by either clicking away, changing equipment, or being attacked will make a sound as if the player jumped into the Worm Hole. The player is not teleported and no Sanity is lost.
* If a character jumps into a Worm Hole and gets killed simultaneously, they will appear on the other side of the Wormhole with the box saying "You are dead!", but they will still be able to move around and are invincible. They won't be able to interact with anything but the "You are dead!" box and they can still move without the mouse. Time still moves and, if playing as Willow, she can still set fires with low sanity. Nothing will attack the player character, including [Charlie](/wiki/Charlie "Charlie"). Performing this glitch has to be frame perfect.
