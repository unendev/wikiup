The **Royal Guard** is a [Mob](/wiki/Mob "Mob") exclusive to the *[Hamlet](/wiki/Don%27t_Starve:_Hamlet "Don't Starve: Hamlet")* DLC. It wields a [Halberd](/wiki/Halberd "Halberd") during the day, and a [Torch](/wiki/Torch "Torch") during the night.

They can be recruited to assist the player by giving them a [Security Contract](/wiki/Security_Contract "Security Contract").

Royal Guard can be set on fire, but its burning time is much shorter (2 sec).

Much like the [Pig Traders](/wiki/Pig_Traders "Pig Traders") they protect, Royal Guards do not become [Werepigs](/wiki/Werepig "Werepig") if eating too much [Monster Food](/wiki/Monster_Food "Monster Food") or during a [full moon](/wiki/Moon_Cycle "Moon Cycle"), in contrast to vanilla [Pigs](/wiki/Pig "Pig") and [Wildbores](/wiki/Wildbore "Wildbore").

## Brain Behavior[]

Royal Guards will attack anyone who does something inappropriate, excluding [Wilba](/wiki/Wilba "Wilba"). This includes:

* Harvesting [Cut Grass](/wiki/Cut_Grass "Cut Grass"), [Twigs](/wiki/Twigs "Twigs"), [Berries](/wiki/Berries "Berries"), [Petals](/wiki/Petals "Petals") or [Rocks](/wiki/Rocks "Rocks") from the farms around the Pig Villages.
* Picking up objects lying around the farmer or miner setpieces: this includes Rocks and tools.
* Picking up items that came from the setpiece or the Guard/dead ex-Guard himself, like an unrooted Berry Bush, the loot from the last Guard, or Rocks from destroyed walls caused by Mob.
* Burning or destroying the aforementioned items **directly**.
* Attacking pigs in town / Royal Guards themselves (includes Wilba).
* Destroying town structures (includes chopping [Birchnut Trees](/wiki/Birchnut_Tree "Birchnut Tree")).

They will extinguish any fire they spot, as [Campfires](/wiki/Campfire "Campfire") or [Fire Pits](/wiki/Fire_Pit "Fire Pit"), either in the night or the day. Other [Light sources](/wiki/Light_sources "Light sources") as [Torch](/wiki/Torch "Torch"), [Lantern](/wiki/Lantern "Lantern") or [Cork Candle Hat](/wiki/Cork_Candle_Hat "Cork Candle Hat") will remain untouched.

If the player has stolen anything, Royal Guards that spotted them will remember and attack the player on sight every time they'll see them again. However, those that remember the player's misdeeds can be bribed off for 10 [Oincs](/wiki/Oinc "Oinc"). If the player attacked a Pig Villager, all Royal Guards around will remember it and attack them on sight. If the player attacked a Royal Guard, all Royal Guards nearby will aggro the player, but only the one attacked will remember.

Wearing the [Swashy Hat](/wiki/Swashy_Hat "Swashy Hat") at dusk or night can prevent guards from noticing the player's normally illegal behaviors.

Killing the Royal Guards and waiting for new ones to spawn in will undo any mistakes made, since the new Guards are completely new Pigs and won't know of the misbehavior.

## Prototype Tips[]

* Since a torch deals less damage than a Halberd (and doesn't set the player on fire, except [Wormwood](/wiki/Wormwood "Wormwood")), it's best to fight Royal Guards at dusk, or night, for an easier fight.
* Before harvesting the farms, it is best to kill all the Royal Guards protecting them, as you will be free to collect what you want without being interrupted. Plus, once the new guards will spawn, they won't remember your misdeeds.
* Caution must be taken when fighting mobs at dusk/night near Royal Guards: they will attack hostile mobs on sight, and even if their Torches don't set the player on fire, they do set fire to the mobs attacked. Due to a lack of caring This might cause massive damage, especially to the farms.
* Both of the Royal Guards weapons are actual items in their inventory, meaning that attacks which drop items (such as [Grasping Vines](/wiki/Hanging_Vine "Hanging Vine"), [Frogs](/wiki/Poison_Dartfrog "Poison Dartfrog") or [Moose/Goose](/wiki/Moose/Goose "Moose/Goose")) will cause them to drop their unequipped weapon. This will prevent them from switching weapons at different times of day.
* Like [Pig Traders](/wiki/Pig_Traders "Pig Traders"), Royal Guards will warn of the Aporkalypse if it is near, making it important to listen so you can avoid it/prepare for it
* Royal Guards wear a [Log Suit](/wiki/Log_Suit "Log Suit"), so the number of strikes to kill them is misleading. Keep this in mind when fighting them, as their armor needs to be destroyed first before taking full damage. Alternatively, indirect damage can bypass the armor and kill them, making them drop the Log Suit.
* A trap made of [Walls](/wiki/Wall "Wall") and an Oinc used as bait can be used to trap a Royal Guard in place, so they can be killed without having to deal with their kiting.
* In order to bribe off the guards, one can drop the 10 oincs on the ground, if attempting to give them to the guard directly, it will simply have them attack.

## Placeholder Trivia[]

* The Royal Guard outfit may have been inspired by the [Pontifical Swiss Guard](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pontifical_Swiss_Guard "wikipedia:Pontifical Swiss Guard").
* The Royal Guards appear to be wearing [Fancy Helmets](/wiki/Fancy_Helmet "Fancy Helmet").
  + Despite not actually wearing Fancy Helmets, Royal Guards do wear wooden armor. Additionally, they are the only creature in the game (other than the player) that wears armor.
  + This also means that Royal Guards are currently the only NPC to spawn wearing functional armor.
* Although their torch and halberd are given infinite durability when the Royal Guard spawns, their armor still has limited durability and can be broken.
* In the game files, their halberd is referred to as an axe when using the function to remove the infinite durability.
