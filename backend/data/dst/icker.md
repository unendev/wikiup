**Ickers** are hostile [mobs](/wiki/Mobs "Mobs") exclusive to *[Don't Starve Together](/wiki/Don%27t_Starve_Together "Don't Starve Together")*, introduced in the *[From Beyond](/wiki/From_Beyond "From Beyond")* updates. When [Shadow Rifts](/wiki/Shadow_Rift "Shadow Rift") are active, they will spawn in groups of 5 to 10 on narrow passages in the [Caves](/wiki/Caves "Caves").

Ickers have [Planar Entity Protection](/wiki/Planar_Entity_Protection "Planar Entity Protection"), which greatly reduces physical [damage](/wiki/Damage "Damage") from a [Weapon](/wiki/Weapon "Weapon").

## Behavior[]

A group of Ickers can fall down from the ceiling if a character walks through a narrow passage that connects [biomes](/wiki/Biomes "Biomes"). Ickers cannot move. When attacked, they drop blobs slowing down [characters](/wiki/Characters "Characters") and mobs. These blobs will crawl slowly back to the Ickers, returning the health they have lost through the attack.

When characters get to close to an Icker, they will be slowed down by its blobs. When the character gets eaten by an Icker, it will perform 4 hits with each 20 damage and 20 planar damage. It will also steal one equipped item before releasing the character. The stolen item can only be released when killing or re-entering the Icker.

A [Beefalo](/wiki/Beefalo "Beefalo") will also get slowed down by Ickers, but both, the Beefalo and the character, will not get eaten by them.

Ickers will despawn once Shadow Rifts close.

## Strategy[]

As Ickers will get healed by their blobs and have planar entity protection, it is hard to kill them alone with regular [weapons](/wiki/Weapon "Weapon"). Using weapons with planar damage like the [Brightshade Sword](/wiki/Brightshade_Sword "Brightshade Sword") will help dealing more damage. Also, the blobs from an Icker can be collected with [Empty Bottles](/wiki/Empty_Bottle "Empty Bottle"), preventing them to heal their host.

## Tips[]

* Ickers are great way of gaining Sanity and Health, when playing as Wigfrid. Because the blobs will heal the Ickers continuously, this also allows her to gain inspiration very easily.

## Trivia[]

* Ickers were introduced in the *[Depths of Duplicity](/wiki/Depths_of_Duplicity "Depths of Duplicity")* update.
* In a *Rhymes with Play* stream, the developers claim that Icker is "basically another state of the [Miasma](/wiki/Miasma "Miasma")".
