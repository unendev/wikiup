**Nightmare Fuel** is a [Refined](/wiki/Refined "Refined") [Item](/wiki/Item "Item") used in [Dark](/wiki/Shadow_Tab "Shadow Tab") and [Ancient](/wiki/Ancient "Ancient") [Magic](/wiki/Magic "Magic"). Most [Shadow Aligned](/wiki/Shadow_Aligned "Shadow Aligned") mobs drop it when killed, it can be prototyped with a [Prestihatitator](/wiki/Prestihatitator "Prestihatitator") and 4 [Dark Petals](/wiki/Dark_Petals "Dark Petals"), or
Wilson can craft it from 2 [Pure Horror](/wiki/Pure_Horror "Pure Horror") if the player has mastered [Shadow Courtier](/wiki/Shadow_Courtier "Shadow Courtier").

Mobs that drop Nightmare Fuel include:

* [Shadow Creatures](/wiki/Shadow_Creature "Shadow Creature")
* [Beardlings](/wiki/Beardling "Beardling")
* [Damaged Clockworks](/wiki/Category:Clockwork_Monsters#Damaged_Knight "Category:Clockwork Monsters")
* Shadow [Splumonkeys](/wiki/Splumonkey "Splumonkey")

Objects that drop Nightmare Fuel include:

* [Graves](/wiki/Grave "Grave") - when dug using a [Shovel](/wiki/Shovel "Shovel")
* [Touch Stone](/wiki/Touch_Stone "Touch Stone") - when resurrected
* [Ancient Statues](/wiki/Ancient_Statue "Ancient Statue") - when mined with a [Pickaxe](/wiki/Pickaxe "Pickaxe")
* [Pig Heads](/wiki/Pig_Head "Pig Head") - when hammered on a full moon
* [Ornate Chests](/wiki/Ornate_Chest "Ornate Chest") (25% chance) & Large Ornate Chests (75% chance)

Nightmare Fuel is used to craft various Magic items. It is also used to fuel [Night Lights](/wiki/Night_Light "Night Light"), [Maxwell's](/wiki/Maxwell/Character "Maxwell/Character") [Codex Umbra](/wiki/Codex_Umbra "Codex Umbra"), the [Magiluminescence](/wiki/Magiluminescence "Magiluminescence") and other objects. [Chester](/wiki/Chester "Chester") can transform into [Shadow Chester](/wiki/Shadow_Chester "Shadow Chester") by placing Nightmare Fuel in each of his nine inventory slots on a [Full Moon](/wiki/Full_Moon "Full Moon").

## Gathering[]

*https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/dont-starve-game/images/c/cb/Glommer.png/revision/latest?cb=20231029201751 See also: [Guides/Nightmare Fuel Farming](/wiki/Guides/Nightmare_Fuel_Farming "Guides/Nightmare Fuel Farming")*

Nightmare Fuel is typically acquired by going insane and [killing Shadow Creatures](/wiki/Shadow_Creature#Fighting_Strategy "Shadow Creature"). See that article for details.

One could pick [Evil Flowers](/wiki/Evil_Flower "Evil Flower") and craft the fuel from [Dark Petals](/wiki/Dark_Petals "Dark Petals"). Picking each costs 5 sanity. Also, Evil Flowers have a negative sanity aura, so just moving to pick one costs sanity. Evil Flowers do not respawn and [Homeless Bee](/wiki/Bee#Homeless_Bee "Bee") propagation is severely limited. Each nightmare fuel crafted this way costs more than 20 sanity and many fuel are needed to craft the typical array of useful magic items. The character will need to restore their sanity multiple times during this process or end up insane and fighting shadow creatures anyway.

One could [trap](/wiki/Trap "Trap") a number of [Rabbits](/wiki/Rabbit "Rabbit"), lower [sanity](/wiki/Sanity "Sanity") below 40% (so the Rabbits turn into Beardlings), then murder them in inventory. This yields [Monster Meat](/wiki/Monster_Meat "Monster Meat") and [Beard Hair](/wiki/Beard_Hair "Beard Hair") in addition to nightmare fuel, but murdering too many innocent creatures quickly can advance the player's naughtiness enough to spawn [Krampus](/wiki/Krampus "Krampus").

The [Ruins](/wiki/Ruins "Ruins") are extremely dangerous, but filled with things that drop nightmare fuel. In Don't Starve, when the [Nightmare Cycle](/wiki/Nightmare_Cycle "Nightmare Cycle") ends, all of the Shadow Creatures that spawned from the [Nightmare Lights](/wiki/Nightmare_Light "Nightmare Light") in the Ruins and [Nightmare Fissures](/wiki/Nightmare_Fissure "Nightmare Fissure") in the [Caves](/wiki/Caves "Caves") will despawn and drop Nightmare Fuel. In Don't Starve Together they tend to despawn without dropping anything and must be killed before the cycle ends in order to drop fuel.

## Gift Icon Downloadable Content[]

In the *[Reign of Giants](/wiki/Reign_of_Giants "Reign of Giants")* and Don't Starve Together, Nightmare Fuel can be obtained by hammering [Pig Heads](/wiki/Pig_Head "Pig Head") and [Merm Heads](/wiki/Merm_Head "Merm Head") during a [Full Moon](/wiki/Moon_Cycle "Moon Cycle").

In the *[Shipwrecked](/wiki/Shipwrecked "Shipwrecked")* DLC, Nightmare Fuel can also be acquired by gambling at the [Slot Machine](/wiki/Slot_Machine "Slot Machine"), by hammering [Wildbore Heads](/wiki/Wildbore_Head "Wildbore Head") during a Full Moon, or by killing [Crabbit Beardlings](/wiki/Crabbit "Crabbit") or [Swimming Horrors](/wiki/Swimming_Horror "Swimming Horror") while insane.

In the *[Hamlet](/wiki/Hamlet "Hamlet")* DLC, Nightmare Fuel can also be acquired by killing the [Ancient Herald](/wiki/Ancient_Herald "Ancient Herald") and a low chance from [A Smashing Pot](/wiki/A_Smashing_Pot "A Smashing Pot"). Nightmare fuel can also be given to the [End's Well](/wiki/End%27s_Well "End's Well") for 1-3 purple gems each, as many times as you want for an endless supply of purple gems.

## Don't Starve Together icon Don't Starve Together[]

In *[Don't Starve Together](/wiki/Don%27t_Starve_Together "Don't Starve Together")*, all variants of the [Reanimated Skeleton](/wiki/Reanimated_Skeleton "Reanimated Skeleton") drop 2-4 Nightmare Fuel when killed. When the [Ancient Fuelweaver](/wiki/Ancient_Fuelweaver "Ancient Fuelweaver") is killed, all [Woven Shadows](/wiki/Woven_Shadow "Woven Shadow") will turn into Nightmare Fuel. It can also be used to refuel certain items. Unlike in DLCs, Nightmare Fuel no longer gets wet. The [Nightmare Werepig](/wiki/Nightmare_Werepig "Nightmare Werepig") fight yields many nightmare fuel as well.

## Placeholder Trivia[]

* The term "nightmare fuel" is a common phrase on the Internet used to describe works of fiction, or scenes therein, that are extremely likely to disturb and/or frighten the audience.
* When dropped on the ground, Nightmare Fuel appears as pulsing goo.
* Unlike Beardlings, [Beardlords](/wiki/Beardlords "Beardlords") do not drop Nightmare Fuel.
* Maxwell, upon examining the [Ancient Guardian](/wiki/Ancient_Guardian "Ancient Guardian"), says "My my, the fuel has changed you." This implies that prolonged exposure to Nightmare Fuel can alter creatures in some significant way. This is also proven by the ability to turn Chester into his shadow form via Nightmare Fuel.
* According to [Webber](/wiki/Webber "Webber"), Nightmare Fuel feels cold. However, according to [Willow](/wiki/Willow "Willow"), Nightmare Fuel feels warm. This may indicate it has different properties to different people.
* In DST, Shadow Creatures no longer drop Nightmare Fuel at the end of the [Nightmare Cycle](/wiki/Nightmare_Cycle "Nightmare Cycle"). It was stated by the developers that the drops in the [original game](/wiki/Don%27t_Starve "Don't Starve") were caused by a bug and not intended.
