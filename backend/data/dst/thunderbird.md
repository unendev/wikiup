The **Thunderbird** is a [Mob](/wiki/Mob "Mob") exclusive to the *[Hamlet](/wiki/Don%27t_Starve:_Hamlet "Don't Starve: Hamlet")* DLC. It can be found in the [Painted](/wiki/Painted "Painted") [Biome](/wiki/Biomes "Biomes"). Its head will follow the player's movements. If the player gets close (about 2 [Pitchfork](/wiki/Pitchfork "Pitchfork") tiles) the bird will charge up, and make electric sounds. If the player gets even closer (about one Pitchfork tile) the bird will emit three [Lightning](/wiki/Lightning "Lightning") bolts around it at random, dealing 10-20 damage per strike (depending on [Wetness](/wiki/Wetness "Wetness") level), likely hitting the player or nearby trees with at least one.

Thunderbirds can be chased down and killed after they release their charge, since it takes them a minute before they can strike again. They respawn at their [Thundernest](/wiki/Thundernest "Thundernest") 5 days after being killed.

## Prototype Tips[]

* [WX-78](/wiki/WX-78 "WX-78") players can use the Thunderbird's electrical attack to attain System Overload.
* The Thunderbird's electrical attack can revive the [Iron Hulks](/wiki/Iron_Hulk "Iron Hulk").
  + If there is an Iron Hulk piece nearby you can approach the thunderbird safely as it will attract all the lightning, even as WX-78.
* During the [Aporkalypse](/wiki/Seasons/Aporkalypse "Seasons/Aporkalypse"), Thunderbirds emit 10 Lightning bolts instead of 3.
* Thunderbirds do not flee from Abigail. Wendy players can easily kill these by staying out of their lightning bolt range and commanding Abigail to attack. During the day time, Abigail will need to be commanded a second time or else she will lose interest due to the lack of counter attack.
* It only takes one hit from a [Blow Dart](/wiki/Blow_Dart "Blow Dart") to kill a Thunderbird, so using the disarming kit to collect Blow Darts from [Striking Carvings](/wiki/Striking_Carving "Striking Carving") will help with collecting iron ore, drumsticks, and [Thunderbird Feathers](/wiki/Feather "Feather").
