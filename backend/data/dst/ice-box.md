The **Ice Box** is a [Structure](/wiki/Structure "Structure") used to store and preserve [Food](/wiki/Food "Food"). Items inside will spoil two times slower. It requires 2 [Gold Nuggets](/wiki/Gold_Nugget "Gold Nugget"), a [Gear](/wiki/Gears "Gears"), and a [Board](/wiki/Boards "Boards") to craft and an [Alchemy Engine](/wiki/Alchemy_Engine "Alchemy Engine") to prototype.

Like a [Chest](/wiki/Chest "Chest"), the Ice Box can hold up to 9 items or stacks of food type items. It cannot, however, contain the never spoiling types of food such as Mandrakes or [Tallbird Eggs](/wiki/Tallbird_Egg "Tallbird Egg").

When demolished by a [Hammer](/wiki/Hammer "Hammer") or other means, it returns one Gear, Gold Nugget, and Board, along with any items stored in it.

It is possible to find an [Ice Box trap](/wiki/Set_Pieces#Icebox_Trap "Set Pieces"). If the Chest next to the Ice Box is opened, [Winter](/wiki/Winter "Winter") has a chance to immediately begin. The player can safely demolish the Ice Box with a Hammer for its components, but demolishing the Chest can still trigger the trap. If Winter was already in progress when the trap has been triggered, the progress will extend as if it were already around the first [day](/wiki/Day "Day") of Winter.

## Downloadable Content[]

In all [DLCs](/wiki/DLC "DLC") as well as DST, [Ice](/wiki/Ice "Ice"), [Hail](/wiki/Hail "Hail"), [Ice Cubes](/wiki/Ice_Cube "Ice Cube"), and [Fashion Melons](/wiki/Fashion_Melon "Fashion Melon") can be stored indefinitely in an Ice Box. [Thermal Stones](/wiki/Thermal_Stone "Thermal Stone") can be chilled if stored. Crafting an Ice Box also costs one [Cut Stone](/wiki/Cut_Stone "Cut Stone") instead of Boards.

In the *Reign of Giants* DLC, Ice Boxes become renewable due to the 1% chance of Gears dropping from [Tumbleweeds](/wiki/Tumbleweed "Tumbleweed"). There is also a version of the Ice Box trap that starts [Summer](/wiki/Summer "Summer") instead of Winter. It's distinguishable from the Winter one since it only spawns in a [Desert Biome](/wiki/Desert "Desert"). Unlike the Winter trap, opening or hammering the Ice Box *or* the Chest will have a chance to start Summer.

In *Shipwrecked* DLC, an Ice Box can become flooded by [Flooding](/wiki/Flooding "Flooding"). A flooded Ice Box does not slow down the food spoilage rate although it can still be used as storage. Ice Boxes are easy to renew using Gears from [Floaty Boaty Knights](/wiki/Floaty_Boaty_Knight "Floaty Boaty Knight").

In *Hamlet* DLC, ice Boxes are easy to renew using Gears from ['The Sty' Oddities Emporiums](/wiki/%27The_Sty%27_Oddities_Emporium "'The Sty' Oddities Emporium") and [Large Iron Hulks](/wiki/Iron_Hulk "Iron Hulk").

## Usage[]

The following items can be placed in an Ice Box:

* Most Food items
* [Ham Bat](/wiki/Ham_Bat "Ham Bat") https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/dont-starve-game/images/f/f8/Reign of Giants icon.png/revision/latest?cb=20140408110712Don't Starve Together
* [Thermal Stone](/wiki/Thermal_Stone "Thermal Stone") https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/dont-starve-game/images/f/f8/Reign of Giants icon.png/revision/latest?cb=20140408110712Don't Starve Together
* [Ice Cube](/wiki/Ice_Cube "Ice Cube") https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/dont-starve-game/images/f/f8/Reign of Giants icon.png/revision/latest?cb=20140408110712Don't Starve Together
* [Fashion Melon](/wiki/Fashion_Melon "Fashion Melon") https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/dont-starve-game/images/f/f8/Reign of Giants icon.png/revision/latest?cb=20140408110712Don't Starve Together

As food spoils faster in Summer in *Reign of Giants*, it is recommended that players put their food in the Ice Box to slow down the spoiling rate of food and help preserve food for Winter.

## Tips[]

* Because destroying an Ice Box returns 100% of Gears used in crafting, extra Gears can safely be used in crafting Ice Boxes without fear of losing non-renewable resources.
* If playing as [Warly](/wiki/Warly "Warly"), crafting [Chef Pouches](/wiki/Chef_Pouch "Chef Pouch") can reduce or eliminate the need for an Ice Box. This is particularly useful when engaging in nomadic play styles, as not all gold is returned when hammering an Ice Box. However, Chef Pouches do not prevent Ice and Hail from melting, unlike Ice Boxes. Additionally, when playing in Don't Starve Together, the Chef Pouch only slows the decay of food to 75% of its base duration, instead of 50%.
* If the player has access to [bundling wrap](/wiki/Bundling_Wrap "Bundling Wrap") they can store bundled food in chests saving room in their ice boxes.

## Trivia[]

* The Ice Box was added in the *[Spoiled Rotten](/wiki/Version_History#Spoiled_Rotten "Version History")* update. Before *[A Little Rain Must Fall](/wiki/Version_History#A_Little_Rain_Must_Fall "Version History")* update, constructing one did not require Gears, instead using Cut Stone. [Ham Bats](/wiki/Ham_Bat "Ham Bat") and [Garlands](/wiki/Garland "Garland") were able to be stored in Ice Boxes until [a November 14, 2013 bugfix/performance update](/wiki/Version_History#Nov_14_Bugfix "Version History"), when the two items were made non-edible.

## Bugs[]

* In Pocket Edition, dragging an item from the Ice Box onto an object in the [Inventory](/wiki/Inventory "Inventory") bar, in order to replace it into the Ice Box, may cause the item in the inventory to teleport onto the ocean.
* In [DST](/wiki/Don%27t_Starve_Together "Don't Starve Together"), if a customized Ice Box is placed on a [boat](/wiki/Boat_Kit "Boat Kit") and the players opens it, the particles it emits will keep flowing out where it has been opened, in the event the boat changes its position.
  + This can be fixed by closing the Ice Box. Opening it again will cause the bug to occur afresh.
