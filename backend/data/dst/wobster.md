The **Wobster** is a catchable sea [Creature](/wiki/Mobs "Mobs") found in the *[Shipwrecked](/wiki/Don%27t_Starve:_Shipwrecked "Don't Starve: Shipwrecked")* DLC. Wobsters spawn from [Wobster Dens](/wiki/Wobster_Den "Wobster Den"), which house one Wobster each. Wobsters are nocturnal, and will only come out at [Dusk](/wiki/Dusk "Dusk"), will stay out all through the [Night](/wiki/Night "Night"), and will retreat back to their dens when the next [day](/wiki/Day "Day") begins. Players who get too close will also cause them to be scared and go back to their dens.

They can be caught using [Sea Traps](/wiki/Sea_Trap "Sea Trap") or a [Trawl Net](/wiki/Trawl_Net "Trawl Net"). They are attracted to [Limpets](/wiki/Limpets "Limpets"), which can be used to bait the trap to improve chances of catching them. Leaving them caught in a trap will cause them to starve to death in 1.5 game days, dropping one [Rot](/wiki/Rot "Rot") instead. Wobsters that have been dropped on land by the player will not move and will die after a short amount of time.

When caught alive, they can be used in [Crock Pot](/wiki/Crock_Pot "Crock Pot") recipes. As inventory items, they can be murdered by right-clicking them to produce a Dead Wobster. The dead version can only be eaten raw or cooked, and cannot be used in Crock Pot recipes.

Wobsters take 1 blast from the [Ice Staff](/wiki/Ice_Staff "Ice Staff") to be frozen and 1 [Sleep Dart](/wiki/Sleep_Dart "Sleep Dart") to be put to sleep.

The **Wobster** was introduced to *[Don't Starve Together](/wiki/Don%27t_Starve_Together "Don't Starve Together")* in the *[Return of Them](/wiki/Return_of_Them "Return of Them")* updates. They spawn from [Wobster Mounds](/wiki/Wobster_Mound "Wobster Mound") during [Dusk](/wiki/Dusk "Dusk") and [Night](/wiki/Night "Night"). They can be used alive in a Crock Pot for their 1.0 fish and 1.0 meat value. Aside from all the fish and meat dishes they can be used for, they have two exclusive recipes: [Lobster Bisque](/wiki/Wobster_Bisque "Wobster Bisque") and [Lobster Dinner](/wiki/Wobster_Dinner "Wobster Dinner"). While both recipes excel at healing by granting 60 HP, the latter also gives 50 sanity and is [Maxwell](/wiki/Maxwell "Maxwell")'s favorite dish, providing him with additional 15 Hunger upon consumption.

They behave mostly like [Ocean Fishes](/wiki/Ocean_Fishes "Ocean Fishes"), as they can be caught with a [Sea Fishing Rod](/wiki/Sea_Fishing_Rod "Sea Fishing Rod") and [weigh](/wiki/Weight "Weight") between 153.67 and 307.34. Unlike the ocean fish, they don't resist and can easily be pulled with consistent reeling. If left on the ground for a small amount of time, they break free from their shock and hurry back into the nearest sea water similar to the behavior of released rabbits. They can be baited by any [lure](/wiki/Lures "Lures"), but they have a special apathy towards seeds. Trying to catch them with seeds or without any lure applies a 0.25 multiplier to the attractiveness. As with other seawater fish, they can be kept alive inside a [Tin Fishin' Bin](/wiki/Tin_Fishin%27_Bin "Tin Fishin' Bin"), which can keep them fresh indefinitely. They can be weighed by the [Pocket Scale](/wiki/Pocket_Scale "Pocket Scale") and those that weigh in the top 30% percentile of their species can be gifted to the [Crabby Hermit](/wiki/Crabby_Hermit "Crabby Hermit") for her 5 Heavy Fish quest. They can also be displayed inside a [Fish Scale-O-Matic](/wiki/Fish_Scale-O-Matic "Fish Scale-O-Matic").

A **Dead Wobster** is obtained by right-clicking on a live Wobster in the [inventory](/wiki/Inventory "Inventory"). It can be cooked over a fire, but cannot be used in the Crock Pot.

A **Delicious Wobster** is obtained from cooking a Wobster at a [Campfire](/wiki/Campfire "Campfire"), [Fire Pit](/wiki/Fire_Pit "Fire Pit"), [Chiminea](/wiki/Chiminea "Chiminea"), or [Dwarf Star](/wiki/Star_Caller%27s_Staff "Star Caller's Staff"). It cannot be used in the Crock Pot.

Since Dead Wobsters and Delicious Wobsters fill the same amount of Hunger and Health, and since only the live Wobster can be used in Crock Pot dishes, the only reason to cook a Wobster is if the player wishes to keep it fresh for longer.

## Tips[]

* To restore a live Wobster in the inventory to full freshness, it can be fed any food in the fish or meat category (including [Monster Meat](/wiki/Monster_Meat "Monster Meat") and [Jellyfish](/wiki/Jellyfish "Jellyfish")). It can also eat [Blubber](/wiki/Blubber "Blubber") or [Eggs](/wiki/Eggs "Eggs") but not any Fruits or Vegetables.

## Trivia[]

* Being able to only use live Wobsters in the [Crock Pot](/wiki/Crock_Pot "Crock Pot") is a reference on how lobsters are often cooked in real life as they are put in boiling water alive.
* The name, as implied by Wilson's quote, may be a reference to the *[Looney Tunes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Looney_Tunes "wikipedia:Looney Tunes")* character Elmer Fudd's [way of speaking](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Elmer_Fudd#Elmer-speak "wikipedia:Elmer Fudd"), particularly when he refers to Bugs Bunny as a "Wascally Wabbit".
* Wobsters were introduced to *Don't Starve Together* in the *[She Sells Sea Shells](/wiki/Return_of_Them#She_Sells_Sea_Shells "Return of Them")* update. While the Wobsters retain their name from *Shipwrecked*, they have a darker coloration, and the two Wobster recipes that were ported from *Shipwrecked* were renamed as Lobster Bisque and Lobster Dinner.
  + Despite being caught in the same way as other ocean fish, being aquatic, and giving fish value in the crockpot Wobsters do not give [Wurt](/wiki/Wurt "Wurt") a sanity regen effect for having one in her inventory, however a dead wobster does give the sanity draining effect from dead fish.
