## Placeholder Trivia[]

* Wortox was released with quotes for "NEXTCHARACTER" in his speech file. These quotes were removed at some time between Warly's update and Woodie's refresh.
* Wortox's quote for a Luxury Axe ("Now where's my golden fiddle?") is a reference to the song "[The Devil Went Down to Georgia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Devil_Went_Down_to_Georgia "wikipedia:The Devil Went Down to Georgia")" by [Charlie Daniels](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Charlie_Daniels "wikipedia:Charlie Daniels").
* Wortox's quote for a Pickaxe ("Heigh ho, heigh ho!") is a reference to the song "[Heigh-Ho](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Heigh-Ho "wikipedia:Heigh-Ho")" from the movie *[Snow White and the Seven Dwarfs](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Snow_White_and_the_Seven_Dwarfs_(1937_film) "wikipedia:Snow White and the Seven Dwarfs (1937 film)")*.
* Wortox's quote for an Orange Gem ("Bright and orange like a... a...") is a reference to how there are no words in the English language that rhyme with orange.
* Wortox's quote for a Streetlight or Small Streetlight ("If it were raining I could sing!") is possibly a reference to the song "[Singin' in the Rain](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Singin%27_in_the_Rain_(song) "wikipedia:Singin' in the Rain (song)")" from [the movie of the same name](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Singin%27_in_the_Rain "wikipedia:Singin' in the Rain").
* Wortox's quote for the Smashed Clock ("Hickory dickory dock, hyuyu!") is a reference to the nursery rhyme "[Hickory Dickory Dock](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hickory_Dickory_Dock "wikipedia:Hickory Dickory Dock")".
* His quote for [Mumsy](/wiki/Mumsy "Mumsy") ("I'd ask hircine, but I think it's Capricorn.") contains a pun in which the words "her sign" are replaced with the word "[hircine](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/hircine "wiktionary:hircine")" which means something that has the qualities of a goat.
* His quote for Spores ("My beautiful spores.") may be a reference to "Monster Factory: Creating The Sequel To Dogs in Spore" in which Griffin McElroy does an impression of *Spore* developer [Will Wright](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Will_Wright_(game_designer) "wikipedia:Will Wright (game designer)"), talking about his "beautiful spores".
* His high research announcement quote ("My brain's grown three sizes this day!") is a reference to how the Grinch's heart is mentioned as growing three sizes in Dr. Seuss' *[How the Grinch Stole Christmas](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/How_the_Grinch_Stole_Christmas! "wikipedia:How the Grinch Stole Christmas!")*.
* His quotes for the Salt Box and Seasoning Salt are references to the use of salt to ward against evil spirits.
* His quote for the Salt Rack ("The mortals crave these minerals.") is a reference to a Tumblr meme in which a photo of Ibex goats licking salt deposits is captioned "They crave that mineral".
* His quotes for the Pomegranate ("You need only eat one seed. Hyuyu!") and cooked Pomegranate ("The underworld can wait.") may be references to [the abduction of Persephone](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Persephone#Abduction_myth "wikipedia:Persephone") from Greek mythology.
* His quote for ponds ("I could gaze upon my reflection all day!") may be a reference to Narcissus from Greek mythology, who fell in love with his reflection and only gazed at it all day long, until he eventually died.
* His quote for Webber ("Hello hello, itsy bitsy spider!") is a reference to the popular nursery rhyme *[Itsy Bitsy Spider](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Itsy_Bitsy_Spider "wikipedia:Itsy Bitsy Spider")*.
* His quote for a dead Carrat ("Ding-dong, the carrot's dead.") is a reference to the song "[Ding-Dong! The Witch Is Dead](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ding-Dong!_The_Witch_Is_Dead "wikipedia:Ding-Dong! The Witch Is Dead")" from *[The Wizard of Oz](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Wizard_of_Oz_(1939_film) "wikipedia:The Wizard of Oz (1939 film)")*.
* His quote for the King of the Merms ("Slimy is the head that wears the crown.") may be a reference to the quote "Uneasy lies the head that wears a crown" from Shakespeare's play *[Henry IV, Part 2](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Henry_IV,_Part_2 "wikipedia:Henry IV, Part 2")*.
* His quote for the [Moon Rock Idol](/wiki/Moon_Rock_Idol "Moon Rock Idol") ("What, pray tell, do you desire?") may be a reference to the television series *Lucifer*.
* His quote for the Sea Strider Nest ("Rock-a-bye spiders, in the treetop...") is a reference to the [Rock-a-bye Baby](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rock-a-bye_Baby "wikipedia:Rock-a-bye Baby") nursery rhyme.
* His quote for a burning Fish Scale-O-Matic ("Double, double toil and trouble, fire burn and fishbowl bubble!") references the line from Shakespeare's *[Macbeth](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Macbeth "wikipedia:Macbeth")*.
* One of his quotes for being stung by Fire Nettles ("Ouch! There wasn't any silver in those nettles, was there?") refers to the use of silver in folklore to kill or ward against monsters.
