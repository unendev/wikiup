The **Volcano Altar of Snackrifice** is a naturally occurring [structure](/wiki/Structure "Structure") found within the [Volcano](/wiki/Volcano "Volcano"). It is active during the [Dry Season](/wiki/Dry_Season "Dry Season"), with the offering bowl shown as full of what appears to be lava. It accepts a variety of items as offerings, some of which provide "appeasement", while others provide "wrath". Items that anger it will deal 6 fire damage to the player on being offered. After being appeased by 66 segments (a little over 4 [days](/wiki/Day "Day") of delay), it will stop accepting offerings.

The Altar is dormant all other [seasons](/wiki/Seasons "Seasons"), only giving off a faint light, enough to keep [Charlie](/wiki/Charlie "Charlie") away during the night.

## Volcano Appeasement[]

Each item that can be offered has a different appeasement value. When offered, it adds its value in day segments (segments on the clock; there are 16 per day) to the time until the next eruption happens. Negative appeasement is wrath; these items will make the next eruption happen sooner. So, for example, offering a Doydoy Feather delays the next eruption by two days, while offering obsidian makes the next eruption happen a day sooner. Once the player has placed their item of offering, the lava in the bowl turns blue and closes.

One can see how close the volcano is to the next eruption by studying the cracks on the altar. The higher up on the altar the cracks are glowing, the closer the volcano is to the next eruption. Sacrificing something to the altar will make the lava move higher or lower depending on the amount of appeasement the sacrifice gives. This can be used to judge what should be sacrificed to the altar, if anything at all.

One should be warned that the pre-eruption warning tremors **do not** reset if the volcano is appeased below the level that triggered the warning. For example, if the first warning tremor occurs and the player then offers enough appeasement to bring down the level of "charge" to below the level that triggered the first tremor, then the first tremor will not occur again once the volcano "charges" back up to that level. Only the second and third tremors will occur (if the volcano charges to the second and third level, of course). This means that if all three warning tremors occur and the player then appeases the volcano, *no warning tremors will occur before the eruption*.

## Prototype Tips[]

* It is recommended not to snackrifice [Doydoys](/wiki/Doydoy "Doydoy") directly, and to instead murder them in the volcano, deal with [Krampus](/wiki/Krampus "Krampus") if he appears, then snackrifice the two feathers, as they will provide twice as much appeasement and a chance to get a [Krampus Sack](/wiki/Krampus_Sack "Krampus Sack") if the player is able to kill him.
  + If one doesn't want to deal with Krampus, bringing 3 or more Doydoys and not murdering the last two will reduce the naughtiness gained enough to prevent him from spawning.
* If the player has a [Birdcage](/wiki/Birdcage "Birdcage"), they can more effectively appease the volcano by converting any would-be snackrifices of [Fish](/wiki/Fish "Fish"), [Jellyfish](/wiki/Jellyfish "Jellyfish"), [Limpets](/wiki/Limpets "Limpets"), and any [Meats](/wiki/Meats "Meats") into [Eggs](/wiki/Eggs "Eggs"), as Eggs appease twice as effectively as most other foods that can be fed to an imprisoned bird.
* Although greatly limited in effectiveness, the Altar could be used as a garbage disposal for unneeded or excess items that can't otherwise be easily removed from the world or converted into something useful, such as [Bone Shards](/wiki/Bone_Shards "Bone Shards") if the player has no need for the equipment they are used to craft.
* With the *[Hamlet](/wiki/Don%27t_Starve:_Hamlet "Don't Starve: Hamlet")* DLC, snackrificing [Tenpiece Oincs](/wiki/Oinc#Tenpiece_Oinc "Oinc") or [Centapiece Oincs](/wiki/Oinc#Centapiece_Oinc "Oinc") rather than converting them to regular [Oincs](/wiki/Oinc "Oinc") first is not recommended unless in an immediate emergency, as Oincs will appease for the same amount and are thus more optimal.
* With the *Hamlet* DLC, snackrificing [Snake Bone](/wiki/Snake_Bone "Snake Bone") directly is not recommended, as it can be [Hammered](/wiki/Hammer "Hammer") to obtain 2 [Bone Shards](/wiki/Bone_Shards "Bone Shards").
* With the *Hamlet* DLC, [Dark Tatters](/wiki/Dark_Tatters "Dark Tatters") can be offered to the Volcano Altar of Snackrifice. This may be a suitable alternative use if the player does not desire additional Vortex Cloaks after killing the Ancient Herald and crafted their first one.

## Placeholder Trivia[]

* The [One True Earring](/wiki/One_True_Earring "One True Earring") is a reference to *[The Lord of the Rings](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Lord_of_the_Rings "wikipedia:The Lord of the Rings")*. In it, the evil is conquered when the "One Ring" is cast into Mount Doom.
* Snackrifice is a combination of words "Snack" and "Sacrifice", possibly referencing the ability to sacrifice food items to the altar.
* A debug spawned altar will be fully functional even outside of the Volcano. However any offering will be forgotten when entering or exiting the Volcano.

## Mosquito Bugs[]

* Any eruption delays gained by appeasing the Altar within the Volcano may be forgotten outside of the Volcano. For example, one can offer a Doydoy Feather to delay the next eruption by 2 days, but leave the Volcano immediately to have the eruption occur within a day or less. However, any appeasement will be remembered as long as the player stays in the Volcano, ironically making it one of the safest places to be in if one fears volcanic eruptions.
* if the player generates the volcano during dry season, no eruption will happen for the rest of the season. [Verification Needed]
