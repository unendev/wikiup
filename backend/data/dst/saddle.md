The **Saddle** is a [Tool](/wiki/Tool "Tool") [item](/wiki/Item "Item") that must be used to mount a [Beefalo](/wiki/Beefalo "Beefalo") for riding. It is crafted at the [Alchemy Engine](/wiki/Alchemy_Engine "Alchemy Engine") with 4 [Pig Skins](/wiki/Pig_Skin "Pig Skin"), 4 [Gold Nuggets](/wiki/Gold_Nugget "Gold Nugget") and 4 [Beefalo Wool](/wiki/Beefalo_Wool "Beefalo Wool").

Before saddling a Beefalo, players must feed it [Vegetables](/wiki/Vegetable "Vegetable") or [Fruit](/wiki/Fruit "Fruit") (typically [Light Bulbs](/wiki/Light_Bulb "Light Bulb"), [Cut Grass](/wiki/Cut_Grass "Cut Grass"), or [Twigs](/wiki/Twigs "Twigs")) to raise its obedience to the desired level (10% per item, max 100%). In Don't Starve Together it's a good idea to also bond with the beefalo using a [Beefalo Bell](/wiki/Beefalo_Bell "Beefalo Bell") before saddling. If the Beefalo's obedience is below 10%, it will reject any attempt to saddle it and attack the player. Below 40% obedience it will quickly shake the saddle off. Obedience must be at least 50% to allow riding.

Saddles only lose durability when a Beefalo's obedience drops below 40% and it shakes the saddle off. A [Saddlehorn](/wiki/Saddlehorn "Saddlehorn") is the only way to manually remove a saddle and it does so without losing saddle durability.

See [Beefalo#Domestication](/wiki/Beefalo#Domestication "Beefalo") for more complete riding and domestication information.

The **War Saddle** is a variant of the normal Saddle that can be shaken off by a disobedient beefalo 8 times instead of 5. It has a 25% speed increase instead of 40%, and adds 16 bonus damage to melee attacks made while riding the Beefalo. It requires 4 live [Rabbits](/wiki/Rabbit "Rabbit"), 10 [Logs](/wiki/Log "Log"), and 4 [Steel Wool](/wiki/Steel_Wool "Steel Wool") to craft, and an Alchemy Engine to prototype. In [Don't Starve: Reign of Giants](/wiki/Don%27t_Starve:_Reign_of_Giants "Don't Starve: Reign of Giants") the bonus damage from the War Saddle will only be effective if the player either has nothing equipped in the hand slot, or has one of a limited number of items equipped, such as a [Lantern](/wiki/Lantern "Lantern") or [Umbrella](/wiki/Umbrella "Umbrella"). For a complete list of items that will deal increased damage while riding a War Saddle, see the main article on [Beefalo](/wiki/Beefalo "Beefalo").

The **Battle Saddle** is a variant of the normal Saddle exclusive to *[Don't Starve Together](/wiki/Don%27t_Starve_Together "Don't Starve Together")*. It can only be crafted by [Wigfrid](/wiki/Wigfrid "Wigfrid") after unlocking the [Battle Saddle Skill](/wiki/Battle_Saddle_Skill "Battle Saddle Skill") and needs 4 [Gold Nuggets](/wiki/Gold_Nugget "Gold Nugget"), 3 [Marbles](/wiki/Marble "Marble") and 6 [Azure Feathers](/wiki/Azure_Feather "Azure Feather") to craft. This saddle allows a 30% riding speed above the base speed of the [Beefalo](/wiki/Beefalo "Beefalo") (vs. 40% for a regular Saddle), and adds 5 bonus damage to melee attacks made while riding the Beefalo, and 40% protection for the Beefalo. It can be shaken off by a disobedient Beefalo 6 times before it breaks.

## Tips[]

* A War Saddle brings Ornery Beefalo attack damage to a whopping 66 points, almost equaling the 68 damage of the [Dark Sword](/wiki/Dark_Sword "Dark Sword") without having to worry about sanity drain or durability. However, a Glossamer Saddle offsets the slowness, making it easier to kite with.
* The 25 damage delivered by a Rider or 20 by a Pudgy Beefalo may not be enough to kill the [Ewecus](/wiki/Ewecus "Ewecus") to obtain steel wool to make a War Saddle. Plan accordingly.
* Obtaining the Butterfly Wings for a Glossamer Saddle can be a lengthy process complicated by spoilage.
  + A [Construction Amulet](/wiki/Construction_Amulet "Construction Amulet") is recommended (if available) to halve the cost of the Glossamer Saddle. Using a Construction Amulet and [Deconstruction Staff](/wiki/Deconstruction_Staff "Deconstruction Staff") on a Glossamer Saddle is a way to multiply Butterfly Wings for food and health, though the saddle must remain above 50% durability if used prior to deconstruction.
  + Seven or eight [Bug Nets](/wiki/Bug_Net "Bug Net") are needed to catch more than 68 Butterflies. The extra are required since butterflies have a one in a fifty chance to drop [Butter](/wiki/Butter "Butter") instead of wings.
  + If keeping the butterflies alive, make sure to have a few [Berries](/wiki/Berries "Berries") on hand to feed them until it is time to kill them. Murdering them all at once in inventory will give the player enough [Naughtiness](/wiki/Naughtiness "Naughtiness") to immediately summon [Krampus](/wiki/Krampus "Krampus"). Do this carefully and away from base.
  + Killing the butterflies as you catch them allows Naughtiness to decrease between murders, but care should still be taken if summoning Krampus is not your goal. An [Ice Box](/wiki/Ice_Box "Ice Box") will double the spoilage time of the wings, and a [Bundling Wrap](/wiki/Bundling_Wrap "Bundling Wrap") or [Gift Wrap](/wiki/Gift_Wrap "Gift Wrap") will prevent spoilage altogether. They cannot be placed inside a [Salt Box](/wiki/Salt_Box "Salt Box").

## Trivia[]

* Saddles were first introduced to *[Don't Starve Together](/wiki/Don%27t_Starve_Together "Don't Starve Together")*. They were later added to *[Don't Starve](/wiki/Don%27t_Starve "Don't Starve")* in the *[Quality of Life](/wiki/Don%27t_Starve/Version_History "Don't Starve/Version History")* update.
* Saddles are considered to be part of *[A New Reign](/wiki/A_New_Reign "A New Reign")*, even though they were introduced before the official start of the beta branch.
* The Glossamer Saddle is the first item in the game that requires Butterfly Wings as a crafting ingredient, in keeping with the word from which it likely derives its name, gossamer
* The Battle Saddle was introduced in the *[Skill Spotlight: Wigfrid & Willow](/wiki/Skill_Spotlight:_Wigfrid_%26_Willow "Skill Spotlight: Wigfrid & Willow")* beta update.
