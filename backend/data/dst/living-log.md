**Living Logs** are [Items](/wiki/Item "Item") dropped by deceased [Treeguards](/wiki/Treeguard "Treeguard"). They can also be obtained by [chopping](/wiki/Axe "Axe") down [Totally Normal Trees](/wiki/Totally_Normal_Tree "Totally Normal Tree"), the stump of Totally Normal Tree can be dug for another Living Log. [Wormwood](/wiki/Wormwood "Wormwood") can always "craft" Living Logs at the cost of 20 Health.

They are used to craft several magic items, including a [Shadow Manipulator](/wiki/Shadow_Manipulator "Shadow Manipulator"), and can also be used as fuel for a [Fire](/wiki/Fire "Fire"). Living Logs will scream when burned on a fire and when eaten by the [Werebeaver](/wiki/Woodie/Cursed "Woodie/Cursed") form of [Woodie](/wiki/Woodie "Woodie") or the [Doydoy](/wiki/Doydoy "Doydoy"), though this has no effect on gameplay.

## Gift Icon Downloadable Content[]

In the *[Reign of Giants](/wiki/Don%27t_Starve:_Reign_of_Giants "Don't Starve: Reign of Giants")* DLC, [Poison Birchnut Trees](/wiki/Tree/Birchnut "Tree/Birchnut") also drop Living Logs.

In *[Shipwrecked](/wiki/Shipwrecked "Shipwrecked")*, [Palm Treeguards](/wiki/Palm_Treeguard "Palm Treeguard") and [Regular Jungle Trees](/wiki/Regular_Jungle_Tree "Regular Jungle Tree") are available sources of Living Logs.

In the *[Hamlet](/wiki/Hamlet "Hamlet")* DLC, Living Logs can be bought for 5 [Oincs](/wiki/Oinc "Oinc") each at [The Flying Pig Arcane Shop](/wiki/The_Flying_Pig_Arcane_Shop "The Flying Pig Arcane Shop") in the [Pig City](/wiki/Pig_City "Pig City"). In addition, [Elder Mandrake](/wiki/Elder_Mandrake "Elder Mandrake")s will drop 2 Living Logs when killed. [Wormwood](/wiki/Wormwood "Wormwood") can exclusively craft Living Logs under the [Green Thumb Tab](/wiki/Green_Thumb_Tab "Green Thumb Tab") with a cost of 20 Health.

## Don't Starve Together Don't Starve Together[]

In *[Don't Starve Together](/wiki/Don%27t_Starve_Together "Don't Starve Together")* the [Mush Gnome](/wiki/Mush_Gnome "Mush Gnome") drops Living Logs upon death.

## Usage[]

A Living Log may be used to repair a [Wood Wall](/wiki/Wood_Wall "Wood Wall"). This works in a similar fashion to using a standard log, except that the Living Log repairs three times the wall health of a standard log.

A Living Log can be used to fuel a fire.

## Tips[]

* In Don't Starve, the base game, Woodie in Werebeaver form can quickly obtain Living Logs. The Werebeaver can quickly destroy a lot of [Trees](/wiki/Tree "Tree"), which will likely spawn Treeguards. Its powerful attacks and its ability to quickly heal by eating [Logs](/wiki/Log "Log") allows the Treeguards to be dispatched with relative ease.

## Trivia[]
