The **Frostjaw** is a [Creature](/wiki/Mobs "Mobs") exclusive to *[Don't Starve Together](/wiki/Don%27t_Starve_Together "Don't Starve Together")*. It spawns out of the [Ice Fishing Hole](/wiki/Ice_Fishing_Hole "Ice Fishing Hole") found on the [Ice Floe](/wiki/Ice_Floe "Ice Floe") in the [Ocean](/wiki/Ocean "Ocean"), when the player fishes in it with a [Sea Fishing Rod](/wiki/Sea_Fishing_Rod "Sea Fishing Rod"). It acts as a NPC but turns into a [Boss Monster](/wiki/Monster "Monster") if attacked. The Frostjaw can be defeated, but not killed. After defeat it will be open to trading with the player, giving [Bootleg Getaways](/wiki/Bootleg_Getaway "Bootleg Getaway") in exchange for [Ocean Fishes](/wiki/Ocean_Fishes "Ocean Fishes").

The Frostjaw only accepts medium-sized or larger fishes, giving 1 Bootleg Getaway for standard fish, and 2 for heavy fish. The Frostjaw will only make 5 total trades with the player, so it is best to trade heavy fish as this will result in 10 Bootleg Getaways gained rather than 5.

## Behavior[]

The Frostjaw is passive by default and just walks around the ice island. When attacked, it becomes aggressive and retaliates.

It has 3 attack phases rotating in this order:

* In the first phase, it performs a melee attack with two hand swipes and a third swipe with his tail, knocking targets back.
* In the second phase, it summons two walls of [Ice Spikes](/wiki/Ice_Spike "Ice Spike") in the direction of the target, followed by a charge performed between the walls.
* In the third phase, it dives under the ice and begins to swim around its target with its fin sticking out of the ice. It will then jump out at the target and stun it.

If the island is enlarged (in [Winter](/wiki/Seasons/Winter "Seasons/Winter")), the island will slowly fall apart at the borders, creating small ice platforms that float away and sink after 30 seconds. This is making the fighting area smaller until a minimum size of 2 tiles radius around the tile the Ice Fishing Hole was.

Upon defeat, the Frostjaw simply gives up instead of dying. After this, the Frostjaw becomes friendly and its name is revealed. It can no longer be attacked in this state, and will despawn after the player has left the area.

## Strategy[]

A [Walking Cane](/wiki/Walking_Cane "Walking Cane") or other form of speed boost is practically a requirement. Multiple good weapons (Such as at least 2 [Dark Swords](/wiki/Dark_Sword "Dark Sword")) and multiple sets of armor are highly recommended, as weapons may break before the boss is defeated due to its high health. The fight can be rendered somewhat unfair to the player due to the slippery ice terrain and seemingly random nature of causing the player to stagger and trip, causing the player to get hit by the bosses attacks. Decent armor is enough to protect from serious blows (Such as [Log Suits](/wiki/Log_Suit "Log Suit") and [Football Helmets](/wiki/Football_Helmet "Football Helmet")) but the player should take multiple healing items in case their health still gets too low.

When the player is not ready to hit the boss, they should have their walking cane equipped for the speed boost, as the Frostjaw can easily close the distance with attacks faster than the player can move away at normal walking speed. The boss only has 2 good openings for getting attacks in, at the end of its ice charge/slide attack when it's stunned, or during its dive bomb attack. It is not recommended to attack while it's doing its swipe/tail attack as there's no safe delay or opening for the player to attack without being hit themselves and thrown back.

If the Frostjaw performs its dive bomb attack, make as much distance as possible between yourself and where its fin disappeared below the ice, giving a better chance to avoid damage compared to running to the sides. When the Frostjaw lands on the ground, its head will be stuck in the ground for a short moment before it repeats the attack. If the player deals any kind of damage while the Frostjaw is stuck, it will pull itself out and be stunned for several seconds, giving the player a chance to get many hits in and ending that attack pattern early. The Frostjaw will signal that its stun duration is about to end by standing up and shaking its head. As soon as this happens, run away and to the sides in case it performs its ice slide attack next.

When the Frostjaw begins its ice slide, it will create a long wall of ice on either side of the player, trapping them in and signaling its attack trajectory. If the player maneuvered well enough prior to this attack, they can escape the Frostjaw's charge path before the ice walls even form to trap them in. If the player does get trapped by the ice, their only options are to tank the oncoming hit, or break their way out of the wall using a [Pick/Axe](/wiki/Pick/Axe "Pick/Axe") or [Brightshade Smasher](/wiki/Brightshade_Smasher "Brightshade Smasher") (A normal [Pickaxe](/wiki/Pickaxe "Pickaxe") can break the small spikes as well, but require 2 hits instead of 1, usually allowing the Frostjaw to attack before the ice breaks). Once the Frostjaw reaches the end of its slide, it will sit stunned, allowing the player to get many more hits in before changing to the next attack pattern.

If the fight is going to extend into night time, bringing [Lanterns](/wiki/Lantern "Lantern") or other forms of light are a wise decision as the Frostjaw can destroy campfires with its attacks. If lanterns are used, they may also be flung away by the Frostjaw's attacks, so taking backups is recommended.

## Trivia[]

* The Frostjaw was introduced in the *[Skill Spotlight: Wigfrid & Willow](/wiki/Skill_Spotlight:_Wigfrid_%26_Willow "Skill Spotlight: Wigfrid & Willow")* update.
* The name "Bruce" is possibly a reference to [*Finding Nemo*](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Finding_Nemo), which has a great white shark character named Bruce.
* The name "Hammer Ed" is a reference to the [hammerhead shark](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hammerhead_shark) family.
* The name "Meg" is possibly a reference to *[The Meg](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Meg)*, which features a giant [megalodon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Megalodon) shark.
