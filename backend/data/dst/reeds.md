**Reeds** are harvestable [Plants](/wiki/Plants "Plants") typically found in [Marsh](/wiki/Marsh "Marsh") [Biomes](/wiki/Biomes "Biomes"). They can be harvested to receive 1 [Cut Reeds](/wiki/Cut_Reeds "Cut Reeds"). Once cut, they will need 3 [days](/wiki/Day "Day") to regrow, but will not regrow during [Winter](/wiki/Winter "Winter"), even if [Applied Silviculture](/wiki/Books#Applied_Silviculture "Books") is read over them.

Reeds cannot be dug up and relocated like [Grass Tufts](/wiki/Grass_Tuft "Grass Tuft") or [Saplings](/wiki/Sapling "Sapling"). They are flammable and will leave behind 1 [Ashes](/wiki/Ashes "Ashes") when burned. They also cannot be uprooted by bosses, such as the [Bearger](/wiki/Bearger "Bearger").

Being found in one of the most hostile biomes in the game, harvesting Reeds is much more dangerous than most other materials, due to the [Tentacles](/wiki/Tentacles "Tentacles"), [Mosquitoes](/wiki/Mosquitoes "Mosquitoes"), [Spiders](/wiki/Spiders "Spiders") and [Merms](/wiki/Merms "Merms") that dwell there.

There is a [Set Piece](/wiki/Set_Piece#Reed_Trap "Set Piece") known as *Reed Trap*, which is a thick patch of Reeds where a dense presence of [Tentacles](/wiki/Tentacle "Tentacle") can also be found.

### Shipwrecked Shipwrecked[]

In the *[Shipwrecked](/wiki/Don%27t_Starve:_Shipwrecked "Don't Starve: Shipwrecked")* DLC, Reeds can also be found on the islands.

### Hamlet Hamlet[]

In the *[Hamlet](/wiki/Don%27t_Starve:_Hamlet "Don't Starve: Hamlet")* DLC, Reeds can be found growing in the water of [Lily Ponds](/wiki/Lily_Pond "Lily Pond"). They can only be harvested by using a [Boat](/wiki/Boats "Boats").

### Don't Starve Together Don't Starve Together[]

In *[Don't Starve Together](/wiki/Don%27t_Starve_Together "Don't Starve Together")*, [Monkeytails](/wiki/Monkeytails "Monkeytails") are a transplantable version of Reeds.

## Trivia[]

* According to [Wickerbottom](/wiki/Wickerbottom "Wickerbottom"), the reeds belong to the rush family Juncaceae, even though species in the family are not used to produce paper. The family Juncaceae is often confused with the sedge family Cyperaceae, which contains *Cyperus papyrus* (also known as Paper Reed), a plant the Ancient Egyptians used to create paper.
  + The appearance of the Reed in-game, due to its telltale flower, suggests that the plant belongs to the cattail/reedmace family Typhaceae. This family includes the genus *Typha*, which is also used to produce paper.
