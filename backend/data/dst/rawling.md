**Rawling** is an [Item](/wiki/Item "Item") exclusive to the *[Shipwrecked](/wiki/Don%27t_Starve:_Shipwrecked "Don't Starve: Shipwrecked")* DLC. It is a sentient basketball that while carried, will comment on player actions. It speaks in red text, much like [Lucy the Axe](/wiki/Lucy_the_Axe "Lucy the Axe"). When speaking a squeaking noise can be heard. It will also restore 2 Sanity/min while equipped in the hand slot. It can also be thrown, and throwing it restores 1 Sanity.

Rawling can be found randomly in the deep [Ocean](/wiki/Ocean "Ocean"), floating in the water. Despite Rawling being a special item, [Prime Apes](/wiki/Prime_Ape "Prime Ape") can still pick them up.

## Tips[]

* When thrown at a tall structure (i.e. [tent](/wiki/Tent "Tent")), Rawling will bounce down back to the player, allowing for fast sanity regeneration by repeatedly throwing and catching Rawling. This can be used as an alternative method to restore sanity, in absence of other sanity restoring items.
* When a wet Rawling is equipped, the sanity will be drained instead of being restored. This is because having a wet item equipped drains sanity faster than Rawling restores it, making the net effect negative.
* While Rawling has infinite durability, it can catch fire so care should be taken about where it is thrown or stored as [Fire Packim Baggims](/wiki/Packim_Baggims "Packim Baggims") will reduce it to ash.
* When playing as [Wendy](/wiki/Wendy "Wendy"), an equipped Rawling will completely negate the sanity drain from the Night.
* In *[Hamlet](/wiki/Hamlet "Hamlet")*-compatible worlds, one can quickly restore sanity by repeatedly throwing and picking Rawling in a corner of [interior](/wiki/Category:Interiors "Category:Interiors").

## Trivia[]

* Rawling was supposed to be an Item exclusive to [Watricia](/wiki/Unimplemented_Characters#Watricia "Unimplemented Characters"), a [Character](/wiki/Characters "Characters") that was supposed to be released with *[Shipwrecked](/wiki/Don%27t_Starve:_Shipwrecked "Don't Starve: Shipwrecked")*, but was later scrapped in favor of [Wilbur](/wiki/Wilbur "Wilbur").
* Rawling is likely a reference to Wilson, the volleyball companion of Chuck Noland from the movie *[Cast Away](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cast_Away "wikipedia:Cast Away")*.
  + In the movie, the volleyball is given this name due to the fact that it was made by [Wilson Sporting Goods](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wilson_Sporting_Goods); meaning that Rawling's name likely comes from [Rawlings](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rawlings_(company)), a similar sporting good company.
* While the item itself is a reference to *Cast Away*, the lines spoken by it are references to [Canadian Heritage Minute](https://www.historicacanada.ca/content/heritage-minutes/basketball?) shorts.
* The line "I believe I can fly!" may be a reference to the 1996 song *[I Believe I Can Fly](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/I_Believe_I_Can_Fly "wikipedia:I Believe I Can Fly")*.
* The voice of Rawling is the squeak of basketball shoes on a wood floor.

## Mosquito Bugs[]

* When thrown during a heavy wind into the direction the wind is going, Rawling will stay in place. It can be picked up and thrown, keeping the same effect, even after the wind is gone. However, it will stop when it hits the ground.
* When throwing Rawling repeatedly against an object or structure, Wilson's beard will not animate correctly.
