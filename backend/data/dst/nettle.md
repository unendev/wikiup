**Nettle** is a [Food](/wiki/Food "Food") [Item](/wiki/Item "Item") exclusive to the *[Hamlet](/wiki/Don%27t_Starve:_Hamlet "Don't Starve: Hamlet")* DLC. It is found on [Nettle Vines](/wiki/Nettle_Vine "Nettle Vine") and has antihistamine properties when eaten, granting immunity to [Hay Fever](/wiki/Hay_Fever "Hay Fever") for 200 seconds while also restoring 10 [Hunger](/wiki/Hunger "Hunger") and 10 [Health](/wiki/Health "Health").

It is required to make [Nettle Rolls](/wiki/Nettle_Rolls "Nettle Rolls") and [Meated Nettles](/wiki/Meated_Nettles "Meated Nettles") in the crockpot. When used as a filler in other crockpot recipes, Nettle is considered an edible ingredient similar to [Ice](/wiki/Ice "Ice") and [Snake Bones](/wiki/Snake_Bone "Snake Bone").

## Prototype Tips[]

* Nettle does not spoil, which makes it a good item to stock on the long-term.
* [Wigfrid](/wiki/Wigfrid "Wigfrid") is currently unable to eat Nettles or [Nettle Rolls](/wiki/Nettle_Rolls "Nettle Rolls"), so the player will want to rely on [Meated Nettles](/wiki/Meated_Nettles "Meated Nettles") or have a [Gas Mask](/wiki/Gas_Mask "Gas Mask") prepared before the [Lush Season](/wiki/Lush_Season "Lush Season") begins to avoid hay fever. [Wormwood](/wiki/Wormwood "Wormwood") is immune to Hay Fever, so he doesn't need to eat Nettles or Nettle Rolls.
* As Nettle Rolls take 6 days to spoil, and Nettles never spoil, the best option is to stock Nettle and cook Nettle Rolls at the last moment. One can even keep some Nettles ready for the next Lush season.
* Utilizing nothing but raw Nettles, the player will need to amass 29 of them to sustain each [Lush Season](/wiki/Lush_Season "Lush Season"). This number is slightly lower due to the slight delay of [Hay Fever](/wiki/Hay_Fever "Hay Fever")'s effects.
* Utilizing Nettle Rolls, the player will need to amass 24 raw Nettles exactly to sustain each [Lush Season](/wiki/Lush_Season "Lush Season").
* Meated Nettles, despite having a lesser duration, require fewer Nettles. 20 Nettles (19.2) are required to withstand the Lush Season
* Besides negating the effects of hay fever, it is also a good healing food.
