:   *For sculptures players can repair using [Suspicious Marble](/wiki/Suspicious_Marble "Suspicious Marble"), see [Marble Sculptures](/wiki/Marble_Sculptures "Marble Sculptures").*
:   *For decorative sculptures dropping [Sketches](/wiki/Sketch "Sketch") when mined, see [Marble Statues](/wiki/Marble_Statues "Marble Statues").*

**Chess Pieces** are craftable objects exclusive to *[Don't Starve Together](/wiki/Don%27t_Starve_Together "Don't Starve Together")*, introduced in *[A New Reign](/wiki/A_New_Reign "A New Reign")*. There are eight different variants that can be crafted at a [Potter's Wheel](/wiki/Potter%27s_Wheel "Potter's Wheel"). Most need to be unlocked by giving their respective [Sketch](/wiki/Sketch "Sketch") to the Potter's Wheel, but the wheel comes with 2 sketches unlocked.

Most of these pieces are for decoration only, but the Rook, Knight, and Bishop can be used to spawn their corresponding [Shadow Pieces](/wiki/Shadow_Pieces "Shadow Pieces") when made from [Marble](/wiki/Marble "Marble") or [Cut Stone](/wiki/Cut_Stone "Cut Stone") and hammered (not mined) during a [New Moon](/wiki/Moon_Cycle "Moon Cycle"). Other nearby chess pieces of these three types made from appropriate material will similarly spring to life when the first is hammered. Eligible pieces for this transformation will rattle and shake when the moon is right for hammering. Unlike the [Marble Sculptures](/wiki/Marble_Sculptures "Marble Sculptures"), they cannot be activated during the Full Moon.

Chess Pieces can be carried by players in the chest slot, just like [Suspicious Marble](/wiki/Suspicious_Marble "Suspicious Marble"), slowing down their movement speed and preventing them from several inventory actions, such as equipping a handheld item. Riding a [Beefalo](/wiki/Beefalo "Beefalo") allows carrying these pieces at full speed.

## Prototype Tips[]

* These structures act as impenetrable walls except to certain bosses. This can be used in the [Moon Caller's Staff](/wiki/Moon_Caller%27s_Staff "Moon Caller's Staff") event to block off the [Werepigs](/wiki/Werepig "Werepig") and [Hounds](/wiki/Hound "Hound") that spawn.

## Placeholder Trivia[]

* Most Chess Pieces were added in the *[A Little Fixer Upper](/wiki/A_New_Reign#September_1st,_2016_-_A_Little_Fixer_Upper "A New Reign")* update, while the Giant Figures were introduced in an unnamed [Bonus Update](/wiki/Don%27t_Starve_Together/Version_History#May_18th,_2017_-_Bonus_Update "Don't Starve Together/Version History") after the completion of *A New Reign*. The Bee Queen, Toadstool, Antlion, Ancient Fuelweaver, and Klaus figures were added in the [May](/wiki/Don%27t_Starve_Together/Version_History "Don't Starve Together/Version History") QoL update.
* Based off of character quotes, it is implied that the Kingly Figure refers to Maxwell, the Queenly figure to Charlie, and the Pawn to all the other playable characters.
* Maxwell's examination quote of the Pawn Figure lampshades the lack of a Clockwork Pawn enemy by stating that he doesn't know what a Pawn is.
* The Queenly Figure used to have speech file lines referring to it having a "struggling" state, but these were later removed or commented out.
* The Moon Shard figure type as well as the Moon Moth, Anchor, and "Moon" Figures were introduced in the *[Turn of Tides](/wiki/Return_of_Them#Turn_of_Tides "Return of Them")* update.
* Knight, Bishop, and Rook Figures made from Moon Shards will not spawn Shadow Pieces if hammered during a new moon. This is intentional.[[1]](#cite_note-1)
