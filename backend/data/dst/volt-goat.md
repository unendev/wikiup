The **Volt Goat** is an [animal](/wiki/Animal "Animal") found in the *[Reign of Giants](/wiki/Reign_of_Giants "Reign of Giants")* DLC and is indigenous to the [Desert](/wiki/Desert "Desert") [Biome](/wiki/Biome "Biome"). It's essentially a living [Lightning Rod](/wiki/Lightning_Rod "Lightning Rod"); any [Lightning](/wiki/Lightning "Lightning") that strikes in the vicinity will seek out and charge the Volt Goat, causing it to radiate electricity and glow for 3 [Days](/wiki/Day "Day"). Unlike most other creatures found in groups, attacking one Volt Goat will not cause the rest of the herd to become hostile to the attacker.

Normally a neutral creature, a charged Volt Goat becomes highly aggressive. While charged, any melee attacks made on a Volt Goat will cause an electric shock to its attacker. This shock deals 25 damage and stuns the victim. Any attacks made by a charged Volt Goat to a Player will also cause an electric shock, but without extra damage. The effects of the electric shock cannot be reduced by use of [Armor](/wiki/Armor "Armor") (but can be reduced by [Wigfrid](/wiki/Wigfrid "Wigfrid")'s natural damage absorption), however it can be prevented by wearing insulated clothing. Therefore to fight the charged goat, it's advisable to wear any piece of insulated clothing. An [Eyebrella](/wiki/Eyebrella "Eyebrella"), [Rain Coat](/wiki/Rain_Coat "Rain Coat"), and [Rain Hat](/wiki/Rain_Hat "Rain Hat") are examples of insulated items available. A charged Volt Goat deals electrical damage to Mobs. That means it gains a 1.5x multiplier to a dry target and 2.5x multiplier to wet target. A charged Volt Goat also emits light.

Upon death, a Volt Goat will drop 3 [Meat](/wiki/Meat "Meat") and has a 25% chance to also drop a [Volt Goat Horn](/wiki/Volt_Goat_Horn "Volt Goat Horn"). If killed while it is charged, it will also drop [Electric Milk](/wiki/Electric_Milk "Electric Milk"). Volt Goats are considered innocent creatures and killing one increases the player [Naughtiness](/wiki/Naughtiness "Naughtiness") level by 4.

Similar to [Beefalo](/wiki/Beefalo "Beefalo") and [Rock Lobsters](/wiki/Rock_Lobster "Rock Lobster"), Volt Goats roam in herds and reproduce every 1.5 to 2 days as long as at least one remains. Due to this, a player can relocate a herd by killing off all but one, and pushing it 2-3 tiles and waiting a few seconds for the spawner location to move. If the player doesn't do this, it will cause the goat to constantly run to where the last spawner was until it reaches it. It's recommended to have them a fair distance away from any other mobs, walls, or general locations frequently visited during [Spring](/wiki/Spring "Spring"). One can also bring a single goat from the herd far away and wall it off without moving the spawnpoint to make sure the whole herd doesn't die inside of potential farms. The herd maximum size is 6 goats.

## Don't Starve Together Don't Starve Together[]

In *[Don't Starve Together](/wiki/Don%27t_Starve_Together "Don't Starve Together")*, Volt Goats are instead found in the [Oasis Desert](/wiki/Desert#Don't_Starve_Together "Desert"), they only drop 2 meat when killed, have 700 health (double their health in RoG), and will flee from the player similar to a [Koalefant](/wiki/Koalefant "Koalefant"). This makes it difficult to engage in battle without cornering it or using ranged weapons.

When tracking [Suspicious Dirt Piles](/wiki/Suspicious_Dirt_Pile "Suspicious Dirt Pile") in the Oasis Desert during a [Spring](/wiki/Spring "Spring") [Rain](/wiki/Rain "Rain"), there is a chance to find a Charged Volt Goat (with a lightning strike signalling its appearance). This makes them renewable, even after all Volt Goats in the world are killed.

## Tips[]

* Wearing a [Rain Hat](/wiki/Rain_Hat "Rain Hat"), [Rain Coat](/wiki/Rain_Coat "Rain Coat") or [Eyebrella](/wiki/Eyebrella "Eyebrella") will insulate the player against the electricity of a charged Volt Goat.
* Using a [Morning Star](/wiki/Morning_Star "Morning Star") or an [Electric Dart](/wiki/Electric_Dart "Electric Dart") on an uncharged Volt Goat will charge it, so using it is ill-advised unless the player is after Electric Milk.
* Setting up [Honey](/wiki/Honey "Honey") production close to Volt Goats will allow for more efficient production of [Ice Cream](/wiki/Ice_Cream "Ice Cream") and [Volt Goat Chaud-Froid](/wiki/Volt_Goat_Chaud-Froid "Volt Goat Chaud-Froid") in *Don't Starve Together*.
* Attacking a charged Volt Goat or being attacked by a charged Volt Goat won't overcharge [WX-78](/wiki/WX-78 "WX-78").
* Chasing a Volt Goat large distances will not make it change the herd location. It must be moved slowly in order for that to happen. Teleporting them via the [Telelocator Staff](/wiki/Telelocator_Staff "Telelocator Staff") won't work either.
  + After moving a herd into an enclosure, using the Staff on only one Volt Goat makes all the other ones able to be killed so that they will not destroy the walls when charged, and they will eventually respawn.
* It's recommended to build [Lightning Rods](/wiki/Lightning_Rod "Lightning Rod") close to the Volt Goat pens, as otherwise they will get charged by lightnings and potentially destroy the fences.

## Trivia[]

* Volt Goats were originally planned to be exclusive to their own biome filled with [Tesla Trees](/wiki/Unimplemented_Features#Unused_Textures "Unimplemented Features"), but this idea was later scrapped.
* [Wickerbottom](/wiki/Wickerbottom "Wickerbottom") refers to it as a subspecies of *Capra aegagrus*. In real life, *Capra aegagrus* is the scientific name of the [Wild Goat](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wild_Goat "wikipedia:Wild Goat").
  + The [Domestic Goat](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Domestic_Goat "wikipedia:Domestic Goat") is also a subspecies of *Capra aegagrus*.
* The death animation of the Volt Goat is reminiscent to how [Fainting Goats](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fainting_goat "wikipedia:Fainting goat") pass out when startled.
* The moment they're struck by lightning, it can be seen that Volt Goats have bones in their ears and beards. Even their horns seem to have smaller bones inside them.
* If the player is killed by an electric shock from attacking a charged Volt Goat, the Morgue will show the cause of death as "Shenanigans".
* When a Volt Goat is struck by lightning, the electrical arcs in between their horns are known as a [Jacob's ladder](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spark_gap#Visual_entertainment "wikipedia:Spark gap").

## Bugs[]

* While trying to unfreeze itself after being frozen, a Volt Goat will sometimes move around in an odd manner while maintaining its frozen position.
* Sometimes an attacked Volt Goat will lose interest in their attacker.
* It is possible for a Volt Goat to be struck by lightning during his death animation and get charged. Apart from that, the Volt Goat will still disappear, and will not drop [Electric Milk](/wiki/Electric_Milk "Electric Milk").
