The **Pig King** can be found in one of the [Pig Villages](/wiki/Pig_Village "Pig Village") that generally spawn in or on the borders of a [Deciduous Forest](/wiki/Deciduous_Forest "Deciduous Forest") biome, usually at the end of a path. He can be found laying on [Wooden Flooring](/wiki/Wooden_Flooring "Wooden Flooring") surrounded by [Obelisks](/wiki/Obelisk "Obelisk"), and may be guarded by [Guardian Pigs](/wiki/Pig/Guardian "Pig/Guardian") if playing in [Adventure Mode](/wiki/Adventure_Mode "Adventure Mode"). He is immune to any damage and cannot be attacked.

During the day, the player can make offerings to the Pig King in exchange for [Gold Nuggets](/wiki/Gold_Nugget "Gold Nugget"). At [Night](/wiki/Night "Night"), the Pig King goes to [sleep](/wiki/Sleep "Sleep") and cannot be traded with. He will not hold any grudge towards the player, even if they kill his fellow [Pigs](/wiki/Pig "Pig") or his Guardian Pigs. Despite Pigs' hostility to [Webber](/wiki/Webber "Webber") and [Wortox](/wiki/Wortox "Wortox"), the Pig King will still trade with them, but he will never trade with [Wurt](/wiki/Wurt "Wurt") (Wurt can at least feed trinkets to [Antlion](/wiki/Antlion "Antlion") for [Desert Stones](/wiki/Desert_Stone "Desert Stone")).

In Adventure Mode, worlds may have different chances of encountering the Pig King based on the specific world's individual rules.

## Don't Starve Together icon Don't Starve Together[]

During the [Hallowed Nights](/wiki/Hallowed_Nights "Hallowed Nights") [event](/wiki/Category:Events "Category:Events") in *[Don't Starve Together](/wiki/Don%27t_Starve_Together "Don't Starve Together")*, the Pig King gives [Halloween Candy](/wiki/Hallowed_Nights "Hallowed Nights") in addition to Gold Nuggets. If the player is wearing a Halloween Costume [Skin](/wiki/Skins "Skins"), the amount of Candy will be increased.

If given a [Golden Belt](/wiki/Year_of_the_Pig_King#Golden_Belt "Year of the Pig King"), he will initiate the [Pig King Wrestling Match](/wiki/Year_of_the_Pig_King#Pig_King_Wrestling_Match "Year of the Pig King").

Knight, Rook, and Bishop [trinkets](/wiki/Trinkets "Trinkets") can be traded to the Pig King in exchange for their respective figure sketches, making these sketches renewable if the originals are lost.

## Prototype Tips[]

* Meat that is not accepted by the King can be converted into [Eggs](/wiki/Egg "Egg") by a [Bird](/wiki/Birds "Birds") in a [Birdcage](/wiki/Birdcage "Birdcage"), and then offered to the King. This makes [Gold Nuggets](/wiki/Gold_Nugget "Gold Nugget") an infinite resource, as Meat is a renewable resource in the game.
* Since trinkets are renewable through multiple means in *Don't Starve Together*, gold can be easily mass-farmed this way as well.
* A low cost method of obtaining Gold from the Pig King would be to place [Lureplants](/wiki/Meat_Bulb_(Lureplant) "Meat Bulb (Lureplant)") on a [boat](/wiki/Boat_Kit "Boat Kit") or surrounded by turf, such as Rocky Turf or Wooden Flooring, where they can't spawn Eyeplants, and repeatedly harvest the bait.

## Placeholder Trivia[]

* The Pig King will throw the Gold Nuggets towards the player's camera. Turning the camera will cause the Gold Nuggets to land in a different place.
* Humorously, the Pig King will refuse a haunting from a [Ghost Character](/wiki/Ghost_Characters "Ghost Characters") in *Don't Starve Together*.
* There is a Pig King [Vignette](/wiki/Vignettes "Vignettes") in *Don't Starve Together*. Its flavor text provides a little background for the Pig King and his followers: "Legend has it the Pig King and his followers left their home long ago to rule freely in [The Constant](/wiki/The_Constant "The Constant")." Their previous home could possibly be the world of *Hamlet*.
* Depictions of the Pig King appear in the *[Hamlet](/wiki/Don%27t_Starve:_Hamlet "Don't Starve: Hamlet")* DLC. For example, one of the [Intricate Topiaries](/wiki/Intricate_Topiary "Intricate Topiary") is shaped like him.
* The way he obtains the gold is currently unknown.

## Mosquito Bugs[]

* Sometimes, a Pig King does not spawn in Sandbox Mode because there isn't enough space for his [Set Piece](/wiki/Set_Piece "Set Piece") to be created during the world generation.
