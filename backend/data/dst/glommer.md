**Glommer** is a passive [Mob](/wiki/Mob "Mob") from the *[Reign of Giants](/wiki/Don%27t_Starve:_Reign_of_Giants "Don't Starve: Reign of Giants")* DLC.

Glommer is found during every [Full Moon](/wiki/Full_Moon "Full Moon") at [Glommer's Statue](/wiki/Glommer%27s_Statue "Glommer's Statue"), along with a [Glommer's Flower](/wiki/Glommer%27s_Flower "Glommer's Flower"). Glommer will follow the player when Glommer's Flower is picked up, similar to [Chester](/wiki/Chester "Chester") and his [Eye Bone](/wiki/Eye_Bone "Eye Bone"). If Glommer's Flower is dropped on the ground or put inside a stationary container, Glommer will remain in the area near the flower. If Glommer's Flower is placed inside Chester, Glommer will follow Chester. When day-time comes and if the flower is still attached to Glommer's Statue, Glommer will fly away up into the air (similar to a [Mosling](/wiki/Mosling "Mosling")). If the flower is destroyed (or used as ingredient), Glommer will be wandering without aim.

When tamed, Glommer provides a [Sanity](/wiki/Sanity "Sanity") aura of +6.25/min, (only while standing extremely close, similar to befriended [Pigs](/wiki/Pig "Pig")) making it useful in countering the nighttime sanity drain of -5/min. It will also drop [Glommer's Goop](/wiki/Glommer%27s_Goop "Glommer's Goop") once every 2–4 days.

Killing Glommer raises the player's [Naughtiness](/wiki/Naughtiness "Naughtiness") by 50 (which is the naughtiness cap), therefore Glommer is the only creature in RoG that will cause [Krampus](/wiki/Krampus "Krampus") to immediately spawn when killed by the player. (This aspect is replicated in the *[Shipwrecked](/wiki/Don%27t_Starve:_Shipwrecked "Don't Starve: Shipwrecked")* DLC, when killing a [Doydoy](/wiki/Doydoy "Doydoy") with only 1-2 left in the world, or the [Sealnado](/wiki/Sealnado "Sealnado") when in [Seal](/wiki/Seal "Seal") form.)

If killed, Glommer will drop 3 [Monster Meat](/wiki/Monster_Meat "Monster Meat"), 1 [Glommer's Wings](/wiki/Glommer%27s_Wings "Glommer's Wings"), and 2 [Glommer's Goop](/wiki/Glommer%27s_Goop "Glommer's Goop"), and will re-spawn the next full moon. It falls asleep after being hit by 3 [Sleep Darts](/wiki/Sleep_Dart "Sleep Dart").

Like other followers, Glommer can be targeted by the player while performing a [force attack](/wiki/Controls "Controls"). Below is the number of hits it takes, with a default damage modifier, to kill Glommer. The [Weather Pain](/wiki/Weather_Pain "Weather Pain") is not included due to the random nature of its projectile.

Glommer will not be attacked by any variety of [Spider](/wiki/Spider "Spider") or [Bee](/wiki/Bee "Bee").

## Prototype Tips[]

* Glommer can be killed indirectly by luring it near a [Catcoon](/wiki/Catcoon "Catcoon") (e.g. to collect its drops for the [Old Bell](/wiki/Old_Bell "Old Bell")). This avoids summoning Krampus.
* A player carrying a Glommer's Flower can bring a Glommer down into the caves. This will provide a renewable source of fuel and Sanity regeneration. Glommer will not follow the player into the caves in *[Don't Starve Together](/wiki/Don%27t_Starve_Together "Don't Starve Together")*, however.
* Standing directly next to Glommer during the night can negate the nighttime sanity loss.
* It is possible to get the Glommer Flower with [Woodie](/wiki/Woodie "Woodie") by planting a [Lureplant](/wiki/Lureplant "Lureplant") near Glommer's Statue on a full moon, then killing the Lureplant after it has eaten the flower. Woodie can then pick up the dropped flower after returning to his normal form. Alternatively, the player can use a longer night, leave the wereform alone for a long period to turn back quickly, and get the flower before the moon end.
* Due to the inexistence of the old bell in DST, it may be better to avoid killing glommer, unless the player wishes to summon Krampus. Instead, it is recommended to set Glommer near or at one's base. It will passively produce [Glommer's Goop](/wiki/Glommer%27s_Goop "Glommer's Goop") (which can act as a fuel, fertilizer, or food in a pinch). Glommer also restores sanity to any player near him.
* If [Mysterious Energy](/wiki/Mysterious_Energy "Mysterious Energy") is activated, Glommer is able to respawn at its statue every night. Killing Glommer every day provides players with a steady stream of Krampii, and produces Glommer goop at a faster rate than Glommer's passive production rate.
* Luring Glommer near Farm Plots in *Don’t Starve Together* can grant an easy supply of fertilizer every 2-4 days.
* Glommer can still appear indefinitely even if the [statue](/wiki/Glommer%27s_Statue "Glommer's Statue") has been damaged with a [Pickaxe](/wiki/Pickaxe "Pickaxe") to get the [Old Bell](/wiki/Old_Bell "Old Bell") recipe.
* Be aware that the Glommer's statue spawns in the catcoon's habitat, that prey on glommer.

## Placeholder Trivia[]

* Glommer makes a sort of "zeitzer", or buzzing, sound.
* Wigfrid's umlauts affect the "o" in "Glommer," making it "Glömmer," which means "forgets" in Swedish.
* [Wickerbottom](/wiki/Wickerbottom "Wickerbottom")'s quote describes Glommer as an [insect](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Insect "wikipedia:Insect") (Insecta), probably due to its eight legs. There are many features that don't fit into the real-life classification, however.

## Mosquito Bugs[]

* When Glommer gets killed right before exiting the Caves, while its flower stays underground, it will be revived despite the fact that its flower turns perishable. Furthermore, during the next full moon, another Glommer will spawn, enabling the player to have multiple at once.
* Sometimes Glommer and Chester cannot be killed even with force attack, except using blow darts.
